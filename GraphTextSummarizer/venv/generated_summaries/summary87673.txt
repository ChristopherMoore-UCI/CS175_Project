The death toll from Thursday's earthquake in southwest China is at least 25, with more than 250 injured, according to the ministry of civil affairs. China Central Television showed damaged buildings with debris and red lanterns lying around as police officers directed traffic on a chaotic street. The area has experienced minor quakes under 5.0 magnitude since the year began, according to the Xinhua news agency. The epicenter was 2 kilometers (1.2 miles) away from the center of Yingjiang county, which has a population of more than 270,000 and is home to several of China's ethnic minorities.
