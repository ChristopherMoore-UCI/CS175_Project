Called AT&T Digital Life, the service will connect users with a vast array of domestic devices and appliances, including cameras, door locks, smoke and carbon monoxide detectors and thermostats. Verizon offers a similar service, while home security market leader ADT features ADT Pulse on approved devices. " Digital Life joins other existing products and wireless companies that are taking advantage of people's increasing reliance on mobile devices. -- AT&T is joining the expanding field of home security and automation, introducing a wireless service that will let homeowners use their mobile devices to remotely set alarms, turn on lights or even shut off water. "AT&T Digital Life will change the way people live, work and play -- and meets a clear need in the market," said Kevin Petersen, senior vice president of Digital Life for AT&T Mobility.
