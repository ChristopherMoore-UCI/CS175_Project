"These surveys on Alaska's North Slope are another important step toward broader commercial use of unmanned aircraft," Transportation Secretary Anthony Foxx said in a news release at the time. The Federal Aviation Administration will announce long awaited rules Sunday governing how Unmanned Aerial Vehicles, often called drones, will be legally allowed to fly in the United States for business purposes. There have been several close calls where pilots of commercial planes came within feet of UAVs. It's unclear how complicated the new requirements for commercial drone use will be. One of the most significant challenges facing regulators is how to keep the small drones away from larger aircraft with people on board.
