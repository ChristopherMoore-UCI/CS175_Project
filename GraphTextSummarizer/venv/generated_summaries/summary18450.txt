Croatia drew 1-1 in the match against Italy in Poznan on Thursday, with Mario Mandzukic scoring for the Croatians and Andrea Pirlo claiming a goal for Italy. European football's governing body opened disciplinary proceedings Saturday against Croatia over what it said was racist behavior by its supporters during a Euro 2012 match against Italy. UEFA said it was acting over "the setting-off and throwing of fireworks, and the improper conduct of supporters," including racist chants and the displaying of racist symbols. Members of the Dutch squad claimed to hear monkey noises during an open training session in Krakow, Poland, before the tournament started, though the Dutch FA opted not to lodge an official complaint with UEFA. UEFA president Michel Platini urged fans who are attending decisive matches on Saturday night to "conduct themselves with dignity and respect.
