"It was a way to connect to job seekers, and also for job seekers to take a little break from the stress of unemployment," Cornett said. Unemployment in Central Florida is the highest it has been in 16 years, according to Cornett. Most contestants were from Central Florida, likely because the grand prize includes tuition for a certificate program at an Orange County technical school. Workforce Vice President Kimberly Cornett said her organization's participation in the singing contest helped spread the word on their "no-cost services" to the community. When she couldn't find a job, her husband supported her decision to enroll in nursing school.
