-- As Hurricane Rafael sped away from Bermuda, taking with it heavy rain and wind, the government discontinued storm watches for the island. Late Tuesday, Rafael was about 135 miles (215 kilometers) east of the island, the National Hurricane Center in Miami said. Bermuda might see 2 to 4 inches of rain from the storm, and some beaches in the Bahamas and the eastern United States will see swells caused by Rafael. Rafael becomes hurricane as it churns towards Bermuda Forecasters said Rafael will break up late Wednesday or early Thursday after a turn to the northeast. According to the Bermuda Weather Service, wind speeds reached 51 mph at the airport Tuesday night.
