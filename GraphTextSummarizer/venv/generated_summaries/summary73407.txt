What to watch for at the Olympics this year Here are the 10 oddest sports that have graced the modern Olympics -- some will feature at London 2012 over the coming weeks, some thankfully will not... SOLO SYNCHRONIZED SWIMMING Oxymoron alert! -- Basketball, track and swimming have been staples at the Olympics for decades, drawing thousands of spectators. "Precise technique and perfect body control are vital for success, with judges delivering marks for difficulty, execution and time of flight, minus penalties," Olympic officials say. But there will be the team synchronized swimming events that most people are familiar with. In the 1900 Games in Paris, swimmers crawled over boats, swam under them and climbed a pole -- all the while swimming 200 meters in the Seine.
