The law was backed by the center-left government of President Cristina Kirchner, who signed the measure into law. Argentina became the first Latin American country to legalize same-sex marriage when lawmakers in the Senate passed the bill this month after 14 hours of debate. Previously, Argentina did not have a nationwide law but let state and local governments decide whether to allow same-sex marriages. -- Two men who have been together for 34 years have become the first couple to obtain a same-sex marriage since it became legal in Argentina on July 15.
