For example, cruise ships that operate from U.S. ports are subject to strict U.S oversight and enforcement of international laws and regulations as well as all applicable federal laws of the United States. Contrary to what the op-ed's author says, significant fires on board ships are rare. Fire safety regulations are continuously enhanced and have reduced the frequency and severity of fires. A U.N. agency -- the International Maritime Organization -- mandates global standards for the safe operation of cruise ships through its 170-member governments, including the United States.
