Schools in 21 states can use corporal punishment. Corporal punishment in schools remains legal in 21 U.S. states and is used frequently in 13: Missouri, Kentucky, Texas, Oklahoma, Arkansas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, Georgia, South Carolina, North Carolina, Tennessee and Florida, according to data received from the Office for Civil Rights at the U.S. Department of Education and cited in the report. "It doesn't take much time to administer corporal punishment, and you don't have to hire someone to run a detention or an after-school program." The highest percentage of students receiving corporal punishment was in Mississippi, with 7.5 percent of students.
