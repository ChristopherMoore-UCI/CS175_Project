Aminulllah Amarkhil, Afghan chief of eastern border police, said Monday that Pakistani military forces fired 35 pieces of artillery in Kunar province Sunday that, primary reports indicate, killed 20 civilians. Afghan President Hamid Karzai met with Pakistani President Asif Ali Zardari in Tehran, Iran, and discussed the attacks, according to a statement from Karzai's office. Pakistani forces have been firing rockets on five districts of Kunar province, causing villagers to leave their homes, the police official said. Zardari said the Pakistani side was not aware of such attacks and that the attacks were not carried by Pakistani army, according to the statement. -- Afghanistan says the Pakistani military is behind recent deadly rocket attacks, but Islamabad says it is not responsible.
