"If you find that charming then you probably would go again," remarks York of Henley's ability to repeatedly draw crowds of thousands year after year. But while Henley's dream of "Olde England" is still alive and kicking, York suggests it's only a matter of time until the event follows in the footsteps taken by some of its rivals. They often witness two races simultaneously, while up to 90 can take place on certain days. "Henley is still relatively better preserved than the other events," York observes.
