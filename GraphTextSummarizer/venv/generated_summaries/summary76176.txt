Two teens found guilty in Steubenville rape case Lurid text messages, social media posts, as well as cell phone pictures and videos helped raise the national profile of the case, which revolved around Richmond and Mays' actions during a series of end-of-summer parties in August 2012. Judge Thomas Lipps on Friday classified Ma'lik Richmond as a tier II sex offender, Jefferson County, Ohio, juvenile court administrator Joe Colabella said. In March, Richmond and Mays -- who were 16 and 17, respectively, at the time -- were convicted after a trial that divided the football-crazed Rust Belt town of Steubenville. Defense attorneys questioned the victim's credibility, as well as whether media coverage of the case tainted testimony.
