Symptoms of IBS-C include abdominal pain or discomfort, hard and infrequent stools, and incomplete bowel movements. CIC has similar symptoms, such as hard stools, and infrequent and incomplete bowel movements, but is usually not associated with pain. OTC treatment options such as laxatives and stool softeners are available and approved to treat occasional constipation. Or you may have a functional bowel disorder; two of the most common are irritable bowel syndrome with constipation (IBS-C) and chronic idiopathic constipation (CIC). You may have a chronic GI disorder such as ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease or celiac disease.
