It is a small wooden frame, running on ball-bearings and pushed by both the 'Make-A-Rail" operators and passengers down the tracks to get to its destination. The journey from the end of the railway line to Liberia's capital Monrovia can take up to 36hours by "Make-A-Rail" --only a 3hour journey by road - but it costs half the price. However, more importantly, the railway line passes through villages that have little access by road. After a few hours, the railway tracks began to vibrate with the sound of an on-coming train and the small band of passengers stood and looked down the tracks expectantly. " "Make-A-Rail" started in the 1990's during Liberia's 14-year civil war that left the country's infrastructure in ruins.
