Metro trains in Washington are being operated manually by train operators until further notice, according to the Washington Metropolitan Transit Authority. Metro trains are operated on automatic mode during rush hour. When trains are running on automatic mode, the operator's main task is to open and close the train doors. In an automated system, a control room houses electronics that regulate the movement of trains. "The design of the system is set up to keep trains separated, to have positive separation between trains, to control speeds, to give them speed information.
