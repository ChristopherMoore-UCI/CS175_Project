"The farm used to get a lot of complaints," says farmer Shawn Saylor. Although Saylor had been interested in digesters for years, his dream didn't become reality until 2006. So it was no surprise that, when faced with rising energy costs, Saylor turned to technology. Overall, the poo power helps Saylor's bottom line. "It used to stink a lot." Growing up on the sprawling spread 90 minutes from Pittsburgh, 36-year-old farmer Shawn Saylor developed into a self-described science buff.
