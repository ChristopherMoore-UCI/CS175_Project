Faber Castell has been making pencils on the outskirts of Nuremberg since 1761 and today they produce one sixth of the entire world's pencils. The Mittelstand often have longstanding apprentice schemes, and German universities work closely with researchers at local firms to ensure products are always improved. Read more: Juliet Mann's blog on the Mittlestand While latest economic data suggests even Germany is being weighed down by the crisis, that could be offset by the Mittelstand's philosophy of longevity, innovation and investment in their workforce. Such an outlook has helped to foster community loyalty, which has kept the Mittelstand steady through economic fluctuations. Mittelstand companies can be found behind hard to imitate niche products for the manufacturing and engineering markets.
