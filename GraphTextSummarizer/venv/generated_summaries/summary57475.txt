It is worth noting that most of the high-profile divisive issues -- family life, gay marriage, abortion, assisted suicide, multiculturalism -- express conflicts over cultural values. Culture became politicized and geopolitical ambitions were framed through appeals to a "way of life." Yet this second Thirty Years War soon gave way to the four decades-long Cold War. World War I would be the last time that a significant body of intellectuals and artists would seek existential meaning on the battlefield. A powerful mood of estrangement and disorientation dominated cultural life.
