Many users recently found their news feeds awash with postings suggesting that the users who displayed them would get extra copyright and privacy protection. While many Facebook and Twitter posts snickered about the hubbub, concerns about online privacy linger. In a world of social media dominated by complex terms of use and sometimes-fuzzy privacy arrangements, could some Facebook users have taken solace in the possibility of having some bit of extra control over their data, even if it was a hoax? This meme is similar to a previous scheme years ago that suggested website owners place protective text on their pages, says the hoax-debunking site Snopes.com.
