Chief Justice John Roberts shows his writing chops in a dissenting Supreme Court opinion. In a July dissent in an especially dry case over who can file certain lawsuits, he quoted singer Bob Dylan. The man in this case is the top man at the Supreme Court, Chief Justice John Roberts, who on Tuesday turned a brief dissent in a criminal appeal into something straight out of a Dashiell Hammett detective novel. The elusive "ain't" has been debated by Dylan scholars. Amid arcane talk of legal standing and redressability, the youngest member of the high court showed his generational bearings by citing part of the lyrics from Dylan's "Like a Rolling Stone."
