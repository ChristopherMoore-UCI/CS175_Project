There is not a black America and a white America and Latino America and Asian America -- there's the United States of America. ... John Kerry, right, and Obama confer at the 2004 DNC, where Obama wowed the crowd with his keynote address. According to an August article in The New Republic, Obama was selected over some better-known names by the Kerry brain trust, which included Cahill and adviser Robert Shrum, for various reasons. Even then, he didn't know he'd deliver the keynote until told by Kerry campaign manager Mary Beth Cahill. As an African-American, he would help attract minority voters; Obama was also youthful and energetic, qualities that the Kerry campaign wanted to display on television.
