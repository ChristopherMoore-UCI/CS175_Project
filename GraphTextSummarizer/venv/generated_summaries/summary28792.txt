When EW caught up with the actor a few hours before he was embarking on his global press tour for "Battleship" -- his second effects-laden big budget studio picture this year -- the 30-year-old said he had "absolutely no regrets" about his first big screen starring role. Kitsch points out that after Battleship, he's got Oliver Stone's "Savages" coming out in July, and then he'll likely start shooting the Navy SEAL drama "Lone Survivor" for "Battleship" director Peter Berg in the fall. When a big movie bellyflops at the box office -- like "John Carter," which has pulled in a cringe-inducing $62 million in the U.S. in its first three weekends, barely a fourth of the film's $250 million budget -- usually everyone involved tries to get as much distance as they can from the film, as quickly as they can get it. "I would do 'John Carter' again tomorrow," he says forthrightly. "I'm very proud of 'John Carter.'
