"What this bus ban tells us is that Uyghurs in China have fewer rights than other citizens, even in their homeland, and that it green-lights discrimination against Uyghurs by ordinary Han Chinese. "Officials in Karamay city are endorsing an openly racist and discriminatory policy aimed at ordinary Uyghur people," Uyghur American Association president, Alim Seytoff said in a statement. There have been numerous attacks in the region, where tensions are simmering between Uyghur Muslims, a Turkic people, and Chinese Han people. In recent years, the Xinjiang region has seen an uptick in violence between Uyghurs and Han Chinese over economic and other issues. Karamay is a resource-rich city of some 450,000 people located in northwest Xinjiang, which has a dense Uyghur population.
