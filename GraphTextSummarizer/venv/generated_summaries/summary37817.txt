Zimbabwe's cholera epidemic, which started in August, has been aggravated by erratic water supplies, shortages of water purification chemicals, broken water and sewer pipes and uncollected garbage. Most suburbs in Harare have had no water for close to two weeks, forcing residents to depend on shallow pits and rivers for drinking water. As Tsvangirai was visiting the cholera affected areas, some residents could be seen getting water from the wells and rivers. "It (cholera) just started after we drank water from a well. Cholera is an intestinal disease cause by bacteria in contaminated water.
