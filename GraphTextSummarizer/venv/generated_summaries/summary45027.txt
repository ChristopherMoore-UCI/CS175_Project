Starting at the junctures of Oxford Street, Sydney's celebrated gay district, vintage clothing stores like Grandma Takes a Trip, Wheels & Dollbaby and Strawberry Hills (+612 9380 8809) make Surry Hills Sydney's capital of alternative fashion. Insider guide to Sydney Surry Hills During the Great Depression, Surry Hills was a Dickensian slum known for brothels and razor-wielding street gangs. Across from Sydney University is Glebe Point Road, home to Gleebooks, Sydney's most popular independent bookstore. A detour through Hallis Lane leads to the Tim Olsen Gallery and Richard Martin Art on Jersey Road, where Sydney's art cognoscenti attend regular exhibitions. Led by global leaders in the fields of fashion, film, publishing, architecture and design, it underlines Sydney's standing as a global epicenter for creative arts.
