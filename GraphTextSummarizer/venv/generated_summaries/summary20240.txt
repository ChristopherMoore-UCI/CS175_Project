When it came to medical marijuana expansion in several states, Obama's Justice Department sent out strongly worded letters warning that even medical marijuana was in "violation of federal law regardless of state laws permitting such activities" and that "this includes prosecution of business enterprises that unlawfully market and sell marijuana." On Election Day, marijuana enthusiasts rejoiced as Colorado and Washington legalized marijuana. Indeed Big Tobacco, for one, has long been perched and ready to make some serious cash from marijuana sales: According to internal documents released during its historic court settlement, in smoking's heyday, Big Tobacco considered marijuana legalization a golden opportunity. Many who support legalization have claimed that we could control marijuana use, especially among teens, if only we regulated it through legalization.
