"We've taken steps to anticipate possible lava floods from upstream," said Syamsul Maarif, the head of the National Agency for Disaster Management. The worst might be over on Indonesia's Mount Merapi volcano, disaster officials said Thursday. To restore a sense of normalcy for displaced students, classes have started at the camps, disaster officials said Thursday. On Thursday, disaster officials said dredging has begun. "Generally speaking, Mount Merapi has passed its dangerous phase," the National Agency for Disaster Management said.
