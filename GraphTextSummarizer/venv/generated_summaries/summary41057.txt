It should let people measure their own blood pressure, heart rate, temperature, oxygen saturation and respiratory rate. In addition to the Scanaflo (a single-use urine test) the company has created the Scanadu Scout, a palm-sized disc you press to your forehead or temple for 10 seconds to take vital signs, including blood pressure, temperature, heart and respiratory rate. Last week, the judges narrowed down the field of 41 teams to 10, which now have until April to create working prototypes for consumer tests. It's all part of a futuristic self-diagnosis kit from startup Scanadu, which is competing to be the future of DIY health care. These types of devices are not meant to replace doctors, but to fill in when in-person medical care is not available, affordable or necessary.
