"Game of Thrones" has been setting ratings records for HBO this season, so it's probably no surprise that the fantasy hit is once again settings records for illegal downloading too. EW:  'Game of Thrones' showrunners talk season 5 This year, HBO added this statement: "There are numerous anti-theft tools we utilize and we have significantly shrunk the international distribution window for original programming to practically correspond with the US premiere. The premium network officially condemns theft, yet also recognizes that "Thrones" is an enormous hit, that content leakage is tough to prevent and that the show's popularity among pirates is inevitable (countries such as Australia, where viewers don't receive new episodes via pay cable in a timely manner, tend to be among the biggest piracy territories). Jeff Bewkes, CEO of HBO's parent company Time Warner, once claimed that piracy resulted in more HBO subscriptions. Earlier this season, "Thrones" topped "The Sopranos" to officially rank as the network's most-watched series of all time, while also ranking as the show's most illegally downloaded season as well.
