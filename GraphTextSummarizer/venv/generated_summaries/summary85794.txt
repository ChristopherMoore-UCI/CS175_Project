The death toll from flash flooding in southern France rose to 25 Thursday as rain continued to inundate the region, authorities said. Rescue operations officially ended Thursday and authorities were focusing on the distribution of food and water,clearing debris and assisting residents of Draguignan, which was hardest hit, authorities said. The flooding also forced French authorities to transfer more than 400 inmates at Draguignan prison to other prisons in the south of France. More local thunderstorms were forecast for Thursday afternoon and evening, the prefecture said. Authorities warned residents not to drink water that appears or tastes unusual.
