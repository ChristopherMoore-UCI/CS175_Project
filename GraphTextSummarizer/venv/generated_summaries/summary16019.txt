Umm Khattab, as she identifies herself online, is one of scores of women that have joined the terror group that has declared an independent Islamic state across huge swathes of Iraq and Syria in recent months. She posts pictures of Vietnamese grilled chicken and other recipes on the Web, peppering her conversations, like many young women her age, with emojis and lols. The Terrorism Research and Analysis Consortium (TRAC) estimates that as many as 15% of ISIS' foreign recruits are women -- possibly up to 200 women from at least 14 different countries. But her ambitions to take on a frontline role appear to have been thwarted by ISIS' strict rules for women. Manning checkpoints and inspecting all women that pass, and enforcing ISIS' strict morality code for women in the caliphate.
