China suffered a spate of knife and cleaver attacks targeting school children in 2010. But in Chenpeng, the only visible line of defense appeared to be elementary students and nearby adults with brooms. A number of measures were introduced at the time, including increased security at schools across the country and a regulation requiring people to register with their national ID cards when buying large knives. Authorities say 36-year-old Min Yongjun first stabbed an elderly woman in Chenpeng before breaking into the elementary school, Xinhua said.
