Death threats were made against her, other students and teachers every day simply because they went to a girls' school in Pakistan targeted by Taliban militants. "Right now, [Swat Valley] is under the control of the Taliban," she said. Now she is safer, 100 miles away in Pakistan's capital, Islamabad, but her thoughts are often with her friends in Swat, near the border with Afghanistan. As many as 200 schools, mostly for girls, have been destroyed since November 2007 when the Taliban began their campaign to take control of the Swat Valley and surrounding areas.
