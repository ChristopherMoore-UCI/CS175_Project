When terrible things happen: Helping children heal After shock and numbness, the second stage is that powerful emotions rise to the surface, often after being buried for years or decades. Studies have shown that even in the face of tragedy, and despite the fact that a memory may be indelible, emotional balance for many victims can return to normal states within two years. Another discovery is that brain trauma causes abnormal activity in at least three areas of the brain. Men have been taught that it is a sign of weakness to show public emotion, and so feel safer crying alone. For the sake of bringing suffering to an end after Newtown, we should pause over the choices that everyone can make as an aid to healing: Hold each other in loving awareness.
