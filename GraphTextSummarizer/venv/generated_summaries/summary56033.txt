The failure of HealthCare.gov to enable people to enroll when the sign-up period began last month undermined the launch of new health insurance exchanges intended to provide affordable coverage for millions who previously were uninsured or under-insured. For the program to work, it needs to sign up younger people less inclined to pay for health coverage they don't believe they need. A properly functioning website is crucial to implementing the most vital provisions of the Affordable Care Act that require people to have health coverage. According to a CNN count based on available figures, a little more than 200,000 people have signed up for new private health insurance under Obamacare -- either through the national system or networks set up in 14 states and the District of Columbia.
