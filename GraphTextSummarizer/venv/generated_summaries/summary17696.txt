Department of Defense The plan increases the Pentagon's base budget to $553 billion, $22 billion more than last year. The wars in Iraq and Afghanistan are covered separately, with $118 billion set aside: more than $41 billion less than last year, mostly because of the withdrawal of troops from Iraq. The savings are planned through decreases in the U.S. Geological Survey, cuts in construction programs and tribal program reductions. If all doesn't go according to plan, Congress must pass a continuing resolution like last year's by 11:59 p.m. September 30 to continue funding the government as lawmakers continue to try to slice up the budget pie.
