Unfortunately a 2005 study found that 75% of school districts affected by campus shootings did not require counseling for teacher witnesses. For teachers who witness school shootings, one diagnosis stands out, and it is well-known in the field of psychology: post-traumatic stress disorder, or PTSD. Teachers, students, administrators and other school workers need to feel valued and respected. School shootings affect teachers, school secretaries, maintenance people and anyone else on campus at the time. Teacher slayings hurt view of schools as safe havens If PTSD is not dealt with, the quality of the teacher's life can degrade rapidly.
