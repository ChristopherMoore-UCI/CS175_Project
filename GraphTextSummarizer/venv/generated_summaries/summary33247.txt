Despite economic boom, Erdogan targeted by protests Under Erdogan and his ruling AKP party, Turkey lifted curbs on public expression of religion, including strict limits on women wearing Islamic-style headscarves. In a 2011 online poll fueled by Turkish social media sites, more people picked Erdogan to be Time's Person of the Year than any other candidate. But Prime Minister Recep Tayyip Erdogan may also be the most polarizing. With his term set to end next year, Erdogan has repeatedly announced plans to transform the country's political system from a prime ministerial form of government to a more powerful presidential system, with himself as head of state. The prime minister has also spearheaded political reforms that brought Turkey more in line with the European Union -- and closer to the West.
