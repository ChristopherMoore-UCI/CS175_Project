By placing Kirk (spirit), Spock (reason) and Bones (emotion) into challenging situations, we get to observe this interplay of these forces, and we get to hear them say what would otherwise be an internal dialogue. Mr. Spock, then, can be trusted to give reasoned scientific analysis in any situation, uninfluenced by emotion or spirit. Students read relevant chapters in the book, and then every other week we watch a related "Star Trek" episode. One of the recurring plot elements in "Star Trek" is the discovery of new creatures. Plato described the human soul as composed of three aspects: spirit, reason and emotion.
