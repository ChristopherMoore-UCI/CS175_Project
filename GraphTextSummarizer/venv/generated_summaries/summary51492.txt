In the end, it was a study of firefighters that helped persuade the government to include cancer in the WTC Health Program. And while someone's cancer may have been caused by his or her work at ground zero, it might also have been a coincidence -- they may have gotten cancer anyway. Researchers found firefighters who worked at ground zero were 19% more likely to develop cancer than firefighters who did not. There were subtle increases seen in a few cancers in particular, including gastro-esophageal cancers and blood cancers such as multiple myeloma and non-Hodgkin's lymphoma. A few months ago Hilaire received a letter from the CDC's National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health, officially offering him medical insurance under the World Trade Center Health Program.
