Since Monday, there has been a ban on motorcycles and bicycles in Baghdad until further notice, and a two-day ban on any vehicles in cities begins Sunday, they said. A curfew will be in place in cities from midnight to 5 a.m. on Saturday, Sunday and Monday, officials said. "The conduct and outcome of the election will be the most decisive moment for Iraqis' future since 2003," Ad Melkert, the head of the U.N. mission in Iraq, said Monday. A public holiday began Thursday and will run until Monday because of the elections. Provincial borders will be sealed, preventing movements between provinces, from Saturday to Monday.
