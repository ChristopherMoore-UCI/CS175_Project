"Iraqi security forces believe that those attackers drove all the way down from desert areas close to Bayji, about 200 km (124 miles) north of Haditha," Salman said. When they asked them to stop until they involve more security officials in their mission, the gunmen began clashes with security forces that lasted an hour, Salman said. The attackers also left some leaflets behind warning Iraqi security that they will carry out more attacks, Salman said. Three of the attackers were killed in shootouts with officers, according to Khalid Salman, head of the Haditha local council The gunmen then headed to the Haditha police station, where they convinced officers they were an official force sent by the federal government to carry out the arrests, Salman said.
