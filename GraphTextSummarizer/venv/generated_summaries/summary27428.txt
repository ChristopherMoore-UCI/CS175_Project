The use of biometrics (broadly defined as technologies that identify people via physiological characteristics) has expanded rapidly in recent years. -- From the fingerprints and digital imaging stored on e-passports, to iris scanners set up at airport immigration, biometrics are a growing part of the traveller experience. According to a recent study by market research firm ABI, investment in biometrics will drive global spending in the field to $7.3 billion by 2013, up from around $3 billion this year. "In the last couple of years the technology has leapt forward in terms of its reliability, its accuracy and its robustness," says Nick Morse, from UK Biometrics, a supplier of biometrics technology to the British market.
