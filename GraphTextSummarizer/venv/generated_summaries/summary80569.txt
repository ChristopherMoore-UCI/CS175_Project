At a spice shop just down the road, meanwhile, the third generation Indian owner tells me that Kenyan Asians are spread widely across industries like construction, retail and tourism. "(My family is involved) in quite a lot," said one businesswoman of Indian heritage we meet in a nearby restaurant. Melting pot Shah is one of the many Kenyan Asians who today run their own businesses in the Westlands and Parklands districts of the Kenyan capital. In the port's heyday "there was ivory being sold like the way we sell cakes now or mahamris as we call them," said historian Satmbuli Abdillahi Nassir. Having started construction of railway lines in India nearly 50 years earlier, Punjabi engineers and laborers had the skills the British required.
