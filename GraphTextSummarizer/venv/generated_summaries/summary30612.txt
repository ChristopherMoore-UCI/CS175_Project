As such, the rise in demand has turned Agadir -- famed for its beautiful beaches and laid-back culture -- into a hive of small-scale worker co-ops, each dedicated to squeezing oil from the notoriously hard-shelled argan nut. But times have changed, and according to the Argan Oil Society, all the co-operatives are now staffed and run by local women. Traditionally, it was uncommon for Berber women to work outside the home.
