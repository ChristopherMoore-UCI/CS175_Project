The newest iconic images from Turkey and from Brazil -- two countries that have promoted themselves as models to emulate -- include shocking scenes of police brutality, of government clampdown against peaceful protesters. The sight of police spraying giant clouds of tear gas and beating peaceful protesters touched a nerve, inflaming simmering concerns about an increasingly authoritarian regime. When President Obama arrived in Northern Ireland on Monday for the meeting of the G8 nations, there were more police officers than protesters. Local issues have a new power to surge with little warning, electrifying the crowds, jolting people out of complacency. We have entered a new age of protests.
