The 33-year-old is the captain of Japan's women's soccer team who lifted the FIFA Women's World Cup in Germany last June. The pay gap between the genders is stark and most of the members of Sawa's World Cup team have full time jobs and could only train in the evenings. -- If there is anyone who embodies the fighting spirit of Japan's disaster-laden year, it is Homare Sawa. "We fought for ourselves and for Japan," says Sawa. So when the Japanese talk about "ganbaro" (Japanese for standing firm in the face of unspeakable odds), Sawa's success is a shining example of how it's possible.
