Unlike some big screen animated features from the United States that rely almost completely on computer animation, in Japan, almost all features are drawn by hand -- a labor-intensive craft practiced by thousands of young artists each year. Yasuo Yamaguchi, executive director of the Association of Japanese Animators, said the industry has been rocked by the country's deepest recession since World War II. Many of these entry-level jobs have been outsourced to the Philippines and South Korea in recent years. Revenues peaked in 2006 but have since fallen off, as lower advertising revenues lead to fewer new programs.
