Since then she has been fervently critical of vaccination, claiming vaccines such as those for measles, mumps and rubella cause autism. Since the age of onset of autism is similar to when children receive vaccinations, some parents falsely connect these two unrelated events. Before measles vaccinations were available, every year in the United States, approximately 500,000 people were infected with measles and 500 died. Autism, in fact, appears to be rising among unvaccinated children as well. She backtracked her stance on vaccines in an op-ed she wrote in April 2014, claiming she is not "anti-vaccine," but the damage has been done.
