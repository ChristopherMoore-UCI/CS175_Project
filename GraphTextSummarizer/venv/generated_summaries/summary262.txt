By the last month of Jonestown's existence, Jones was so intoxicated at times that he had great difficulty even reading the news aloud to his followers. "He was very charismatic," Leslie Wagner-Wilson, a Jonestown survivor, says of the Rev. Jim Jones. Audiotapes of such sessions reveal Jones laughing, apparently entertained. How much more attraction can you have than to be ready to have your eyes plucked out?" By the end at Jonestown, Jones was more rock star than preacher. Like all powerful speakers, Jones' greatest asset was his ability to determine what listeners wanted to hear and give it to them in simple language that appealed to them on an almost instinctual level.
