This week, MME looks at the path of Islamic finance in the economies touched by the Arab Spring. From the election of moderate Islamic group of Ennahda in Tunisia to talk of Sharia Law in Libya. Western politicians and businesses are keeping a keen eye on whether the Arab Spring has opened the door for Islamic finance. In the wake of a season of regional revolts, Islamist parties are reasserting themselves in the post- Arab Spring states. Investor confidence has been dampened by a shaky political climate, while ratings agency, Moody's, downgraded the North African country citing economic weakness and financial deterioration.
