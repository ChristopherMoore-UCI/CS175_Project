Before we start blaming agencies for failing to stop the Boston bombers, and before we push "intelligence reforms" that will shred civil liberties without making us any safer, we need to stop seeing the past as a bunch of obvious dots that need connecting. But before a terrorist attack, when there are millions of dots -- some important but the vast majority unimportant -- uncovering plots is a lot harder. Is it a sailboat, a puppy, two guys with pressure-cooker bombs or just an unintelligible mess of dots? We have no idea how many potential "dots" the FBI, CIA, NSA and other agencies collect, but it's easily in the millions. Opinion: Agencies often miss warning signs of attacks
