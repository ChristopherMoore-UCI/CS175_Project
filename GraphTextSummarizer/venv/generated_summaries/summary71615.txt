The Colombian military on Sunday rescued  Gen. Luis Mendieta and Col. Enrique Murillo, two top national police officers held by the FARC guerrillas for nearly 12 years, President Alvaro Uribe announced. "May I allow myself to communicate to the Colombian people, from Quibdo, some great news: our joint military forces just rescued General Mendieta and Colonel Murillo," the president said. A police spokesman said the military operation took place in eastern Colombia in Guaviare province, and that the two rescued hostages are safe. According to Colombia's El Espectador Newspaper, Mendieta celebrated his 53rd birthday on Sunday. Mendieta and Murillo were both taken prisoner by the FARC in jungle town of Mitu.
