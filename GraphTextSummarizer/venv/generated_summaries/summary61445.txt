Deadline reports that Sony Pictures is planning an all-male "Ghostbusters" reboot starring Channing Tatum and directed and produced by brothers Joe Russo and Anthony Russo. "We want to expand the Ghostbusters universe in ways that will include different films, TV shows, merchandise, all things that are part of modern filmed entertainment," Deadline quoted Reitman as saying. The studio is also creating a production company, Ghostcorps, which will include Ivan Reitman and Dan Aykroyd, who were among the original "Ghostbusters" team, according to Deadline. "It's just the beginning of what I hope will be a lot of wonderful movies," Deadline quoted Reitman as saying. All all-male version is tentatively expected to begin filming in the summer of 2016, to be released the following year, Reitman told Deadline.
