The decade saw the full flower of youth culture, which was intertwined with Vietnam War protests, increasing drug use and distinctive music. Perlstein says he's long had an obsession with the '60s -- which, in "Nixonland," start with the Democratic landslide of 1964 and end with the Nixon landslide of 1972. If Perlstein's history of the 1960s and early '70s in America has a throughline, it's mistrust. And in the midst of it all was Richard Nixon: Red-baiter, former vice president, failed gubernatorial nominee, punch line, political strategist and president, a master at playing both sides to maintain his hold on power. Richard Nixon was "the guy who exploited these tensions to create a new kind of politics," says Rick Perlstein.
