So far, tablets (even the iPad) are mainly intended for media consumption, simple interaction, and light content creation or curating. Tablets like the GenTouch could become popular among people who have never owned a computer, or who own a computer and just want light web access on the go. It's is often available for free at schools, libraries, and many kinds of businesses; but it's a bigger hurdle for people in rural or poor communities. In fact, this device is roughly the size and cost of the Wi-Fi version of the latest Amazon Kindle. That's a big market -- one that probably includes people who wouldn't ever purchase a computer, netbook, or smartphone.
