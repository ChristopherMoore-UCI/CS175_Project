But as an adult, she's working to change that, promoting the work of Native American fashion designers and artists. "It's disheartening to see Native American fashion as cheap knockoffs," Yellowtail said. "To change that, we have to be the voice for what Native American fashion is, instead of just complaining about it." Part of the work is advancing mainstream America's perception of Native fashion, going beyond headdresses, fringed jackets and, yes, buckskin.
