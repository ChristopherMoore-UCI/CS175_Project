Douglas Erwin, president of The Paleontological Society and curator of the Smithsonian's National Museum of Natural History, said "theft of fossils from pubic lands has long been a problem." An omnibus public lands bill, which the U.S. Senate passed Thursday, includes penalties for fossil theft from public land. Paleontologist Nate Murphy is expected to plead guilty to stealing fossils from federal land. In a written statement sent to CNN on Friday, he said such thefts "can often result in the loss of important scientific information and the disappearance of specimens that belong to the public.
