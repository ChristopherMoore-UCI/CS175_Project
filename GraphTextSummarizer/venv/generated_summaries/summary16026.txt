The end product, ready for export, is pure sheep skin prepared to be turned into gloves -- labeled with the thickness and the area it covers. "The fashion glove is made of sheep skin which is unique to Ethiopia," explains Tsedenia Mekbib, general manager at Pittards Products Manufacturing. And it's not just Pittards that have realized the opportunity to make gloves in Ethiopia. Export ban In a move to encourage value addition and increase revenues generated by the leather sector, the Ethiopian government banned all exports of raw hides and skins in 1989. Shoes is another major area which uses Ethiopian leather.
