After 22 years of speaking out against corruption, first as a lawyer and then as a congresswoman, De León says she remains fearful given the legacy of violence and instability in Guatemala. President Álvaro Colom and his party, the National Union of Hope, came to power last year with a promise to crack down on corruption, crime, poverty and impunity. Anabella De León is not well known outside Guatemala. The inquiry is being led by the International Commission against Impunity in Guatemala (CICIG), a United Nations-backed body established in 2007 to battle corruption in the country. "People in Guatemala are tired, tired of injustice, tired of corruption, tired of abuses, of violations," she said.
