Some of it might sound like an idea straight from the 1950s -- a car made for women -- complete with numerous pink features, as well as a windshield and air conditioning that claims to help a woman's sensitive skin. "She's" sticking to a fairly predictable road with pink stitching in the seats, pink tweaks in the dashboard and a windshield that the company says blocks more ultraviolet rays. Kawaii is the one word we heard nearly every time we showed the car's brochure to women on the streets of Tokyo. In fact, the idea of marketing a car to women was tried in the 1950s. -- Japanese car makers are grappling with sluggish demand at home and abroad as well as a strong yen.
