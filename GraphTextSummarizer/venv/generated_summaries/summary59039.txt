Carole Simpson says women are gaining prominence at a time when major network news is endangered. Women began entering television news in significant numbers in the 1970s during the women's liberation movement. With fewer resources and the death knell sounding, why not put women in charge of the network evening news programs? The audience for network news has been dwindling year after year as viewers turn to cable or the Internet for their news, or avoid the news altogether. Millions of Americans still watch network news programs, but they are only a semblance of what they were in terms of quality and content.
