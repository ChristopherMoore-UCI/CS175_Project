While most Southwest Airlines travelers -- 93 percent --  managed to avoid extra fees, passengers flying with Continental Airlines, JetBlue, Delta Airlines, American Airlines, United Airlines, US Airways, Alaska Airlines and Frontier Airlines had to pay fees. The passengers shared their experiences from a collective 29,720 domestic flights from January 2010 to January 2011. -- A new Consumer Reports survey reveals that air travelers want comfort without emptying their wallets -- and many airlines don't deliver. Southwest Airlines topped the rankings for overall satisfaction, with JetBlue Airways coming in second in the survey of nearly 15,000 passengers. These survey results were compiled before cracks were discovered in some of Southwest Airlines' planes.
