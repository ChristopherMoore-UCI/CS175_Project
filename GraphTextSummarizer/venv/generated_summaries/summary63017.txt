Research by Wharton management professor Maurice Schweitzer and colleagues shows that when people are rewarded for goal achievement, they are more likely to engage in unethical behavior, such as cheating by overstating their performance, especially when they fall just short of their goals. One way to keep us on track is to identify ways to make ethical standards salient at the time we face ethical challenges. The ways that goals and financial incentives are set can lead most people not to follow their moral compass. Even merely thinking about money leads people to be less helpful and fair in their dealings with others, to be less sensitive to social rejection and to work harder toward personal goals. Here is the logic behind the incentive system: Reducing wait times by getting patients seen quickly is good for both patients and hospitals.
