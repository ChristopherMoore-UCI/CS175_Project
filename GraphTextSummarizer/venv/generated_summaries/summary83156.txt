Big chunky glitter gives off a different kind of light than finer glitter. Glass glitter, from Germany, has been used on Christmas ornaments, but unlike plastic or mylar-based glitter options, glass glitter requires careful handling. The newest glitters, according to Brill, are made with lasers and have a more brilliant reflective quality than glitters of the past. Speaking by phone from Kurt Adler's New York headquarters, Brill struggled to find the words to describe the magic that glitter adds to an object. Plastic foil glitter, Brill said, was developed in the United States after World War II because German glass glitter was difficult to obtain.
