Andrew Grant-Thomas, the deputy director of the Kirwan Institute for the Study of Race and Ethnicity, says he believes the statistics for unemployed black Americans is far greater than what's being reported. The report says legislation needs to focus on minority job training, tax incentives and extensions for unemployment benefits. In addition, black-owned businesses represent a paltry 5 percent of privately owned companies, and the study says more are needed to help spur job growth in minority communities. The figures don't include people not actively seeking employment or people with higher education degrees forced to take jobs for which they're overqualified.
