In the United States, the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act distinguishes between U.S. persons and non-U.S. persons. The FISA statute explicitly protects First Amendment rights to free speech, which include criticisms of government policy and peaceful protests against government actions. Moreover, foreign countries know as much or more, often from the public record, of United States defensive capabilities.
