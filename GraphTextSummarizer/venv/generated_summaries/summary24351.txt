In 1920 guerilla warfare between what became known as the IRA and Unionists led to the partitioning of southern Ireland and Northern Ireland. The long road to peace The "troubles" have been a centuries-old dispute between England and Ireland over who controls Northern Ireland. Today, Northern Ireland is still part of the United Kingdom but the British government has said if there is a majority of votes then a united Ireland will be possible. For those that have lived through the turmoil in Northern Ireland, peace achieved though diplomacy must have seemed like an unrealistic goal. But diplomacy has worked in bringing peace to Northern Ireland.
