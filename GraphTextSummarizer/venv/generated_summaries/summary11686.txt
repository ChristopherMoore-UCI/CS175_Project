Despite food safety measures, the threat of foodborne illness remains in meat and produce -- and some types of illness are on the rise, recent reports say. When you get home, storing raw meats in a plastic bag or container to prevent any juices from dripping on other foods is also important. Salmonella remained the top cause of foodborne illness last year, according to the CDC's 2012 report card on food poisoning, issued Thursday. Although pesticides are not a cause of foodborne illness, produce can be a source of food poisoning. At the store A CDC study published in 2010 once again highlighted the fact that young children can be exposed to raw meat and poultry products while riding in shopping carts, particularly if they ride in the basket of the cart.
