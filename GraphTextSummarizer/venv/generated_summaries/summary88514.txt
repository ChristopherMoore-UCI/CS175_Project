Its inventors, RCA graduates Isabel Lizardi, Matt Johnson, Bibi Nelson and Becky Pilditch, call their creation "Bare Paint." Today, Bare Paint jars and pens are sold on the Internet and stocked by Radio Shack electronics stores across the United States. Johnson says that conductive paint opens up an enormous range of creative opportunities. Projects being done by Bare Paint users include everything from interactive color wheels to homemade electric toys. According to Johnson, the online encyclopedia provided them with almost everything they needed to know about crafting conductive materials.
