Captain Marvel Jr. saves the bad-hair day Like most American kids in the 1940s, Elvis Presley fantasized about growing up to be like his favorite comic book superheroes. If you think comic book characters do amazing things in comic books, you won't believe what they can do off the page. During his early teen years, Elvis was obsessed with Captain Marvel Jr., known as "America's most famous boy hero." A copy of Captain Marvel Jr. Captain Marvel Jr. paid tribute to The King in one issue, referring to the singer as "the greatest modern-day philosopher."
