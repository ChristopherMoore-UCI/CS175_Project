This was "Milosc," playing free jazz, going so far as to describe its form of jazz as "yass" thereby creating a new stream or genre in Polish jazz. Jazz was being featured in TV programmes; jazz critique was growing as were jazz festivals. In Stalinist Poland, jazz was banned and went on to develop its own style underground. A multiplicity of small jazz clubs have sprung up throughout the country as have a number of jazz festivals and independent record labels. The 50s also saw the first official jazz festival taking place in Sopot beginning a tradition of jazz festivals in Poland that lasts to this day.
