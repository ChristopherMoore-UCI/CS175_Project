Kompasu whips through South Korea Kompasu also unleashed torrential rain and strong winds on North Korea Thursday, according to the state-run KCNA news agency. -- Three people died after Typhoon Kompasu hit central South Korea Thursday morning, the Yonhap News Agency reported. The typhoon was expected to further devastate crops in secretive North Korea, which has been gripped by food shortages. According to Yonhap: A flying roof tile killed an 80-year-old man in Seosan, South Chungcheong province. As of late afternoon Thursday, Kompasu was carrying maximum winds of 55 miles per hour and had moved away from both Koreas.
