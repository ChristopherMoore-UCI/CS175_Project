When the message or link is opened, social network users are lured to fake Web sites that trick them into divulging personal details and passwords. Cybercriminals are employing phishing and malware attacks for a number of reasons, including trying to redirect users to sites where profit is fueled by the number of visitors. Most sites have information pages dedicated to educating users about the risks of Internet scams. Social networking sites show there is ample opportunity to find more victims; the average Facebook user has 120 friends on the site. Security experts said it makes sense that cybercriminals are turning to social networking sites.
