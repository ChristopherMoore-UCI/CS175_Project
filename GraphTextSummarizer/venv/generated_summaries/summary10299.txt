The big record labels, once more prone to awkward lawsuits against individual file swappers, now allow fans to play their songs free on social networking sites like Last.fm that use music as the social glue. In music circles now, "music wants to be shared" seems to be the idea. And music sharing seems ever more entwined in everyday life, sometimes in subtle ways. "Jook did something amazing by just saying, 'let's put this social media connection on the lanyard, make it cheap and make it multiplatform,'" says Andrew Hawn, a strategist with consumer trends research firm Iconoculture.
