Since mid-August, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention had confirmed 153 cases of respiratory illness caused by Enterovirus D68 in 18 states: Alabama, Colorado, Connecticut, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Minnesota, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, New Jersey, New York, Oklahoma, Pennsylvania and Virginia. Two of the confirmed cases are children between 5 and 9, said Dr. Danuta Skowronski, lead epidemiologist on emerging respiratory viruses at the British Columbia Centre for Disease Control. " Canadian health officials have confirmed three cases of Enterovirus D68 in British Columbia. But Enterovirus D68 seems to be exacerbating breathing problems in children who have asthma. "Several states are investigating clusters of people with severe respiratory illness, and specimens are still being tested for EV-D68.
