The dynamics of group buying mean that Groupon has a significant early advantage, however: The more users a site can gain, the more leverage it has to score big discounts from businesses. Web rumors this week suggest Groupon may be in the process of raising a new round of venture capital at a $1.2 billion valuation. With almost constant web connectivity provided by smartphones and free Wi-Fi at coffee shops, airports and bookstores -- not to mention the immediacy of Twitter and Facebook -- we've come to expect instant gratification online. There's a further trend Groupon capitalizes upon: Social networking brings us together to share opinions and experiences ... why not deals too? Groupon's competitors are seeing big investment, too.
