Dark tourism is a broad ranging and often contentious consumer activity that can provoke debate about how death and the dead are packaged up and consumed within the modern visitor economy. Much of its history revolves around World War II atrocities. The worst natural catastrophe of the century thus far, the tsunami left a trail of havoc along Thailand's Andaman Coast. When "dark tourism" destinations are presented with artistic panache the results go far beyond partisan politics and propaganda purposes. But there's a brighter side to dark tourism.
