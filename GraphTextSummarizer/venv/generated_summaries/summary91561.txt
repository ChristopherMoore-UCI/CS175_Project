Truman about 11 p.m. Wednesday, Navy officials said. -- Three U.S. Navy aviators are missing at sea after a command and control plane crashed over the Atlantic on Wednesday night, Navy officials said Thursday. Navy officials said they do not know what caused the plane to crash, and search and rescue missions are under way from units on the Truman, the carrier USS Eisenhower and the Coast Guard. The E-2C  Hawkeye was conducting exercises off the Virginia-North Carolina coast. The plane was part of a training squadron, VAW 120, and is based at the Naval Station Norfolk in southern Virginia.
