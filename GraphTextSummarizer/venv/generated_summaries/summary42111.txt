She saw the disbelief in my eyes, smiled, and said: "They buy suitcases for their suitcases." "Oh," I said, surmising that, in these circles, "jets" was a synonym for "private jets." One morning I walked to the Hermès store at the corner of 62nd Street and Madison Avenue. At that Hermès store in New York, I asked the clerk why someone would pay so much for a suitcase that would inevitably get scuffed and scratched in transit. "The people who pay that much for a bag usually fly on jets," she said.
