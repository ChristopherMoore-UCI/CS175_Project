Google is offering one terabyte of Google Drive storage for Internet subscribers and is heavily pushing its Chromebook for $299. Never before have so many people all of a sudden thought, "I wonder if Kansas City is a nice place to live?" That's because Google recently announced the details behind Google Fiber, its incredibly fast new Internet service that'll cost eligible Kansas City residents $70 per month. If Google Fiber ever gets out of Kansas City, other Internet providers better watch out. As long as people have slow Internet connections, relying solely on Google's cloud isn't really an option.
