After students share their responses to Dr. King, pose the following questions for class discussion: Correlated Standards Social Studies II. Time, Continuity and Change Social studies programs should include experiences that provide for the study of the ways human beings view themselves in and over time. Direct each student to compose a response to Dr. King, explaining what life is like in the U.S. today and to what extent his dream has been realized. Then, challenge each student to compare Dr. King's dream to the reality of life in the U.S. in the year 2009. Inform students that, on August 28, 1963, Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. shared his vision of racial equality in America in his historic "I Have a Dream" speech.
