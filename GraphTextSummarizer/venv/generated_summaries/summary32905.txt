Teen access to Plan B uneven The program, which also allows for the distribution of condoms and birth control pills, began in January 2011. One New Yorker who strongly supports the program -- which, so far, has led to more than 1,100 students receiving birth control pills, including Plan B, according to the health department -- is its mayor. At a time when half of teen mothers never graduate high school and 47% of U.S. high schoolers say they are sexually active, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, they say that the more options teens have, the better. Others, though, feel that it is a misguided and possibly dangerous intrusion into the lives of families and the authority of parents. Parents can "opt out" by signing and returning a form to the school.
