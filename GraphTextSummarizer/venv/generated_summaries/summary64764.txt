According to King's advisers, Clarence B. Jones and Andrew Young, Martin would have utilized Twitter and other social media. And, surprisingly, much of it fits within the most constraining of social media platforms, the 140 character limit of Twitter. And, a more important question: How well would Martin Luther King, Jr.'s gift for rhetoric translate over social networks? What about today, in the age of social media? He would have recognized that social media is the public square of the 21st century.
