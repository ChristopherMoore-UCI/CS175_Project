Striking back once the enemy had overextended itself was central to German DNA throughout World War II. In World War I the Germans executed 48 men for desertion; during World War II that figure rose to 30,000. At the start of World War II the best German units were more than a match for their Allied opposition -- but by 1944 that had changed radically.
