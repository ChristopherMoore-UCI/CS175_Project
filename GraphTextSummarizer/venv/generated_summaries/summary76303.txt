Democrats need to compete harder in red states Of the 12 new U.S. House seats that will be created this year because of population shifts revealed by the 2010 Census, eight are in solidly red states: Arizona, Georgia, South Carolina, Texas and Utah. These processes don't get much attention, but the stakes are high: They help determine control of Congress and state legislatures and, because they determine the Electoral College map, who wins the White House. Redistricting of House and state legislative districts will occur in all states by early next year. The 2010 Census numbers will take two House seats each from Ohio and New York and give seats to states like Georgia and Arizona.
