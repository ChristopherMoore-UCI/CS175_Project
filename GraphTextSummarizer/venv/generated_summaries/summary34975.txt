Flying there by helicopter, a thin film of oil over the water is visible -- evidence of the biggest industry in the Caspian Sea region: hydrocarbons. Over 60 years after it was built and Oil Rocks is still producing oil and gas, still contributing to the Azeri economy. Oil Rocks is a fascinating link in the chain of eastern supply and western demand. Oil Rocks was the world's first attempt to tap into offshore reserves, buried beneath the water. In 1900, half of the world's production of crude oil came from this corner of the former Soviet Union.
