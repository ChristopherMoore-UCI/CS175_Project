Some voices argue that eco-tourism could provide a small but possible alternative to mining, helping local communities generate income and also preserve the ecosystem. "These sands are mineralized," says Andrew Denton, general manager of mining at RBM. According to RBM, the minerals removed from the sand through mining comprise about 5% of the dunes' total volume. Environmental lawyer Jeremy Ridl is part of a group of eco-activists who successfully blocked dune mining in the area of St. Lucia -- north of where RBM is currently working -- in the 1990s. Ridl describes mining operations in the region as "completely destructive.
