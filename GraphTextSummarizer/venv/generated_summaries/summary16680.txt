Thousands of Somalis flee as troops close in Kismayo, south of the capital of Mogadishu, is a key battleground between African Union forces and Al-Shabaab, an Islamic group that has long tried to overthrow the Somali government, but has been increasingly beleaguered. The looming fight for Kismayo prompted thousands of civilians to flee the city this month, according to the U.N. refugee agency. Related: Soldiers killed in blast targeting new president CNN's Lillian Leposo and journalist Mohamed Amiin Adow contributed to this report. Related: Mogadishu bombing kills broadcaster Kenyan forces said last week they were using land, sea and air forces to attack the militant group before the Kismayo offensive. After losing control of much of Mogadishu last year, Kismayo became even more critical to the militant group.
