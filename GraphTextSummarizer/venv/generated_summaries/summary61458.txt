The quake startled Davy Ebanks, general manager of the North Sound Club, a golf course on the Seven Mile Beach strip of western Grand Cayman Island. Tuesday's quake struck about 32 miles (52 kilometers) east-southeast of Bodden Town in the center of the southern coast of Grand Cayman Island. George Town, the capital, is on the western shore of Grand Cayman Island. There are reports that the quake toppled power lines in Bodden Town, said Kafara Augustine, a news producer for Cayman 27. The 6.2-mile deep quake hit at 9:23 a.m. ET, 40 miles from George Town, Cayman Islands, the USGS reported.
