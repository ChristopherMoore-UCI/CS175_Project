Police are running three separate investigations -- into phone hacking, e-mail hacking, and police bribery -- and two parliamentary committees and an independent inquiry are also probing British press practices. A former spokesman for Prime Minister David Cameron, ex-News of the World editor Andy Coulson, is among those arrested. News of the World was 168 years old and among the best-selling newspapers in the world when it closed. Under Murdoch, News of the World specialized in undercover investigations and salacious stories that earned it the nickname "News of the Screws." The parent company of both tabloids, News International, "closed our sister paper the News of the World over the phone hacking scandal.
