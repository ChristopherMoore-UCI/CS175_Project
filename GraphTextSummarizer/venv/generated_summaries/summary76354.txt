" Ko has no doubt that Nishikori's run in New York represents Japan's greatest moment in tennis and one of its greatest in any sport. Public interest and attendances surged last year after he became the first home champion of the Japan Open since 1972 -- he won the title in October 2012 -- and peaked again this week due to his New York success. A Japan Times article praised the "tennis marvels ... doing as much to put Japan right before the world as all the diplomats put together." -- With his heroics at the U.S. Open in New York, Kei Nishikori has blazed a trail through history. After Japan had recovered enough to contemplate sport, tennis remained popular with aristocrats -- including the royal family and Crown Prince Akihito -- but public interest slipped away toward baseball and football, and the talent production line ceased.
