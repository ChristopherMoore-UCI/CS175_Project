He was, in fact, network television's first anchorman; his broadcast -- "Douglas Edwards with the News," it was called -- first aired at the dinner hour on CBS in 1948, and continued until 1962, when Edwards was told he was being replaced by Cronkite. Douglas Edwards was the anchorman of CBS' evening news broadcasts for 14 years. When ABC began its "Nightline" program, Edwards held out hope that CBS would try to match it late at night.
