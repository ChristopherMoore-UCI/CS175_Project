-- Prince William and Catherine, Duchess of Cambridge, presented medals Saturday to soldiers of the Irish Guards who have served in Afghanistan. The 1st Battalion Irish Guards returned in April from a six-month tour in Afghanistan. "Along with thousands of others back home, I followed your tour in Afghanistan with a mounting sense of awe. The regiment, which is based at the Victoria Barracks in Windsor, was formed in 1900 by order of Queen Victoria. About 400 Irish Guards received operational medals during the ceremony, British media reported, with the duke and duchess pausing to speak to each soldier as they presented them.
