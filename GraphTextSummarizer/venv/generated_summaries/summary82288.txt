How he and other key leaders take up stands in this struggle for ideological dominance will be immensely important for China and its direction in the next decade. After three decades of reforms, China's social economic landscape has been transformed. In the post-Mao era, Chinese leadership change and power transition have become institutionalized and more predictable. China is in urgent need of a soul, a set of dominant ideas, as the efficacy of the Chinese Communist Party's official ideology -- emphasizing "harmonious society" and "scientific development" -- diminishes. Unlike leaders like Bo Xilai who used iron-fisted measures when dealing with social discontent, Wang has a reputation for listening to netizens, including their criticisms of the government.
