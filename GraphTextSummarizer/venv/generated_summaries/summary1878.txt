We are thrilled to see so many people embracing the Teal Pumpkin Project as a way to ensure kids with food allergies can enjoy a safe, fun Halloween experience just like their friends," says Veronica LaFemina, spokeswoman for Food Allergy Research & Education. No tricks this Halloween, just allergy-friendly treats Tips for parents Parents should also use extra caution when checking Halloween candy if their child has an allergy. With eggs, soy and wheat also common allergies in kids, according to the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases, finding treats without tricks can be a challenge on Halloween night. That's why the Food Allergy Research & Education organization wants Halloween lovers to go teal this holiday. Halloween can be extra scary for kids with food allergies.
