Within the sample group there was a 27% drop in the risk of contracting colorectal cancer if you switch from fully vegetarian to eating fish. "While the study is observational and cannot prove a cause/effect relationship, it is exciting to think that in addition to regular screenings, a diet rich in fish and fiber-rich foods may play an important role in reducing the risk of colorectal cancer." It is the third most diagnosed cancer, and the third leading cause of cancer-related death in the US in 2014, according to American Cancer Society statistics. "In addition to other dietary factors, fish may provide added protection from its high content omega-3 fatty acids. The authors of the study suggest that omega-3 fatty acids may be the key to such a low risk of cancer in the pescetarian group.
