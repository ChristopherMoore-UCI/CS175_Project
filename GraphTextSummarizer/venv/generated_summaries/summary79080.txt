Republican lawmakers overwhelmingly agreed on Sunday talk shows that shutting down the government to avoid unilateral action by President Barack Obama on immigration is not a good option "I think there's got to be more productive ways for us to be able to impress on the President the need to work for a permanent solution as opposed to a temporary stop-gap solution," Mitt Romney, former Massachusetts governor and two-time presidential candidate, said on the CBS show "Face the Nation. Speaking at the G20 Summit in Australia on Sunday morning, Obama outlined his conditions. Despite vocalizing his aggressive plans for unilateral action on immigration, Obama did concede that any executive action he takes could be undone by his successor. Obama is scheduled to return to Washington on Sunday night, and the issue is expected to be at the top of his agenda. "The very nature of executive authority means future presidents can reverse those actions," Obama said.
