A job that requires a lot of risk The head of emergency services in Gaza, Dr. Bashar Mourad, directs the nearest ambulance to the latest Israeli airstrike. "The anger boils inside us as the pressure grows, knowing our actions are a matter of life or death." says 39-year-old Yassin Mutier, the station chief of Tel El Hawa ambulance station. This close-knit band of paramedics and ambulance drivers risk their lives saving others on the front lines of Gaza's wars. The fight between the Israeli military and Gaza militants began over a week ago. The room erupts into cheering and song as the radio announces that the Egyptian government has proposed a cease-fire between Israel and Gaza militants for the following day.
