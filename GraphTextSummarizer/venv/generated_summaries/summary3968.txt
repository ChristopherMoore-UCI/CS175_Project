Jones, 81, died Friday at Vanderbilt University Medical Center in Nashville, the public relations firm said. By 1959, Jones had moved to Nashville and recorded his first No. 1, "White Lightning. Jones continued to struggle in the early '80s -- once leading police on a car chase in Nashville -- but with the help of his fourth wife, Nancy Sepulvado, he got clean.
