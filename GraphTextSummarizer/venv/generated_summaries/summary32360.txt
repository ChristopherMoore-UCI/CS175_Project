The bill strips intelligence community employees of their pensions if the Director of National Intelligence decides they leaked classified information, even if the information reveals only that Dagestani weddings last three days. How to deal with disclosures of classified information about government misconduct is a tricky question. Intelligence officials could raise concerns through approved government channels, but if that failed would have no meaningful way to bring evidence of government fraud, waste, abuse, or illegality to the public's attention. How to deal with disclosures of unclassified information about government misconduct should be a no-brainer. At the end of July, the Senate intelligence committee marked up legislation drafted in response to recent high-profile leaks of classified information.
