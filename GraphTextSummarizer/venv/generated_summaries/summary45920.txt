"He died right where he loved to be -- and that was doing eye care in remote areas," Little said from her home in New York. Risking their lives to help disadvantaged Afghans became almost a norm for Tom and Libby Little. Tom Little was heavily involved with training in optometry overseas, "enabling Afghans to start picking up the work of eye care, because it wasn't there," his widow said. Tom Little, a New York optometrist, was among 10 people killed by gunmen in Badakhshan, a remote northeastern region of the country. It was the remote areas of Afghanistan, Libby Little said, where the need for her husband's services was often greatest.
