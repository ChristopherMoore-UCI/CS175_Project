While drones are flown on missions in war zones such as Iraq and Afghanistan, the actual "cockpit" of the unmanned aircraft is on U.S. soil -- the operators work at Nevada's Creech Air Force Base. Unmanned aerial vehicles have become indispensable for military planners who depend on constant combat air patrols. In recent years, the United States has sharply increased its used of unmanned planes to target insurgents in Pakistan's tribal region, a volatile area bordering war-torn Afghanistan. -- The systems that control U.S. military drones have been infected with a computer virus, a U.S. defense official confirmed to CNN on Monday. The infection was first reported by Noah Shachtman for Wired magazine last week.
