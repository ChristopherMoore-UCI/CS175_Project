Cambodia calls for investigation Cambodia Prime Minister Hun Sen called for an inquiry into the mass HIV infection last week. More than 800 panicked residents of Rokar village sought testing after reports of infections emerged last week. An unlicensed doctor has been charged with causing an HIV outbreak in a remote village in northwestern Battambang province, local media report. "After questioning Yem Chroeum, he confessed he did order his son-in-law to burn down the evidence behind his house, and (treated) patients with negligence, as well as using the same needles to treat them," Chet Vanny, deputy police chief of Battambang province told the Phnom Penh Post.
