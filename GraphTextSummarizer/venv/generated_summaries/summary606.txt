READ MORE: Dolphins killed as Taiji's controversial hunting season resumes Japan officials defend controversial hunt The albino dolphin currently sits in a small holding pen where it is being trained to eat dead fish and adapt to human interaction, said members of the Sea Shepherd conservationist group, which monitors dolphin hunts in the region. "This brutal hunt is carnage carried out in the name of profit, not culture," said Melissa Sehgal, a Sea Shepherd campaign coordinator. Most dolphins are killed for their meat, while some are sold live to aquariums around the world. The controversial hunt takes place annually between September and March in Taiji, Japan.
