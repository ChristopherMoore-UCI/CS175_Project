Former Argentine Nestor Kirchner accepts defeat Sunday in legislative elections in Buenos Aires. Nestor Kirchner also lost a race for a congressional seat in Buenos Aires province. -- Former Argentine President Nestor Kirchner resigned as leader of the ruling political party Monday following a poor showing in Sunday's elections, the official news agency Telam reported. The new composition of Congress could shift power away from Argentine President Cristina Fernandez de Kirchner, Kirchner's wife. He lost his bid to Francisco de Narvaez, who leads a rival Peronist party, Union PRO, by a tally of 34.6 percent to 32.1 percent.
