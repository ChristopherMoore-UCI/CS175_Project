We should all be troubled by Sony's decision to entirely cancel the release of the film "The Interview" in response to threats of physical violence by belligerent hackers. The Sony attack made headlines in part because it revealed Hollywood secrets and celebrity squabbles that were fodder for celebrity gossip and well-suited for tabloid spectacle. They shattered any notion we may have had about our personal data and private communications remaining private. Sony is, in fact, just the latest victim in a recent, escalating spate of such attacks aimed at undermining our economic and national security interests.
