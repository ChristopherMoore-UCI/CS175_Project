"There's a whole lot of gray areas where intermediate agents, sub-brokers, are taking advantage of loopholes in the law in order to extract large amounts of money from vulnerable people, uninformed (people) who are going off to work in a country where they don't speak the language," Plant said. "Eighty percent of forced labor is in the private economy, but this is very, very rarely been prosecuted, if at all in most countries," said Roger Plant, one of the authors of the ILO report. Marietta Dias, who runs the Migrant Workers Society, says the mind-set regarding immigrant workers here has got to change. The exploitation of workers is a huge business worldwide. The ILO tried to set standards for private agencies that place workers, he said.
