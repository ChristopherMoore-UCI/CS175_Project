Abbott didn't deny he had sought an assurance of loyalty from his deputy, Foreign Minister Julie Bishop. Since his election in September 2013, Abbott has accumulated more critics than supporters. He is appearing to be a tin-eared leader, out of touch with ordinary Australians. He's managed to keep one of his central promises: to dump a carbon tax introduced by Julia Gillard, the predecessor he maligned for breaking promises. So when Australia's Tony Abbott faced journalists and his colleagues to deliver a highly anticipated speech in Canberra on Monday, he fessed up and begged for time.
