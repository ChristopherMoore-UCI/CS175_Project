Perhaps no one better understands what the family of U.S. Rep. Gabrielle Giffords is going through like the widow and children of Punjab Governor Salmaan Taseer. Governor's accused killer makes unscheduled court appearance Taseer had been an outspoken critic of Pakistan's blasphemy laws, which make it a crime to insult Islam or the Prophet Mohammad. "I mean, my heart is totally with the congresswoman," says daughter Sara Taseer. Taseer was assassinated by his own security guard last week in Islamabad, Pakistan's capital. But in Pakistan, even Taseer's supporters have been mostly silent: perhaps calling his loss a tragedy, but stopping short of criticizing the man who killed him.
