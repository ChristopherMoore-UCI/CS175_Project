Previously it was believed that the job dispute -- which, among other strikes of Chinese Honda plant has impacted the global supply pipeline of its cars -- was resolved when the company offered a 200 yuan (U.S. $30) per month pay increase. The wages debate at Honda Lock is a microcosm of labor disputes that are sweeping companies across developed regions of China. Honda Lock workers interviewed by CNN said they want their monthly base salary increased to 1600 yuan ($234). As workers seek better wages and working conditions, concerns are being raised that the turbulence could disrupt China's development model of offering multinational companies access to a massive, low-cost labor force.
