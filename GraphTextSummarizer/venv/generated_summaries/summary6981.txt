Ashe was the first black man to win the U.S. Open in 1968 and Wimbledon in 1975, and become known in later years for promoting racial tolerance and AIDS awareness. The largest collection ever of Arthur Ashe memorabilia will be auctioned off next week on the 20th anniversary of the tennis legend's death, with everything from his trophies to his wisdom teeth up for grabs. Among the more than 300 items up for auction: -- With a starting bid of $2,500, Ashe's 1993 day planner that contains "his own handwritten record of his very last days." With a starting bid of $1,000, the U.S. Open doubles trophy silver plate awarded to Ashe in 1968. Ashe was forced to disclose he had AIDS in 1992 after being told that USA Today intended to print a story about his illness as soon as it could confirm it.
