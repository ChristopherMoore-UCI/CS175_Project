For climate change "next year" is now. Clean technology investments surged to $310 billion last year. In baseball, there's a traditional comeback after a tough season: "Wait 'til next year!" In the United States, carbon emissions fell by 10% from 2007 to 2013, the largest absolute emissions reduction recorded, even as the United States has recovered from the Great Recession. This common-sense and achievable plan to decarbonize the U.S. economy will result in significant cost savings from cleaner technologies and create more American energy jobs to power our homes and businesses.
