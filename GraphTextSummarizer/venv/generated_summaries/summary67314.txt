In recent years Apple has come under sometimes withering criticism for its supply chain in China, both for shipping U.S. jobs overseas and for the working conditions at plants run by the companies Apple contracts with there. In both interviews, Cook emphasized American jobs Apple has already helped create -- from the companies that make components of its popular iPhone to the developers who have built businesses creating apps for Apple mobile devices. Apple will begin manufacturing a computer in the United States next year, says CEO Tim Cook, marking a shift in perhaps the most-criticized element of the company's beloved gadget empire. Cook told NBC that Apple isn't concerned about the higher cost of manufacturing in the United States creating pressure to raise prices. He visited China in March to tour a factory owned by Foxconn, which makes electronics for Apple and other companies.
