The ruling elite, fearful of this, will naturally resist opening up political systems and resort to economic and security measures in a desperate attempt to stem the tide of change. What has taken place in the Arab world is the start of a genuine and permanent process of change where the average citizen suddenly discovered real power. Ruling establishments in the Arab world have always made sure that political parties and civil society organizations are either suppressed or outlawed. Although the ability to effect change peacefully is a new phenomenon in the Arab world, it is a wave that will only grow. Ruling elites shouldn't conclude that the status quo can be maintained because public support for protests seems to be wavering.
