In the 80-year history of official White House press secretaries, only two women have been named to the job: Dee Dee Myers, who served former President Bill Clinton, and Perino. Dana Perino said goodbye to the White House press corps at her last briefing as White House spokeswoman Friday. -- White House spokeswoman Dana Perino on Friday said she would do the job again -- but only if she could work for her current boss. It's been 15 months since Bush named Perino to succeed Tony Snow, who later died of colon cancer, as White House press secretary. "I wouldn't do it for anybody but President Bush," Perino said as she briefed reporters for the last time Friday.
