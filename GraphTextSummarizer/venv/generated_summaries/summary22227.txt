Prime Minister Hun Sen announced last week that Cambodia is committed to stopping new HIV infections by 2020. Cambodia Prime Minister Hun Sen has called for an inquiry into a suspected mass HIV infection in a remote village in northwestern Battambang province. More than 800 panicked residents of Rokar village have sought testing after reports of infections emerged last week. New HIV infections have dropped by 67% from 3500 in 2005 to 1300 in 2013, according to UNAIDS. UNAIDS estimates there are 76,000 people living with HIV in Cambodia.
