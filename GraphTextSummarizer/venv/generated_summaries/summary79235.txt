Videla, 84, has previously been found guilty of other human rights abuses, including kidnapping, torture and homicide. There are 31 defendants in the two trials, the Judicial Information Center said on its website Tuesday. Also scheduled to go on trial this week in a separate proceeding will be Luciano Benjamin Menendez, former head of the Third Army Corps.
