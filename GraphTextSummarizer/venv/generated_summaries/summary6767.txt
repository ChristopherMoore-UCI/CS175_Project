Twenty-one percent of the 33 homicides of Georgia prisoners since 2010 took place at Smith State Prison, located in Glennville, Georgia, according to the Southern Center for Human Rights. The report highlights three Georgia prisons as particularly dangerous: Baldwin State Prison, Hays State Prison and Smith State Prison. Thirty-three prisoners and one officer have been killed by other prisoners in Georgia since 2010, according to a report released Wednesday by the Southern Center for Human Rights in Atlanta. Last year, some 21,000 men and women were released from Georgia prisons, according to the report. -- On September 1, 2012, an adult inmate was attacked at Baldwin State Prison in Hardwick, Georgia.
