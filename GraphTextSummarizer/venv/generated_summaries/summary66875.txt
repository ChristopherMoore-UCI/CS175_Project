Noir Kennedy Stuffed rats hang from traps alongside taxidermied ravens; 1980s patent leather pumps and reconditioned baseball jackets spill from coffins; leather jackets hang from the ceiling, and the changing rooms are fashioned from classic red British telephone booths. "I sell items with emotional instead of commercial value," explains the owner, Charles Mas, voicing an ethos that still holds sway among many Parisian shop holders. Parisians still love their little neighborhood vendors selling one thing and selling it very well -- shops of a kind apparently threatened with extinction in other big cosmopolitan cities. "It makes a nice change from the clothing and shoe shops around here," says one enthusiast.
