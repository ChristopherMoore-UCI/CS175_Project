Lee Myung-Bak made the pledge during an unscheduled meeting with reporters Sunday in the press room of his presidential office, the state news agency reported. "I promised to spend my whole salary earned as a public official on public welfare," Lee told reporters. As mayor of Seoul from 2002 to 2004, Lee donated his salary to the children of street cleaners and firefighters. During the election campaign, Lee, 66, vowed to donate his entire personal fortune of more than 30 billion won ($30.2 million) to the poor. Lee is a former CEO of an engineering and construction company with a vast personal fortune.
