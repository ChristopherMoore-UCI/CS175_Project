In countries with state-run health services, such as Britain and Canada, long waiting times for surgery are encouraging patients to look overseas for a cheaper alternative to private treatment in their own country. Those countries are home to private health care chains that target international patients and are building modern, high-tech hospitals with a reputation for quality care. -- Medical tourism is one of the hottest topics in health care as patients around the world are increasingly traveling abroad for treatment. South Korea recently limited foreign patients to no more than five percent of its hospital beds, to ensure domestic patients do not lose out.
