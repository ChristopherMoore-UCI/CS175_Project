Protesters from 48 local communities had gathered Thursday at a stretch of highway known as Cuatro Caminos to express their opposition to high energy prices, among other issues. In addition to high energy costs, the protesters also oppose an educational reform that will increase the duration of studies needed for a teaching degree from three years to five years. -- At least seven people were killed in southwest Guatemala when security forces clashed with protesters blocking a highway More than 30 others were hospitalized with injuries, 15 of them in serious condition, Damaris Juarez, a spokeswoman at Jose Felipe Flores Hospital, said Thursday. The leader of the protests, Carmen Tacan, blamed security forces for the deaths and demanded an investigation, according to a government news release.
