Read more: Mandela used humor to charm and cajole Read more: A look inside Mandela's memorial garden Read more: Nelson Mandela laid to rest in Qunu The prisoner turned president's funeral in his childhood village of Qunu marked the end of 10 days mourning, during which tribal elders guided Mandela's transition to the afterlife. Mandela's family says his spirit is now in Qunu. Friends and family mourned alongside heads of state, royalty and celebrities who had made their way along South Africa's back roads to Mandela's burial place. Inside that tent -- filled with the sweet scent of white roses and lilies -- the Mandela family shared their grief with some 4,000 guests and television cameras broadcasting the service to the world.
