The currency is in free fall, forcing traders to peg their prices to international currency to hedge against losses. Even vegetable vendors prefer the U.S. dollar, South African rand or Botswanan pula, and most workers now demand their salaries in foreign currency. As a result, the end-of-year examinations taken in November are yet to be graded after the markers demanded their wages in foreign currency. "In a move meant to ensure that the public has access to their money from banks, the Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe has introduced a new family of bank notes which will gradually come into circulation, starting with the 10 trillion Zimbabwe dollar," the bank said in its announcement. The new 100 trillion dollar bill would be worth about $300 in U.S. currency.
