Rolling Stone sales: a mixed picture Some critics call the Rolling Stone cover a desperate effort to boost sales. "It is absolutely impossible to predict what newsstands sales are going to be," Rolling Stone Managing Editor Will Dana told NPR after the anger erupted. McCain: Rolling Stone cover was stupid Recent ad page figures -- a metric often used to determine a magazine's health -- show a mixed picture. The "more buzzy covers" have lifted sales by up to 70% above yearly average, an official with Bloomberg Businessweek told Ad Age. Ad Age puts it this way: "Outrage aside, controversial magazine covers often pay."
