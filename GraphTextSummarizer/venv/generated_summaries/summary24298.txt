Hardly ever a somber, low-key affair, Ghanaian funerals are a social event attended by a large number of mourners, which could reach hundreds -- the more, the better. Mourners, usually dressed in black or black and red traditional funeral clothing, may travel to other towns or villages, and in turn they expect the bereaved families to provide food, drinks, music and dance. -- Funerals in Ghana are not only an occasion to mourn. The Boafos, like many grieving Ghanaian families, hold a church service for Joanna as well. "We are investing in the dead rather than the living ... and that is bad," legislator Alban Bagbin once said.
