Year after year, polling shows that Americans are willing to spend 10 times what the government now allocates to foreign aid. GOP presidential hopeful Rick Perry proposed setting foreign aid accounts to zero if elected. Financially savvy Americans know to protect their assets by spending small amounts on insurance and other precautionary measures. -- Foreign aid, a topic often overlooked on the presidential campaign trail, seems to sport a giant bull's-eye among Republicans this election season. Just as Americans' systematically examine their own family's spending decisions, I suggest that GOP presidential candidates apply similar personal finance principles to evaluate why foreign aid is worth the investment.
