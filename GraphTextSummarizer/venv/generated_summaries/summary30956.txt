Modern society is confronting a tragic, silent epidemic of frail elderly people who suffer needlessly at the end of life because of unwanted life support. The fundamental question is this: When is it time to accept the inevitability of death, change the goal of care to comfort, and let the patient have peace? The epidemic of futile support at the end-of-life persists. Death used to be simple. Now, in the modern era of high-tech medicine, death is difficult, too.
