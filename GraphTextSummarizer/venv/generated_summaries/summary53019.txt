On our journey to the city of Jhang in southern Punjab, the road passes along the River Chenab, which literally translates as Moon River. The city of Jhang lies closest to Trimmu Barrage, through which the Chenab flows. But according to Pakistan's National Disaster Management Agency (NDMA), on the evening of September 12, close to 800,000 cusecs of water will pass through the barrage, causing flooding in the surrounding districts of Multan, Muzafargarh, Khanewal, Jhang and Toba Relief camps On the outskirts of Jhang, a relief camp has been set up by the government. Helicopter perspective CNN was given access to a military rescue helicopter that flew over the flooded areas close to Jhang and near the head of Trimmu Barrage.
