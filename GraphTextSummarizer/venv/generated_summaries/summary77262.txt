Many medical doctors are just finishing advanced training in their mid-30s. The colorful and trustworthy former surgeon general of the United States, Dr. C. Everett Koop? Nothing is more reassuring to a sick and troubled patient than the calm and steady voice of a physician offering sound advice and proper medical treatment. Republicans say he's too young, and his prime qualification for the nation's most prestigious medical post appears to be that he was once co-chairman of "Doctors for Obama." Searching for a prestigious hospital affiliation and joining a good medical practice are their primary concerns, not the formation of national health policy.
