There was no time to develop the sometimes inspiring, sometimes disheartening, but always memorable scenes of human life that the Titanic disaster continues to present. Other disasters were either too big or too small to develop this kind of interest. The Titanic had what every great drama needs: a relentless focus on the supreme choices of individual lives.
