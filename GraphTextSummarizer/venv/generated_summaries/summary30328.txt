The modest side street outside her home in eastern Mexico City was swarming with police, health workers and civil protection officials. It's rush hour deep below the streets of downtown Mexico City. Since midweek, the government abandoned its initial reporting and began to speak only of confirmed swine flu cases and confirmed deaths due to the H1N1 virus. In these days of swine flu paranoia, just coughing, or worse sneezing, in the subway, or "metro" as they call it here, brings black looks from fellow passengers. Once healthy people are suddenly falling sick and dying from a new disease, H1N1 swine flu.
