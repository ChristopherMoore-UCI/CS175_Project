People of color are no more likely to use or sell illegal drugs than white people -- yet from 1980 to 2007, blacks were arrested for drug law violations at rates 2.8 to 5.5 times higher than white arrest rates. Have U.S. drug laws reduced drug use? When it comes to drugs, we should focus on the goals we agree on:  protecting our kids, protecting public safety and preventing and treating drug abuse and addiction. Today, the American people are showing their dissatisfaction with the war on drugs by voting for change, often in the face of federal law. It also represents racial discrimination and targeting disguised as drug policy.
