Slackers usually settle for its more accessible clones (Salinas River State Beach, Moss Landing State Beach and Marina State Beach also front Monterey Bay). Just mile after mile of beautiful beaches, fishermen and a few palapas. Despite the lack of crowds on this string of beaches punctuating Oregon's rugged coastline, skinny dipping is probably out. Should you go, it'll likely just be you, the seals, the whales and Mother Nature showing off her finest work on more than a dozen stretches of spectacular beach. Locals nicknamed it "Secret Beach" and not surprisingly, there are no lifeguards or restrooms or beach umbrellas.
