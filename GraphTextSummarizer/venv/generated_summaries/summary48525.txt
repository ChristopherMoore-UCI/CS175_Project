They tend to be a lot smarter about their social environment than adults, and they are far more likely to adhere to community standards if they are involved in creating and monitoring those standards. When adults are bystanders, they inadvertently reinforce the bad behaviors of bullies and the passivity of students. In addition, educators can play their part by breaking down the high wall that often separates the cultures of adults and students. Each year, 20% of high school students report being bullied -- physically, emotionally, or socially. When adults are able to gain the trust and respect of the students, they have more credibility in resolving conflicts.
