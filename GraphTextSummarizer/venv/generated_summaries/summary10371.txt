Little kids such as Ahmed completely stop growing and become stunted in time. Ahmed would have his blood drawn to check for anemia and possible bacterial infections. As soon as he entered Dadaab refugee camp with his father after 10 days of walking under the East African sun, it was clear his tiny prone body may have been robbed of nutrition for too long. The pulse, the blood pressure and body temperature all precipitously drop. Not too long after the food is cut off, the body resorts to fuel reserves in the liver and fatty tissues.
