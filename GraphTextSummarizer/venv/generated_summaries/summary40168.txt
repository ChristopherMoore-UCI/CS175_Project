Frequently asked questions about the court and the case Arguments usually begin at 10 a.m. and since most cases involve appellate review of decisions by other courts, there are no juries or witnesses, just lawyers from both sides addressing the bench. Don't get your hopes up Each week, the court receives more than 150 petitions for review, decisions by lower courts appealed to the high court. Court arguments are open to the public in the main courtroom, and visitors have the option of watching all the arguments or only a small portion. Justices divide their time between "sittings," where they hear cases and issue decisions, and "recesses," where they meet in private to write their decisions and consider other business before the court.
