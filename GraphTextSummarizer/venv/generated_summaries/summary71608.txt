"Dick Williams and Karl Behr were wonderful, dignified men, who would never have exhibited the characteristics that are displayed. Remarkable recovery Williams made a remarkable recovery and less than three months later he faced the more experienced Behr in a tennis tournament on the lawns of Longwood Cricket Club in Boston. Williams raced into a two-set lead, but the wily Behr prevailed in five sets. Williams was on the rise and won the 1914 U.S. National Championship (now known as the U.S. Open), beating Behr in the quarterfinals.
