Behind closed doors, the scientists and agents of the FBI scrutinize fibers, poisons, explosives, DNA and just about any other shred of evidence that might help solve crimes. As the FBI hits the 100-year-mark and continues to evolve to meet the demands of the world, CNN visited the state-of-the-art crime lab in Quantico, Virginia. A lab worker examines a tire tread at the FBI's famed crime lab in Quantico, Virginia. When a CNN crew visited in June, it was not allowed into areas where scientists were working with real evidence. Karen Lanning, chief of the evidence control unit, said evidence has to be dealt with correctly from the second it arrives at the FBI.
