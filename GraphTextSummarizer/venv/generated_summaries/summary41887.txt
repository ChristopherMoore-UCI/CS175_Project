Another tip: Keep a close eye on your card's interest rates and find out if there is room for negotiation Credit card companies are increasing fees and cutting credit limits, and some are increasing rates, according to Arnold, so be sure to scrutinize your monthly statements. Try shifting high-interest credit card debt onto cards that have lower interest rates. Your credit report is being even more closely scrutinized today by credit card issuers, mortgage lenders, auto dealers, insurance carriers and even potential employers. Consumers have racked up more than $2.2 trillion in purchases and cash advances on major credit cards in just the last year. Many credit card issuers already have policies in place.
