Despite the reports, a Freedom House survey last year showed that Mugabe's party was regaining its popularity. -- After more than three decades, Zimbabwean President Robert Mugabe is not ready to give up power. Different paths Mugabe and South Africa's anti-apartheid icon, Nelson Mandela, both battled to free their nations from white minority rule. War hero Once hailed as a liberation hero, Mugabe became a household name during a guerrilla war against white colonial rulers in Zimbabwe, then known as Rhodesia. In recent years, political rivals have accused him of turning a nation once known as the breadbasket of southern Africa into one racked by hunger and once sky-high inflation.
