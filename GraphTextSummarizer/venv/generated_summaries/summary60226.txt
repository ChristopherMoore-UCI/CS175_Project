The United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization estimates that 98 percent of Kenya's public primary schools and 80 percent of public high schools lack computers. For the past eight years, the Kenya native has been refurbishing computers, printers and other electronic educational resources otherwise headed for landfills, then sending them to grateful students back home. Every two years, Ndambuki visits recipient schools to show teachers and students the basics of computer programming and maintenance. Ndambuki ships a 40-foot container loaded with hundreds of refurbished computers to Kenya for distribution once a year.
