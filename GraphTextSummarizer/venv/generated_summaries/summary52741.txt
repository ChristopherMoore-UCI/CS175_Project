Google continues to add features to its social network in an apparent effort to spur growth and further differentiate itself from Facebook and Twitter. Writing on the PBS blog Mediashift, Dan Reimold adds: "The stream of updates has basically run dry -- reduced to one buddy who regularly writes. Once you're 'On Air,' up to nine others can join your hangout (as usual), and anyone can watch your live broadcast," Google's blog post says. On Tuesday, the company announced a few more, including: Better search for topics and personalities on the site: "Google+ search results include items that only you can see, so family updates are just as easy to find as international news," the company says. For the past three months, Google+, the company's social network, was available only to users who had been invited by the company or by friends who already had access to the site.
