James totaled 1,074 points including 85 first-place votes, from ballots cast by a panel of sportswriters and broadcasters, as well as fan votes. Despite joining an elite group of NBA players who have been named MVP three times, James said his real goal is to win a championship. James, 27, beat out Oklahoma City's Kevin Durant (889 points) and the Los Angeles Clippers' Chris Paul (385 points). James led the NBA in plus-minus score differential and ranked third in scoring. The Los Angeles Lakers' Kobe Bryant (352 points) and San Antonio's Tony Parker (331 points), rounded out the top five vote-getters.
