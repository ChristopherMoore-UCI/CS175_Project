Policy makers must factor in such behaviors when planning for accidents at nuclear power facilities. Experts continue to debate the size of safe zones in nuclear accidents. For some, nuclear power's promise of a stable, carbon dioxide-free power source is worth any risks from accidents and the problem of nuclear waste storage. The Nuclear Regulatory Commission has agreed to evaluate the possibility of establishing a 50-mile emergency evacuation zone around nuclear power plants in the United States.
