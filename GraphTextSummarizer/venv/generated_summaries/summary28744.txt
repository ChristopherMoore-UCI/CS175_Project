Monahan said she believes mobile users up to age 45 will make the transition to mobile payments relatively seamlessly. And as more apps and smartphones push mobile payments, consumers will become increasingly willing to ditch plastic for their phones. People have possessed the ability to spend and send money with their smartphones for years, but mobile payments have yet to take off in a big way. But four recent events suggest that mobile payments are about to take off in a big way. What's not clear is which form of mobile payments will become dominant.
