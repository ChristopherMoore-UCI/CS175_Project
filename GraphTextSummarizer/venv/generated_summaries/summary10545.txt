It's time to tell them that tax and entitlement reform is essential -- rational proposals that will reduce health care costs and sustain investments in research and development. Young scientists are rethinking their career paths or moving abroad to countries that have accelerated investments in research. It's certainly possible if elected officials get beyond the rhetoric and take decisive action to strengthen our nation's investments in research. Americans understand the importance of a long-term investment in medical and health research to spur private-sector innovation and produce the therapies, medical devices and treatments that will save lives. In fact, more than half of Americans are willing to spend more in taxes if they were certain that all of the money would be spent on additional medical research, according to polling (PDF) commissioned by Research!America, a nonprofit advocacy alliance.
