Deterring and denying asylum seekers in Australia Border crackdown Soon after his election as prime minister in September 2013, Tony Abbott declared an unofficial war on people smugglers. The operation may have been successful in reducing the number of boats arriving in Australia, but the government's policy on asylum seekers has attracted fierce criticism. One of the most controversial centers is on Manus Island, Papua New Guinea, where hundreds of male asylum seekers are being held in four security compounds. He announced "Operation Sovereign Borders," a military-led crackdown meant to deter potential asylum seekers from making the risky voyage to Australian waters. Violence on Manus Island After taking office, the Abbott government said it would continue a Labor plan to process all asylum seekers at offshore detention centers.
