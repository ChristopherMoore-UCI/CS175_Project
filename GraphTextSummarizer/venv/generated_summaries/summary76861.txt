More: Cyprus rejects bank levy, bailout in doubt Anastasiades and the leaders of Cyprus' political parties will now meet on Wednesday to try to hash out an alternative plan, but they'll have to come up with a workable solution quickly. Under the original Eurogroup proposal on March 16, all holders of bank accounts in Cyprus -- both residents and non-residents -- would have been required to pay a one-off tax on their existing deposits. In 2001, the IMF released a report on bank debit taxes that had been imposed in Latin America -- however, these taxes were on financial transactions not an outright grab as is being proposed in Cyprus. Talks have dragged since then, as Cyprus resisted many of the tough economic measures demanded by potential institutional lenders in return for bailout funds.
