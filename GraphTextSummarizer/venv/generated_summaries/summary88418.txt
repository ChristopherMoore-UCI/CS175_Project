After weeks of racial protests across the country, President Barack Obama is taking the time to deliver encouraging words about the future of race relations in America to a network that reaches a predominately young African-American audience. Obama announced last week that he and outgoing Attorney General Eric Holder are "not going to let up" in the effort to ease racial tensions that exists between law enforcement officers and minorities in communities nationwide. In his interview, the President says African-American youth need to be both persistent and patient in order to make progress on the issue of racial tensions in America. "What I told the young people who I met with -- we're going to have more conversations over the coming months -- is, 'This isn't something that is going to be solved overnight,' Obama says in an interview with BET that will air Monday. The President also urged persistence in the effort to solve racial tensions, reminding the audience that typically progress occurs in "steps" and "increments.
