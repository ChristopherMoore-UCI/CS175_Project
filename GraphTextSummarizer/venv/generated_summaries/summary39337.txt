The U.S. military has identified frozen remains found atop a California glacier as those of a World War II era airman who vanished more than half a century ago. Peter Stekel and a friend found the remains of a World War II airman atop a glacier in California. Then, in October 2005, backpackers discovered frozen human remains of a crew member, later identified as Leo M. Mustonen. Last year, two hikers found the frozen remains of a man with blond, wavy hair in a remote area of Kings Canyon, east of Fresno, California. Ernest G. Munn had been missing since his training flight disappeared over the Sierra Nevada mountain range on November 18, 1942, the U.S. military said Monday.
