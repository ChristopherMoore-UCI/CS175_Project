Laura Stachel The problem: During a two-week trip to Nigeria in 2008, Stachel witnessed countless times when the lives of mothers and babies were at risk simply because of a lack of reliable electricity. How Stachel took action: With the help of Hal Aronson, her husband and a solar energy educator, Stachel developed a special solar energy kit that can easily provide much-needed light and power for medical equipment. Georges Bwelle, Richard Nares and Laura Stachel, three of this year's top 10 CNN Heroes, are medical marvels dedicated to improving other people's health. In many countries, including the United States, people are suffering because they can't get the health care they need. Many people are treated for malaria, tuberculosis, malnutrition, diabetes, parasites and sexually transmitted diseases.
