As we've seen in the past, divided government isn't a reason to do nothing; it's often been a spur to achieve big things. The President himself has noted that the American people are counting on him to work with the Congress they elected to get things accomplished. Our job is to focus on serious ideas designed to boost the middle class and help restore our nation's promise for future generations. Reagan worked with Democratic leaders to pass bold reforms to taxes and Social Security. A Republican Congress worked with Clinton to pass groundbreaking welfare reform.
