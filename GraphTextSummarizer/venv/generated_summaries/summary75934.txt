Yoo Byung Eun, the subject of an intense, nationwide manhunt who went missing after the South Korean ferry sank in April, was declared dead, police said Tuesday. But the search ended Tuesday when police announced Yoo's death. Even South Korea's president, Park Geun-hye weighed in, accusing Yoo's family of "mocking the law and causing public rage. Yoo, through his representatives, vehemently denied any connection to the Chonghaejin Marine Company in late April. Yoo's wife, Kwon Yun-Ja is under arrest in South Korea, according to Yonhap.
