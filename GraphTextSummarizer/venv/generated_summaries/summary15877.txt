As its name suggests, "DOMS is muscle soreness that becomes evident six-to-eight hours following activity, peaking around 24 to 48 hours post-training," says Jon Mike, an exercise scientist at the University of New Mexico. Enter delayed onset muscle soreness, better known as DOMS. Yes, DOMS appears to be caused by trauma to your muscle fibers, but it's not a definitive measure of muscle damage.
