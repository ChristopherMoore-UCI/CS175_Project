The band opened the tour September 30 at Minneapolis' Target Center and played New York's Madison Square Garden on October 6 and 7. The long-running band, which famously aired its internal problems on such albums as "Rumours" and "Tusk" and broke up for several years in the '80s and '90s, announced Thursday that its new "On With the Show" tour has been extended well into 2015 with 28 new dates. The tour includes stops in Boston, Los Angeles, Atlanta and Toronto, as well as a return engagement at Madison Square Garden. The new tour includes keyboardist and singer Christine McVie, who had been away from the group for 16 years. Christine McVie rejoining Fleetwood Mac The band made the announcement on NBC's "Today" show, where they stopped to play and engage in some upbeat banter.
