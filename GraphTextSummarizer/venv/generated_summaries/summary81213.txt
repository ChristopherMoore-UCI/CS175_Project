It represented China's fifth manned mission to space in a decade, and the first during the presidency of Xi Jinping, who took office in March this year. China only sent its first astronaut into space in 2003, but has made rapid advances in the intervening decade. The Shenzhou-10 mission -- hailed by Zhang Youxia, commander-in-chief of China's manned space program, as a "complete success" -- is part of Beijing's ambitious multi-billion dollar program to establish a manned space station by 2020. The Shenzhou-10's mission was the second and final manned voyage to the space station, following China's first manned docking mission a year ago.
