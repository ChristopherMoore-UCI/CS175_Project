It can continue to provide humanitarian aid, and if a stable government does emerge, it can aid in the process of recovery and development that will take years. An al-Assad victory would leave a seething majority of Sunnis under a dictator who could only survive by using repression at the cost of recovery, development, human rights and freedom. Like many of the countries in the Middle East -- Bahrain, Egypt, Iraq, Lebanon, Tunisia and Yemen -- it has already been destabilized by a mixture of population pressures, weak economic development, authoritarianism, corruption, failed governance, and deep ethnic and sectarian divisions. It will take years for them to achieve stability and move along some path toward growth and development. It can push for the full destruction of the chemical arsenal and an end to the far more lethal use of conventional force against civilians.
