-- Actress Elaine Stritch, known for her brash persona, gravelly voice and versatility over seven decades on Broadway, died Thursday, longtime friend Julie Keyes told CNN. "Elaine Stritch's big personality was matched by her big talent," Broadway League Executive Director Charlotte St. Martin said. The marquees of Broadway theaters will be dimmed for one minute in Stritch's memory at 7:45 p.m. Friday, the Broadway League said. Just 17 when she moved to New York to study acting, Stritch made her Broadway debut in 1946. "Collaborating with some of Broadway's greatest playwrights and composers throughout her lengthy career, her signature numbers and singular style created a memorable legacy.
