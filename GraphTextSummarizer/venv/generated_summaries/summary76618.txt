The new law is a reminder that same-sex marriage is the civil rights issue of our era and just how far the movement for marriage equality has come. During the 1960 campaign, Kennedy ran as a forward-looking Democrat who understood the necessity of civil rights. But once in office, Kennedy made civil rights a low priority. In retrospect, Kennedy's reputation as a supporter of civil rights was greatly exaggerated. Kennedy believed that promoting civil rights would jeopardize other aspects of his domestic agenda during a national crisis.
