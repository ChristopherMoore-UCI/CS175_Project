FIFA president Sepp Blatter has confirmed that world football's governing body will donate $250,000 to the families of those people killed in last week's football riots in Egypt. A bank account has been set up by Al Ahly to help support families who lost loved ones, with Blatter appealing to the world of football to show their support for the north African nation. More than 70 people lost their lives after violence erupted at the end of a match between Al Masry and Al Ahly in Port Said.
