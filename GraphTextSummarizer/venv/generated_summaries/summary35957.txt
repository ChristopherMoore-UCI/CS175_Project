"What I did was collect things, collect and collect like any fan, of football, stamps, action figures, whatever -- the little bit that I could get because in Buenos Aires there wasn't a lot of material," Vazquez said. Now, for an entrance fee of about $2.50, other aficionados can catch a glimpse of the collection, which Vazquez says is the first Beatles museum in Latin America. By 2001, Vazquez secured a Guinness World Record for the "largest collection of Beatles memorabilia." Remembering John Lennon: The interviews Vazquez said he bought some of the material with his own money, but acquired most of it through exchanges with fans around the world. Museum creator Rodolfo Vazquez said he started his private collection after someone gave him the group's "Rubber Soul" record when he was 10 years old.
