And as more and more of our television is provided through the internet, other internet giants such as Verizon and AT&T will have to look toward merging with CBS/Viacom or ABC/Disney. Network executives swore in congressional hearings that they wouldn't give their own programming preferred access to the airwaves. We'll end up with a few megacorporations in control of the flow of information -- not just on TV, but now online as well. Net neutrality may sound like a technical issue, but it's the key to preserving the internet as we know it -- and it's the most important First Amendment issue of our time. Back in the 1990s, Congress rescinded rules that prevented television networks from owning their own programming.
