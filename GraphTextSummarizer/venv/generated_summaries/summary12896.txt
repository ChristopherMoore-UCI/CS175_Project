"We are all going to hell or Cuba," Potts said he told the pilot of the Miami-bound flight. -- William Potts, the "homesick hijacker" who returned from a life on the lam in Cuba to the United States in November, may soon plead guilty to commandeering a passenger plane to the island at gunpoint in 1984, according to U.S. federal court documents. After smuggling a pistol aboard Piedmont Airlines flight in a fake cast, Potts threatened to kill everyone aboard the plane if the pilot didn't divert the aircraft to Cuba. A militant past Nearly 30 years ago, Potts was a black militant hoping to overthrow the United States government by violent means. After Potts' arrival and arrest in Miami, Potts' family said they feared he had made an error by handing himself over.
