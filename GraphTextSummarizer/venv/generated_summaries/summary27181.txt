Type 2 diabetes is usually a byproduct of obesity; extra fat on the body makes it hard for your system to produce enough insulin to keep blood sugar levels at a normal level. Now, marathons are his love. The doctor told him that he had to lose weight and that he would have to prick himself every day to check his blood sugar. He walked everywhere, which allowed him to shed a noticeable amount of weight. What's more, he realized he liked running, not only because it helped him lose weight but because it was a stress reliever.
