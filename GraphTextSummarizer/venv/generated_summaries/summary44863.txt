About half of the sexual assault cases with victims younger than 11 involved family members. "For any survivor of sexual trauma, it's challenging, and it takes a lot of courage to come forward," said Jennifer Wilson, director of the National Sexual Assault hot line. A U.S. Bureau of Justice Statistics report found that of the 60,000 sexual assault cases reported in 12 states in 2000, about a quarter were perpetrated by family members. And if he or she tells a relative, that family member may have "a knee-jerk reaction," refusing to believe it. At times, a victim may feel unable to tell other family members what's happening.
