Reda's style draws from techniques of jazz, ballet, Hindu dance and Russian folkloric dance. -- When it comes to theatrical dance in the Arab world, all roads lead to Mahmoud Reda. During a glittering career, the 79-year-old Egyptian dancer and choreographer has performed on the world's most prestigious stages, including New York's Carnegie Hall and Congress Hall in Berlin. His universal appeal, likened to U.S. dance legends Gene Kelly and Fred Astaire, has allowed Reda to export his particular mix of theatrical and traditional dance around the globe. It has had influenced and shaped the dance form known as "Raks Sharki" (Oriental dance), more commonly known as "belly dancing" outside the Middle East.
