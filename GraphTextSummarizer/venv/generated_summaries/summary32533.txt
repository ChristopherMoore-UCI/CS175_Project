Afghan cricket has gone from strength to strength. The first Afghans learned the game in Pakistani refugee camps, having fled the Soviet invasion of 1979. In 2008, the team played its first matches in division five of the ICC's global league -- against Japan and the tiny island of Jersey, a self-governing British Crown dependency off the coast of northern France. The scenes of jubilation on the pitch as Afghanistan recently brushed aside a disappointing Kenya team couldn't have been farther from the reality of life for many Afghans after decades of conflict and turmoil in their country.
