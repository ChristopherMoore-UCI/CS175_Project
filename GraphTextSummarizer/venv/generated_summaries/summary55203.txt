In the 1970s, however, credit rating agencies started charging the issuers of new investments fees for ratings. Credit ratings agencies had a starring role in the financial crisis, after the firms blessed mortgage-backed securities with AAA ratings -- the safest rating possible -- and fed the global mania for these risky investments. Investors across the world look to credit rating agencies to judge where to place their bets in the market. There are moves afoot in Europe to have more stringent regulation on credit ratings agencies. So what do ratings agencies do, and why are they so important?
