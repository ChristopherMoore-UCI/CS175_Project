Forecasters had warned of an additional 1 to 3 inches of snow accumulation in the South on Sunday night into Monday morning. -- Early-morning commuters in much of the Deep South, braced for the possibility of a second round of snow and ice, had an easier-than-expected ride Monday as warmer temperatures kept winter weather at bay. Most winter weather advisories and warnings were canceled early Monday except for portions of northern Alabama and Georgia at higher elevations. Forecasters initially had expected the rain to turn into snow after 1 a.m. Monday. During that period, 8.8 inches of snow fell on Harkers Island, North Carolina, according to weather service data.
