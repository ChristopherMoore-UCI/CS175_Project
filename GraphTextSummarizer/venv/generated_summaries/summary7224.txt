I saw them every day for a month as they grappled with confusion and mixed messages from Malaysia Airlines and the Malaysian government. Families complain of harassment by local communist party officials, several have been detained by Chinese police for holding meetings and some say they have been barred from speaking to the international media. "I think psychologically it is understandable, they choose to believe that their loved ones are still missing, so they can still have hope for them to return," says May Lam, a psychiatrist at The University of Hong Kong. I first met family members of those who were on the airliner that night as they stood in shock at the arrivals hall of Beijing airport. He boarded the Beijing-bound Malaysian Airlines Flight 370 flight in Kuala Lumpur on March 8 last year when it disappeared barely an hour into its journey.
