Shooting survivor says blood donations saved her life Sickle cell disease is a genetic blood disorder affecting the red blood cells. To help reduce the number and severity of these episodes, I take daily medications and, with the help of the American Red Cross, I receive red blood cell transfusions every six weeks. My name is Marquita Gaines, and I have sickle cell disease. At first glance, no one would know that I am a patient living with sickle cell disease.
