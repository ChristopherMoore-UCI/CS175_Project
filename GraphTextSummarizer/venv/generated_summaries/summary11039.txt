Amnesty International said in a briefing paper issued Tuesday that Al-Atrash is one of many people who have vanished in what they say is a "campaign of enforced disappearances" -- a practice by pro-Moammar Gadhafi forces trying to squelch the opposition movement. One relative told Amnesty that "he has simply disappeared," and speculates he was taken to areas under the control of pro-Gadhafi forces. The cases include "three broad categories," the report says: -- Government critics, pro-democracy activists, writers and others detained in the days before the February 17 "peaceful demonstrations" across Libya. Among those missing are political activists and "those suspected of being rebel fighters or supporters of fighters. It shows prisoners roughed up and taunted by Gadhafi forces.
