Read more: Mutti Merkel is no Iron Lady Thatcher Youth unemployment in Spain and Greece is above 50%, where strict austerity programs are in place, while rates in Portugal, Italy and Ireland are all above 30%. In four years, Greece, Portugal, Cyprus and Ireland have received over 400 billion euros ($534 billion) in bailout packages from the euro-area's rescue funds. In return for state aid, creditors have imposed strict rules on debtor nations, forcing them to carry out harsh austerity measures. Since the crisis began, European governments have had to contend with spiraling borrowing costs and state bailouts as nations struggled to repay their debts. Speaking at the Fund's headquarters in Washington DC, managing director Christine Lagarde told CNN's Richard Quest that member states cannot afford "fatigue" on their commitments to create jobs and to shore up the region's banks.
