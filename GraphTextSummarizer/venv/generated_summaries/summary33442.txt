China is known for chasing superlatives -- now it can add marathon train journeys to its growing list of unparalleled achievements. The train set off from Yiwu in eastern China on November 18, chugging its way through Kazakhstan, Russia, Belarus, Poland, Germany and France, before ending its journey in Spain's capital, Madrid. " Regular train services between China and Germany already exist, connecting Chongqing with Duisburg, and Beijing with Hamburg. The trip was a test run to gauge the possibility of establishing regular service between China and Spain to boost bilateral trade. Madrid Mayor Ana Botella Serrano dubbed it the "Silk Road of the 21st century.
