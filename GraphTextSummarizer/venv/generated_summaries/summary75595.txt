Boko Haram has terrorized northern Nigeria regularly since 2009, attacking police, schools, churches and civilians, as well as bombing government buildings. Nigeria's elections have been postponed from next Saturday to March 28, the country's election commission announced Saturday. Boko Haram attacks Nigerian city in second takeover attempt CNN's Lillian Leposo and Christian Purefoy reported from Nigeria and Steve Almasy wrote in Atlanta. The announcement comes the same day Benin, Cameroon, Niger, Nigeria and Chad pledged to deploy 8,700 troops, police and civilians, as part of a regional effort to fight Boko Haram. "In consideration of the advice by the security agencies, it will be unconscionable to have elections without adequate security," said elections chairman Attahiru Jega, according to a tweet from the Independent National Electoral Commission.
