Either John McCain or Obama will be the first incumbent member of Congress to be elected president since John F. Kennedy in 1960. • Four presidents have won the presidency while losing the popular vote: John Quincy Adams (1824), Rutherford B. Hayes (1876), Benjamin Harrison (1888) and George W. Bush (2000). Bill Clinton was the last lawyer elected president, and the first was John Adams. In the House and Senate • Democrats controlled both houses of Congress from 1955 to 1981. The House has twice decided the presidential election because no candidate received a majority in the Electoral College (Thomas Jefferson in 1800 and John Quincy Adams in 1824).
