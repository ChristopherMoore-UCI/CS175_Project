The 34-year-old producer and DJ has collaborated with some of the biggest names in music, producing Amy Winehouse's 2006 "Back to Black" album, reworking songs from seminal British bands The Smiths and Radiohead and working with Kanye West and Duran Duran among other musical luminaries. Moving our from behind the decks Ronson took to producing other artists and has picked up awards for his work in the studio including a Grammy for Best Producer in 2008. Coming from a wealthy family in Manhattan, Ronson grew up with music and celebrity at the kitchen table before making his name as a DJ on the New York club scene in the early 1990s. "I think we live in an era certainly where it's not unusual to be many things, especially in music," he told CNN. That's one of the things that helps and more importantly I grew up liking such a wide range of music that I love working in different genres.
