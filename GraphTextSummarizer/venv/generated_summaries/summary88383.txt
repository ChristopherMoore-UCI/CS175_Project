The bill aims to strengthen consumer protection, rein in complex financial products and head off more bank bailouts. It would also establish new procedures for shutting down giant financial firms that are collapsing. The legislation will vastly reform the way big financial firms do business. The bill aims to shine a brighter light on some complex financial products, called derivatives, that are blamed for exacerbating the collapse of financial companies such as American International Group and Lehman Brothers.
