Proposition 1 funding, which enacted the Water Quality, Supply, and Infrastructure Improvement Act of 2014, will funnel $272 million into safe drinking water efforts and maintenance of water recycling infrastructure. As part of the changes, Brown said additional measures will crack down on water inefficiency as California enters the fourth year of a worsening-water crisis. And making sure we meet emergency needs, prepare for short term problems and advance longer-term projects are an important part of that effort. The March snowpack measurement came in at 0.9 inches of water content in the snow, just 5% of the March 3 historical average for the measurement site. "The drought isn't letting up, so we can't let up either," Assembly Speaker Toni Atkins said.
