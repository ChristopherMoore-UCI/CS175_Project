For his 1995 visit to the Philippines, local car maker Francisco Motors produced a Popemobile based on a 4x4. The early days of motorized papal transport were far removed from the Mercedes vehicles he currently drives. Until 1978, his primary mode of transport for meeting his public was the sedia gestatoria, a glorified sedan chair carried on the shoulders of various papal attendants. When John Paul died in 2005, this Popemobile was put on display in a Philippine church and became a pilgrimage destination for the country's Catholics who couldn't afford to go to the Vatican for the burial ceremony. -- One of the first questions many people ask when they start a new job is: what type of car will I get?
