In Barboursville, West Virginia, Brett Walker said, "the scene at Chick-fil-A seemed like a massive silent protest." Walker said he chose to eat at the restaurant Wednesday "in support of free speech" and waited 40 minutes to receive his order. The company, which said it was not involved in creating the event, says it set a sales record on Wednesday, The chain won't release sales numbers, but "we can confirm reports that it was a record-setting day," said Steve Robinson, Chick-fil-A's executive vice president of marketing.
