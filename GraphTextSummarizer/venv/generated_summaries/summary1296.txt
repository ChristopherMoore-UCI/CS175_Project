U.S. conducts airstrikes on ISIS militants in Iraq ISIS overtakes Iraq's largest Christian city On Friday, with extremist Sunni fighters less than an hour's drive from Irbil, the Kurds got their wish: U.S. fighter jets pounded ISIS artillery positions outside Irbil. Peshmerga soldiers -- badly outgunned by ISIS fighters equipped with American weapons and armored vehicles stolen from Iraq's army -- withdrew from their towns and villages amid the onslaught, and families had just minutes to pack and leave their homes. And as they pass the hours in the scorching summer heat, no escape for 40,000 desperate people hiding in the mountains of northwestern Iraq from the killers surrounding them on the ground below. Now that ISIS militants have captured Kurdish towns just 35 miles away, no one is sure how long things will stay that way.
