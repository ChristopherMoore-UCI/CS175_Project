The Pakistani military is fighting Taliban militants in the country's north, and missile attacks from suspected U.S. drones have targeted militant leaders. File picture taken on November 26, 2008 of Pakistani Taliban commander Hakimullah Mehsud Leader Hakimullah Mehsud met reporters Sunday in South Waziristan, according to the journalists, who spoke on condition of anonymity because they are not authorized to talk to the news media. Mehsud also vowed to avenge the recent death of Taliban leader Baitullah Mehsud, they said. The new leader of the Pakistani Taliban vowed revenge on Pakistan, the United States and NATO forces for drone attacks along the Afghan border, two local journalists who attended a meeting with him told CNN. U.S. officials do not comment on suspected drone strikes, which have caused tension between Pakistan and the United States.
