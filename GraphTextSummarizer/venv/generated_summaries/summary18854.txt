Rather than his qualifications, reports often featured derogatory comments -- some veiled, many not -- fat jokes, weight-related puns and abundant stereotypes, providing a clear example of how socially acceptable weight bias and discrimination against obese persons have become in our society. Recent estimates even show that weight discrimination is comparable to prevalence rates of racial discrimination. As a researcher who studies weight discrimination, I anticipated some of the comments on Christie's body weight that inevitably emerged in the press. Only one state -- Michigan -- has legislation making it unlawful to discriminate against obese persons. Research shows that weight discrimination in the United States has increased by 66% over the past decade.
