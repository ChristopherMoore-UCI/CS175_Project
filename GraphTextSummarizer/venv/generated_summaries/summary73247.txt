In addition to writing, Hosseini dedicates his time to providing shelter for returning refugee families through the Khaled Hosseini Foundation, which funds learning centers for women and helps to protect children involved in the rug-making industry. Hosseini yearns to take his son, Haris, and his daughter, Farah, 10, to Afghanistan. Afghanistan provides the inspiration and backdrop for "And the Mountains Echoed" as well as Hosseini's previous novels, "The Kite Runner" and "A Thousand Splendid Suns." Hosseini and his family came to the United States as asylum seekers.
