Google Reader's "share" feature coordinates with Google Plus these days -- and even e-mail lists can be pulled from Google friend "Circles." And there's evidence, some of it from independent traffic monitors, that Facebook users engage much more frequently and for longer than Google Plus users, at least in public posts (A comScore report in January said Facebook users spent an average of 7.5 hours on the site, compared to 3.3 minutes on Google Plus.") Google Plus, Google's much-chided version of Facebook, celebrated its first birthday last week. As The Atlantic put it: "People are 'on' Google Plus, but they are not really ON Google Plus.
