According to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration's annual survey, seat belt use has steadily increased since 1994, coinciding with a decline in the percentage of unrestrained daytime passenger vehicle fatalities. It points out that 32 states and the District of Columbia have passed primary laws requiring seat belt use. Seat belt use continues to be higher in states that have primary belt laws that allow law enforcement officers to issue citations to motorists, the safety agency said. Some of the most significant improvements occurred in the South, where seat belt use rose from 80% in 2011 to 85% this year.
