Adverse health effects associated with its use include seizures, hallucinations, paranoid behavior, agitation, anxiety, nausea, vomiting, racing heartbeat and elevated blood pressure. According to the DEA, increased use of synthetic marijuana has lead to a surge in emergency-room visits and calls to poison-control centers. The drugs are manufactured overseas and are typically laced on plant material and sold as herbal incense or plant food. The ban that takes effect Tuesday was announced by the DEA in November, with the agency saying then it would make possessing and selling the chemicals used to produce synthetic marijuana  illegal in the United States for at least one year while the DEA and the Department of Health and Human Services study  whether the chemicals should be permanently controlled. The Drug Enforcement Administration is making the sale and possession of synthetic marijuana illegal for at least one year.
