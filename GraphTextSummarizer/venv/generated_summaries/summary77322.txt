David Petraeus, who served as the top U.S. commander in Iraq and Afghanistan, was sworn in Tuesday as the new director of the Central Intelligence Agency. On July 18, Petraeus handed over Afghan command to Gen. John Allen. Petraeus -- sworn in by Vice President Joe Biden at the White House -- succeeds Leon Panetta, who is now the secretary of defense. He went on to become arguably the most influential general of his generation, and Esquire magazine called him one of the most influential people of the 21st century.
