Oknoplast's annual turnover topped $120 million last year, but, with unemployment rising and growth in Europe and Poland slowing down, exports are coming under pressure. Read more: Auto industry revs up recovery on Spain "Last year was the first year when most of our production went to export", Oknoplast President Mikolaj Placek told CNN. At Oknoplast's production site outside Krakow, Poland, windows of all shapes and sizes are stacked up ready for delivery -- and they all have homes to go to. Poland has been a success story amongst the transition economies, proving resilient to the cold winds of recession which have swept across Europe. Now, 45,000 a month are sliding off the production line, most of them destined for export locations across Europe.
