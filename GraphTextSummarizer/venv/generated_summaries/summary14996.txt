Bankers and athletes showed the most drastic drop in prestige: Both are down seven points from last year. U.S. adults, according to a recent survey by Harris Interactive, see firefighters, scientists and teachers as the most prestigious occupations while bankers, actors and real estate agents are the least prestigious occupations. " Consequently, five occupations are perceived to have "hardly any prestige at all" by at least a quarter of adults: stockbrokers (25 percent), union leaders (30 percent), entertainers (31 percent), real estate brokers (34 percent) and actors (38 percent). Among the least prestigious occupations are real estate brokers, actors and bankers. The profession that saw the biggest increase in prestige from 2006 was that of farmers, who rose five points.
