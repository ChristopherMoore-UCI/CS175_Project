Beyond local labor, the most neglected facet of disaster recovery is local data. If you are going to get involved with recovery, you have to do so responsibly, with the safety and well-being of both survivors and volunteers as your priority. While large aid organizations like the Red Cross and Salvation Army can mobilize massive amounts of crucial resources, they often do not manage spontaneous donations, track local volunteer hours, or stay long term. Baffled by the acute lack of community recovery tools, my sister and I decided to build them. Disaster recovery is changing in the United States.
