At first, Emmons donated her locally grown, chemical-free fruits and vegetables to churches and food pantries. Access to fresh food is an issue for many communities throughout the United States. Eventually, Emmons would love to expand her organization across the country, combating food deserts wherever they exist. It showed that more than 72,000 low-income city residents, many of them minorities, lived in "food deserts" -- areas without a supermarket with fresh food nearby. For residents who lacked transportation, buying healthy food was even more difficult.
