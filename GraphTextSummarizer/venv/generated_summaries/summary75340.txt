Viktor Troicki brushed aside Michael Llodra in straight sets in the deciding singles rubber as Serbia won the Davis Cup final for the first time with a 3-2 win over nine-time champions France on Sunday. Serbia's incredible journey to the Davis Cup final The final time came as Llodra served to keep his country in the tie at two sets and 5-3 down. Serbia had been up against it after Llodra and Arnaud Clement beat Nemad Zimonjic and Troicki in Saturday's pivotal doubles to go 2-1 up. Troicki was drafted into the deciding singles in place of Janko Tipsarevic and it proved an inspired choice by Bogan Obradovic. However, world number three Djokovic stepped up to the plate to sweep aside Monfils, who had been in such good form as he beat Tipsarevic in the opening rubber of the five-match final.
