" When Davis was 23, her work took a turn: Reading through her old journals, she was struck by the fact that her grievances—being overweight, missing out on romantic love—had been the same for years. In the first self-portrait she ever snapped, Davis sits on a bamboo mat on the sand, on spring break in Myrtle Beach, her one-piece bathing suit concealed by a green cover-up and black shorts; the friends around her are slender in their bikinis and swim trunks, and the uneasiness on her face is palpable. She's beautiful, with bright blue eyes and the kind of straight blonde hair the rest of us have to fake. There's so much to live for—including the thrill of going on first dates. " Davis's inhibitions are understandable.
