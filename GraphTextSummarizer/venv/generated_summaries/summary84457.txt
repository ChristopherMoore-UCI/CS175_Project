Jewish life in Cuba has its roots in Old Havana, where my great-grandparents settled. Steve Kastenbaum talks with Salomon Leyderman outside the Adath Israel synagogue in Havana, Cuba. My grandmother, the daughter of Russian immigrants, was born in Havana, and my grandfather came to Cuba when he was just 3 years old. More than 15,000 Jews were living in Cuba in the 1940s and '50s. At the front door, Salomon Leyderman introduced himself to me as the oldest Jew in Cuba.
