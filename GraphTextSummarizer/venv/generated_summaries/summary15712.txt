Since 2011, India-based Wildlife SOS has rescued 11 elephants subjected to torture. They also are kept in temples as representations of the Hindu elephant god Ganesh. It is illegal to trade or commercially exploit elephants in India, but the practice is common. Different owners, repeated cruelty It is not known whether elephants can cry, but experts say they're known to be emotional animals. "It was a very emotional moment for us, because we've never seen an elephant cry like that," says Kartick Satyanarayan, co-founder of Wildlife SOS.
