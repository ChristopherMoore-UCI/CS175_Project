Rich Boehne, chief executive officer of Scripps, told employees the newspaper was the victim of a terrible economy, an upheaval in the newspaper industry and multimillion-dollar annual losses. The dramatic decline in advertising dollars in a brutal economy has forced newspapers to cut costs by firing cartoonists, columnists and others, leaving them searching for jobs in a struggling industry. "What a huge loss for Colorado today. The Rocky Mountain News in Denver, Colorado, published its last edition Friday after 150 years. Our time chronicling the life of Denver and Colorado, the nation and the world, is over."
