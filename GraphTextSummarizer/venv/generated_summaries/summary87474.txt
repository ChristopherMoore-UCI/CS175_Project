At the helm of this transformation is Krzysztof Pastor, a globally renowned choreographer from Gdansk, northern Poland. "For everyone in Poland, not only for ballet dancers but also in other jobs, you know one has to be innovative, creative," Pastor explained. The country's national ballet has been up and running since April 2009 (when it was given artistic autonomy within the existing structures of the Teatr Wielki "Grand Theater" in Warsaw), pushing out exciting new works and stage shows to wide acclaim. For Poland's National Ballet, the present no longer means reveling in the fleet-footed legends of past stage glories.
