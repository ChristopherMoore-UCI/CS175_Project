When Barack Obama became the nominee of the national Democratic establishment, the candidate of hope ran into political reality: His party's canons of governing are the opposite of change. The Obama campaign's clear message in the primaries was a bottom-up, organic populism that cast voters themselves as the agents of change. Barack Obama may believe "change doesn't come from the top down, it comes from the bottom up," but the leadership of his party doesn't. The hope for real change in Washington has been suffocated by an older generation's embrace. How did the soaring campaign of change become grinding politics-as-usual and crash so thunderously to earth?
