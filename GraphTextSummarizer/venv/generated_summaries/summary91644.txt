In its World Economic Outlook in October 1999, the IMF observed: "Continuing economic and political uncertainties have led to further capital flight and a sharp decline in foreign investment." Russia may not need foreign approval, but it does need foreign money. The IMF noted that "Investment had already been weak, reflecting in part policy uncertainty." In 1999 Russia was already coming out of a decline when Putin took the reins.
