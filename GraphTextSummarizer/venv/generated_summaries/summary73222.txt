Researchers looked at the Facebook profiles and likes, along with surveys and personality tests, for 58,466 individuals. "Facebook likes have a meaning that we can use to understand the psychology behind what people do," says David Stillwell, a co-author of the study. A similar MIT experiment in 2009 predicted whether people were gay based on who they had friended on Facebook. Companies like Google and Facebook decide what ads to show people by using scraps of information they gather as users go about their regular browsing and clicking routines. "Kurt Donald Cobain" and "Vampires Everywhere" indicated neurotic personalities, "Wes Anderson" and "serial killer" were liked by spontaneous people, and competitive types liked "Sun Tzu" and "I hate everyone.
