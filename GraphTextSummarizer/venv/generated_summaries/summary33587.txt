-- Two men have been arrested in the death of a popular television and radio disc jockey who was shot to death on Staten Island early Sunday morning, according to New York City police. William Williams, 21, and Richard Cromwell, 20, both of Staten Island, were arrested Wednesday and charged with murder, robbery, and criminal possession of a weapon. Corey McGriff, known to fans as DJ "Megatron," died of a gunshot wound to the torso near his home shortly after 2 a.m. March 27. Williams and Cromwell are expected to be arraigned Thursday according to Peter Spencer, spokesman for the Staten Island District Attorney. "The BET Networks Family is saddened by the news of the untimely loss of our friend and colleague, Corey 'Megatron' McGriff," the statement said.
