Worst coal mine disaster in U.S. history: 362 miners are killed in an explosion at the Monongah Nos. 6 and 8 Coal Mines in Monongah, West Virginia. * January 10, 1940 -- 91 miners are killed by an explosion at Pond Creek No. 1 in Bartley, West Virginia. * November 20, 1968 -- 78 miners are killed by an explosion at Consol No. 9 miner in Farmington, West Virginia. Five miners are killed in an explosion at the Darby Mine No. 1 in Harlan County, Kentucky. An explosion at Orient No. 2 mine in West Frankfort, Illinois kills 119 miners.
