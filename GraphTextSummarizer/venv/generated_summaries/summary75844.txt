-- A South Korean navy ship sank in the Yellow Sea near North Korea late Friday, and the navy shot at an unidentified ship toward the north, according to reports quoting South Korean government officials. As a result of the incident, South Korean government officials held an emergency meeting of ministers handling security-related matters, officials told Yonhap. However, South Korean government officials said it isn't certain whether North Korea was involved in the incident. The news agency also quoted South Korean military officials saying North Korea conducted dozens of artillery firing drills Friday. Yonhap quoted South Korean naval officials as saying a South Korean vessel fired at a ship toward the north later.
