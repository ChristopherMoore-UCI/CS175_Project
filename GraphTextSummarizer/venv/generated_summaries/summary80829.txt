She lent her stature to the anti-apartheid movement, to the response to the AIDS crisis and to the need for better educational opportunities for young black men and women. As with many famous people, the celebrity she achieved came with public attention beyond imagination -- and some of it wasn't always very kind. I met Whitney Houston after the debut of her first self-titled album on Arista Records. I vividly recall sitting next to a star who once had fame approximating Whitney's but whose revenues were clearly on the downswing, speaking loudly to the rest of her table about how awful and lucky Whitney was and how she didn't deserve her fame.
