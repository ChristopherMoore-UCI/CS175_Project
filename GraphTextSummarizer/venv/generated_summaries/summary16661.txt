Jones and Kohler have also set up a non-profit organization focused on developmental work in rural communities in South Africa, seeking to explore the boundaries of new puppet theater forms. Having started their career with children's plays, Jones and Kohler moved later onto works for adult audiences. Despite all the success garnered from War Horse, Jones and Kohler are determined not to rest on their laurels. With their magnificent puppets, Basil Jones and Adrian Kohler have been pushing theatergoers' experiences to new heights, casting a spell on audiences across the world. Jones and Kohler decided not to join the army and fled to Botswana's capital, Gaborone, where many of their compatriots had also found shelter.
