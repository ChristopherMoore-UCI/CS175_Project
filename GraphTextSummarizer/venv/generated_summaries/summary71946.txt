Alkadamani visited Jordan this month, helping to hand out sweaters to refugees who were crossing the border. They began collecting winter clothing and blankets for refugees in Akkar and the Bekaa Valley in north Lebanon. Life is harsh there, a far cry from the comforts of home refugees left in Syria. She couldn't stand the thought of Syrian refugees shivering in brutally cold temperatures while she and her friends were sleeping in their warm beds. She says Syrian refugees are just normal people -- from doctors to pharmacists to teachers -- with normal lives before the war tore their world apart.
