He grew up in Brooklyn and Queens and spent years shooting around Coney Island. Brighton Beach has it all -- wild prints on shirts and dresses, fluorescent bathing suits that don't exactly fit, garish makeup that's been lovingly applied the same way for 50 years. After years of shooting gangsters, revelers and whoever happens to be out and about, it all unfolds so quickly that almost nobody makes a fuss, he says. For decades, Gilden shot in black and white. He's 68, and he had just returned to New York from an assignment in South Africa.
