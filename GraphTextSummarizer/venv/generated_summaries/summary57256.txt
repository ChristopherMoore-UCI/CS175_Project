As more business is done by email and more sensitive personal data is passed by phone, the demand for secure communication is growing. Silent Circle, itself based in National Harbor, in Maryland, and in Silicon Valley, has located its servers in Canada because it feels the country has superior privacy laws. But growing government surveillance has raised privacy concerns. Zimmermann has always felt strongly about the right to private communication in the digital age and feels that it should be simple -- you dial a number and no one else can listen; you shouldn't have to be a rocket scientist to make your conversations secure. Eric King, head of research for privacy campaign group Privacy International, in London, said he expected the product to be genuinely secure and easy to use.
