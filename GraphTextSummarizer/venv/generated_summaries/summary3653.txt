Teacher Shi hopes that tourism may reinvigorate the Dong's unique culture and encourage younger people like Shi to stay closer to home and engage in traditional pursuits. The others are dotted around the country, with most, like the Dong, clustered in the poorer mountainous regions of southwest China. In their 20s and 30s, one is an accountant in Wuhan, a large city in central China. In villages already on the tourist trail, minority culture is a commodity to sell to passing crowds. Ostensibly, there is little overt friction with the Chinese majority although Shi says when he was younger, he was afraid if Han people came to the village.
