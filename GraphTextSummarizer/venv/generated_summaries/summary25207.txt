In the tiny town of Milltown in southwest Ireland, bartender Trish Morierty says smokers at pubs will still smoke. March 29 marked the 10-year anniversary of a workplace smoking ban in Ireland, extending to pubs and restaurants. In 2005, one year after the ban began, the Research Institute for a Tobacco Free Society examined secondhand smoke exposure in 42 Dublin bars and among 73 bar workers, finding an 83% reduction in air pollution in bars, an 80% decrease in airborne carcinogens, and an improvement in the respiratory health of bar workers. Now, dozens of countries from Brazil to Bhutan have joined Ireland in creating a nationwide workplace smoking ban -- the United States not among them.
