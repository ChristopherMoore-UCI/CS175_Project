The government predicts that by 2018, the number of train passengers in Jakarta will increase to over a million. But railway officials say they have found the perfect deterrent, concrete balls, suspended on chains from a steel frame, just 25 cm or 10 inches, above any passing train. Indonesia has long fought a losing battle against commuters who stubbornly ride on train roofs to beat the rush hour. Mateta Rizahulhaq, a spokesman for the state-owned railway company PT Kereta Api, told CNN no one has dared an attempt it. Everyone here agrees that what this growing metropolis needs is an improved and expanded railway system that gives passengers no excuse to get on train rooftops.
