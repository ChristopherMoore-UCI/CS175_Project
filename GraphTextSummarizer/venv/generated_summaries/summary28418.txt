We cannot deter frauds when we do not prosecute them, fire those responsible or recover the wealth they gained through fraud. JPMorgan should pay the damages it caused to others through fraud. A settlement of this kind would release JPMorgan and its officers from civil and criminal liability for a wide range of alleged frauds. Dimon knew that acquirers of failed banks can refuse to buy unless the FDIC agrees to bear the banks' liabilities for fraud. Second, Dimon knew that if the FDIC offered broad indemnification, rival banks could outbid JPMorgan and buy the banks.
