Turning our faces towards the bust of Ataturk -- the founder of modern Turkey -- and keeping our arms straight like soldiers, we would chant in perfect unison: "I am a Turk, I am correct, I am diligent ..." Turkish society is stubbornly patriarchal and homophobic. And beneath the weight of sameness, Turkish society is more polarized than ever. Women with headscarves, women in miniskirts. One thing that makes me happy as a writer is to witness the diversity of my readers in Turkey: conservatives, liberals, leftists, secularists, Kemalists, Kurds, Alevis, Jews, Armenians ...
