-- The United Nations is urging Pakistan to look deeper into reports that thousands of people inside the country have been illegally detained or disappeared. The U.N. commission that receives reports of missing has more than 500 cases in its docket for all of Pakistan. In a few cases, the U.N. group reports, lawyers defending the families were reportedly victims of enforced disappearances. In many cases, the group found, families were often intimidated or threatened against reporting cases to authorities and were given empty promises that if they didn't, their loves ones would be returned to them. A U.N. delegation spent 10 days inside Pakistan at the invitation of the government, and interviewed families of the missing.
