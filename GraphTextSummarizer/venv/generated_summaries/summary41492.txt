A recent survey of Saudi Arabia's business confidence by financial services company, SABB, found that while confidence remains robust, over half of respondents were concerned that inflation would lead to rising business costs. If you want to grow at that base, you have to swallow a price every once in a while in the form of high inflation," said Abdulrahman Al Harithi, CEO of investment bank, MENA Financial Group. Oil money is paying for Saudi Arabia's growth but it is also the main cause of rising prices Prices in the kingdom will continue their upward trend, according to the analysts at SABB. In March, the cost of fuel and water increased almost 16 percent and other everyday staples also saw double digit gains.
