Over the past several weeks, press accounts have outlined a series of Obama administration moves dealing with the cyberdefense of the United States. But they also suggest the failure of a deeper national policy process and, more importantly, the failure to develop national consensus on some very difficult issues. Chinese military leading cyber attacks Let me reason by analogy, and in this case the analogy is the program of targeted killings supported and indeed expanded by the Obama administration. And we also believe that we can live with the second and third order effects of targeted killings. Rather than settle the roles of these institutions by dealing with the tough issues of security and privacy domestically, we have opted for a policy not unlike targeted killing.
