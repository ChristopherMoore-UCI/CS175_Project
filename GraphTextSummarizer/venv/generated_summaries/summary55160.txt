While his wife cheered for McLain during his playing days, now it's the other way around. McLain made a life-changing decision to have bariatric surgery last June. " "He's doing remarkable," said McLain's physician, Dr. Carl Pesta. Bariatric surgery can be done a few different ways -- either by reducing the size of the stomach with a gastric band, by removing a portion of the stomach (often called a sleeve gastrectomy) or by resecting and re-routing the small intestines to a small stomach pouch, known as gastric bypass surgery. Sharon McLain was diagnosed with Parkinson's Disease a year ago.
