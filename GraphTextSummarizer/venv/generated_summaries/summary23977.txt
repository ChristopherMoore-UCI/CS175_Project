On a visit to Japan on Tuesday, Vice President Joe Biden assured his hosts that the United States will express concerns to China over Beijing's recent claim of a large swath of airspace in the region. The Chinese action, Biden said, raises the possibility of "accidents and miscalculation." Biden promises to press China on airspace dispute He praised the alliance between the United States and Japan, sayings the U.S. commitment to Japan is critical. In addition to the China airspace claim, Biden and Abe discussed ways to fortify U.S.-Japan relations economically and militarily. Asia trip gives Biden chance to show off diplomatic cred "I will be raising these concerns with great specificity directly when I meet with the Chinese leadership" on a later leg of his trip, Biden said. "We, the United States, are deeply concerned by the attempt to unilaterally change the status quo in the East China Sea," Biden said at a news conference alongside Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe.
