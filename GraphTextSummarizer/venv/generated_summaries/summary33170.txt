The official news site Granma reported Tuesday that the Cuban government will no longer require a travel permit and a letter of invitation. A resident in the country that the Cuban wanted to visit would also have to write a letter of invitation. While the report does not say how the law will be altered, it does add that the government will fight brain -- and money -- drain "from the aggressive and subversive plans of the US government and its allies."
