Kavya Shivashankar of Olathe, Kansas, reacts to winning the Scripps National Spelling Bee on Thursday night. The first National Spelling Bee was in 1925 and featured nine contestants. According to the contest's Web site, 117 of the spellers speak languages other than English, and English was not the first language of 33 of the spellers. In an event that has seen contestants visibly crack under the strain of the national spotlight in past years, Shivashankar -- competing in her fourth national finals -- appeared composed throughout.
