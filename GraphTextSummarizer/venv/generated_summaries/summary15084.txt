When you find ways to do less while increasing your impact, you'll gain more time to spend on things you actually enjoy. Split your to-do lists into a Master list and a High Impact Task (HIT) list. See also: Don't let e-mail run your work life Just say no First, say "no" to more work. While the Master lists tracks everything that needs to get done at some point, the HIT list includes only a reasonable number of items that can be accomplished each day, so you're constantly focused on key priorities and work on them in the proper order: P1: You will get fired if this isn't done today. Spending hours in the office to make sure all the assigned tasks get done bleeds into our family time, and even at home, it seems there's a never-ending cycle of things that must be done.
