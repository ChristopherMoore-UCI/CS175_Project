But there is some overlap between micro-units and tiny homes and the people who live in them. Small homes aren't for everybody, but those who embrace the lifestyle tend to become their most enthusiastic boosters. The small home movement has been around for years in small cities and rural areas, but this summer, it made a big incursion into one of the country's largest and most densely populated urban centers. Most of the iReporters who shared their stories of living small with CNN.com also build tiny homes or run businesses dedicated to helping others build their own. Many of them live in homes built on trailers so they can move around; others, like the Berzins, live on property they own.
