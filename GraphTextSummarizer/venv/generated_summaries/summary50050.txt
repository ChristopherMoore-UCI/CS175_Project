It could easily be viewed as an open invitation for other extremist groups to set up training camps, including the likes of Nigeria's Boko Haram. Uncounted thousands of men, women and children were hiding in the forest eating leaves and sucking swamp water. Since that November visit, the Central African Republic has spiraled into deeper chaos and violence -- an unimaginable violence in which children are directly targeted, raped, tortured, mutilated and killed. In 2007 and 2008, I came away from my visits to this remote nation thinking that the women and children of the Central African Republic are surely the most abandoned people on Earth. UNICEF is working with local partners to provide lifesaving support to hundreds of thousands of children.
