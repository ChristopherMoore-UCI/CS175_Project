The tribulations of New Democracy and PASOK were matched by triumphs of a number of other parties that were also-rans and in some cases nonexistent just a few years ago, propelled by voters angry about stringent austerity measures. Sunday's election results were widely seen as a message to politicians to back away from the harsh economic austerity measures imposed in Greece. The center-right New Democracy party finished first in Sunday's voting, but with just 19% of the vote, giving it 108 seats in Greece's 300-seat parliament.
