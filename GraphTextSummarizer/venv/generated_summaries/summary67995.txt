Everything that has been bad about games in recent years was slowly, carefully addressed and ultimately dealt with in London. London has conclusively shown that by being more realistic in ambition, everything can be done without the heartache and angst of previous games. When it comes to taking stock on London 2012, I believe the London Games will be regarded as having brought the Olympics concept home. With its green fields and satanic mills, to the self-deprecating humor from the Monarch herself, London sent the not-so-subtle message: This wasn't going to be "Olympics as usual. In 1908 London rescued the Olympics after Mount Vesuvius volcano erupted, causing the selected city of Rome to pull out.
