NYPD Deputy Commissioner Paul Browne confirmed the department's continued, if limited, use of the 20th-century writing devices, explaining that they're mainly used for filling out property vouchers -- forms that officers must file when they seize items during case investigations. Typewriters create significant efficiency and storage problems for the department, he added, causing extra labor and unwieldy paper trails. Typewriter company Swintec received a $982,269 contract from the city in 2007.
