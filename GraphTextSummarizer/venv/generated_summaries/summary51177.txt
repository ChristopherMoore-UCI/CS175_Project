How did storms end up with female names ? Here is an explainer on how storm names are chosen: . -- In another time, Tropical Storm Katia, which was barreling across the Atlantic early Wednesday, would have been called Katrina. The United States started off by naming storms using a latitude/longitude designation of their origin. The names were hard to remember and convey to the public, so meteorologists started naming storms after their wives and girlfriends during World War II.
