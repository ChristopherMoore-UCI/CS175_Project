While the media satellite trucks are likely to leave shortly after Isaac passes, plenty of hardship stories will be left to tell once Gulf Coast evacuees are allowed to return to their homes. But despite the painful experiences the Gulf region has endured in the aftermath of recent storms, the path to recovery will still be a rocky one. Isaac will provide a reminder that managing messy real world challenges does not lend itself to simple formulas favored by political partisans. The average Gulf Coast family fleeing the storm will have to dole out and estimated $250 to $300 a day for food and lodging. But when it comes to planning evacuations, maintaining levees, and supporting the speedy recovery of communities leveled by a storm -- government matters.
