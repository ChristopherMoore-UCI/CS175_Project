Senate insiders look outside as they run for re-election Outside groups spent $457 million on Senate and House races in 2012, according to the report, compared to $9 million in 1986. On average, House incumbents outspent their rivals $1.7 million to $587,000, a ratio of almost a 3-1. House incumbents who lost their re-election bids in 2012 on average spent more than their challengers -- $3.1 million to $2.5 million. Challengers narrowed the spending gap with incumbents in competitive House races but not by much.
