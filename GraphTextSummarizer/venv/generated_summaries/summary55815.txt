Tax in the U.S. is per bottle rather than then by alcohol level, and Fuller, Smith & Turner has successfully tapped the market by selling a wide variety of drinks with different alcohol levels. But this time the crisis is twofold: The economic crisis facing Europe and the impact of beer duty at home. Traditional beers such as London Pride and London Black Cab have also benefited from what's been dubbed "the London-effect," that came with hosting the Olympics, celebrating the Jubilee and the royal wedding. It's not just a question of turning your attention to international markets in the hope of making up the bleak numbers at home. Despite the deepening crisis in Western Europe, Michael Turner, chairman of Fuller, Smith & Turner, told me the latter crisis was his bigger concern.
