Seventy percent of central Manila was flooded on Tuesday morning, the official Philippines News Agency reported, citing Johny Yu, officer in charge of the Manila Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Council. The national weather agency, Pagasa, issued a red alert for the Manila metropolitan area and nearby provinces on Tuesday morning, warning that "severe flooding is expected." Severe flooding in the region a year ago, also caused by monsoon rains, killed scores of people. Those measures continued Tuesday as photos and videos showed flood waters reaching waist or chest deep on some roads in the Manila region. Flooding clogs streets of Manila
