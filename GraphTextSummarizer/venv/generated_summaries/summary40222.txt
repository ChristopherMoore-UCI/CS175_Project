Single mom fought alongside combat troops in Afghanistan Several U.S. allies, including NATO members France, Canada and Germany, also allow women to serve in combat posts. Chief of the General Staff, General Sir Nicholas Carter, said UK military operations in Afghanistan had shown that there could be "significant physiological risk to women" serving in ground close combat roles. But it calls for more research into the physiological demands placed on those in close combat roles before a final decision is made on allowing women in. "I look forward to the prospect of opening ground close combat roles to women, but we have to look at this in a responsible way," said Chief of the Defence Staff, General Sir Nicholas Houghton.
