Roper said she strongly believes police officers want to do the right job when the department's expectations are made clear to them, but an "ingrained police practice" will not be a quick fix. In fact, it might mean for the department to retrain officers on not just when it's appropriate to stop pedestrians but also how to appropriately conduct a stop, which the ACLU may decide to request, she said. Since reviewing the stop material, Roper found that 45% of pedestrian stops do not have a legally adequate reason. This is not the first time Philadelphia police practices have been under scrutiny.
