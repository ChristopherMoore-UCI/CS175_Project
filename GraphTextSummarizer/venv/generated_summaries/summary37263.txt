After years spent living beneath a posh condominium in east Beijing, she has since moved "above ground. "I think for some people there is true upward mobility but for many people the hukou system, whereby migrants can't actually buy homes and settle down, is still a huge barrier to them building lives and families here. Together they are members of what the Chinese press unkindly refer to as the "rat tribe" -- people who live underground in a warren of basements and air raid shelters in Beijing. Some use dehumidifiers in summer to take away the damp and in Beijing's freezing winter months it's warmer than above ground homes. The number of rental ads for underground housing increased during her year researching the phenomena in 2013.
