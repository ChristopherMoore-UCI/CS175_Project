"You go over this invisible line, and there are people with farmers' markets, people with organic food ... healthy sandwich shops," she said. The neighborhood where Keatley lived, East Harlem, has the highest diabetes rate in Manhattan, according to city health officials. Keatley said that when she would visit more affluent neighborhoods near East Harlem, she wouldn't see nearly as many amputees. "It's so shocking to me to see people who are poor and unhealthy and literally dying in the street," said Keatley, an award-winning chef and nutritionist. Once a week, Keatley and her army of volunteers, driving in a vegetable-oil-powered truck, hand out 500 to 1,000 pounds of fresh produce to people on high-trafficked corners.
