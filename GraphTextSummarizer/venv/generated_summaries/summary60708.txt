On June 1, an Air France Airbus A330 crashed off Brazil while en route from Rio de Janeiro to Paris, France. The Airbus A310 was inspected in France in 2007 by the French civil aviation authority and "a certain number of faults had been noted. The airline operating an Airbus A310-300 jet that crashed in the Indian Ocean on Tuesday with 153 people aboard was being monitored by EU authorities, according to France's transport minister. Yemenia Air had used the jet since 1999, on about 17,300 flights, Airbus officials said. Dominique Bussereau told French television that inspectors in his country had also noted several faults on the doomed Yemenia Airways plane, Agence France-Presse reported.
