I wanted to look at how a family is a kind of four-dimensional system, a system not of physical forces but emotional forces, like memory and regret, and how, over time, a family creates its own story of itself. Yu's genre-defying story of time travel is receiving critical praise for being a quirky mix of string theory, "A Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" and Philip Roth. I wanted to ask some of the age-old questions about time and identity and self, but I wanted to articulate those questions in new ways. How do you find time to keep up with everything?
