Opinion: Olympics street art should be embraced Street art is a culture in Brooklyn. Street art is considered rebellious in nature and illegal in practice, while public art is commissioned by cities or property owners and is considered culturally enriching and socially acceptable. In the realm of public space, street and public art are often indistinguishable. Like Dimopoulos, Ferrari sees public space as a canvas and public art as an avenue to bring his aerosol-inspired artwork to a larger audience, further blurring the lines between street and public art. It's the nature of street art: spontaneous, public, fleeting.
