-- After months of speculation and hype, the first biopic about Apple co-founder Steve Jobs hit theaters Friday. The movie's final frames play Jobs' (OK, Kutcher's) "Think Different" narration over romanticized images of Apple engineers. Jobs' dark side The movie has several scenes of Jobs delivering dynamic speeches while reverent Apple employees respond with wild applause. But "Jobs" focuses on his early years at Apple and ignores almost everything else. A second Steve Jobs movie, written by Aaron Sorkin and adapted from the best-selling Jobs biography by Walter Isaacson, is due as early as next year.
