The comparisons between Mrs. Kennedy and Mrs. Obama are as apt as the comparisons between their husbands. The push-pull of knowing fashion and appropriately wearing fashion may take Mrs. Obama longer to manage. In just 200 days, Michelle Obama has put her own stamp on one of America's most unusual political positions -- the unelected, undefined job of being first lady. Mrs. Obama, because she has made fashion "more democratic," says style expert Robert Verdi, has made her own impact on a world full of elites and -- let's face it -- snobs. Mrs. Obama has avoided making the same mistake during the worst economic times in recent memory.
