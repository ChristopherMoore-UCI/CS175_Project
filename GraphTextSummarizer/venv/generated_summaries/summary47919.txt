"Products marketed as a vitamin but which contain undisclosed steroids pose a real danger to consumers and are illegal," said Howard Sklamberg, director of the FDA's compliance office. Women also reported unusual hair growth and missed menstruation, and men reported impotence and low testosterone. The company has declined to voluntarily recall the product or to warn consumers about the potential for injury, according to the FDA. Regulators received 29 complaints associated with the product, including fatigue, muscle pain and cramps, and liver and thyroid problems, the FDA said. Anyone who used the product and has symptoms should seek medical care and report the case to the FDA, regulators said.
