Congressional earmarks and pork barrel spending have a long history in the United States. But Reid has a point, and many Republicans who spent much of their careers mastering the art of congressional spending agree with him -- even though many are scared to say so in public. " Earmarked spending is a good way to build broad and durable party support for important public policies. The complex patchwork of military defense contracting throughout the Sunbelt, often a result of legislative efforts to get money to valuable districts, was essential for Republicans in building support for a muscular national security policy against the Soviet Union. However, for every "bridge to nowhere" there are congressional earmarks that fund university research and public works programs that play a vital role for Americans.
