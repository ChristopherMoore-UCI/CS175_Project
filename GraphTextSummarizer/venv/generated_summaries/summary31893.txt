His homes have a designer feel -- interior wood paneling, stainless steel kitchens, built-in bookcases -- packed into a space about the size of walk-in closets of upscale homes. In nearby Sebastopol, California, Jay Shafer designs tiny homes and has even started a blog about living on less. The upstairs loft has a bed in 56 square feet of space. The downstairs has a sitting area, tiny kitchen and bathroom in a space that's 98 square feet. His smallest home has 65 square feet; his biggest (a three-bedroom place) has 774 square feet.
