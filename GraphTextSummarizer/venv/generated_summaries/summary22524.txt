-- Chelsea booked a semifinal date with Champions League holders Barcelona with a 2-1 win at Stamford Bridge over 10-man Benfica Wednesday. Chelsea's continued their passage under interim manager Roberto Di Matteo, but their fans were kept on the edge of their seats until the very end of their second leg tie. Chelsea, who won 1-0 win in Lisbon last week, went through 3-1 on aggregate. Real Madrid thrashed spirited Cypriot minnows Apoel Nicosia 5-2 in their quarterfinal second leg in the Santiago Bernebeu stadium to win 8-2 on aggregate. Cristiano Ronaldo put Real ahead midway through the first half as he connected with Marcelo's cross. Former World Player of the Year Kaka capped an electrifying first half performance with the second on 36 minutes with a curling shot.
