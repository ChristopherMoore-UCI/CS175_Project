Former teammates of cycling superstar Lance Armstrong recounted a wide range of techniques used to beat the sport's drug-testing regimen to investigators from the U.S. Anti-Doping Agency, which released its findings on the seven-time Tour de France winner Wednesday. "At the time, the whereabouts programs of drug testing agencies were not very robust." "We hid," the report quotes former U.S. Postal Service team member Tyler Hamilton. During Armstrong's comeback attempt in 2009, his Astana team "benefited from privileged information or timing advantages during doping control tests." The sport's international governing body, the UCI, "did not even have an out of competition testing program.
