Two, Internet companies deliberately make privacy not salient. When it was proposed, it was supposed to give users the right to demand that Internet companies not track them. Internet companies fought hard against the law, and when it was passed, they fought to ensure that it didn't have any benefit to users. More generally, lots of companies are evading the "Do Not Track" rules, meant to give users a say in whether companies track them. For years, these sites and others have systematically improved their "product" by reducing user privacy.
