Rights groups: Court ensures justice for all Rights groups are urging African leaders to support the court, saying it is crucial in ensuring justice for everyone. Accusations of double standards The court has consistently said it is not a substitute for domestic justice systems, and only intervenes if the national judicial system is either unwilling or unable to carry out justice. Kenyan and Sudanese presidents face charges at the court, and African leaders have long accused it of unfair treatment. Rights groups say the court is crucial in ending impunity in African politics. The trial for Kenyan Deputy President William Ruto is under way while his boss, President Uhuru Kenyatta, is scheduled to appear in court next month.
