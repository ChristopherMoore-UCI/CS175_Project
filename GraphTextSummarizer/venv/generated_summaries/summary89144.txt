Rather than recreate the Andalusian fashions donned by the women in de Torres' portraits, Hirschfeld picked fabrics from the Senegalese markets, and had dresses made by tailors on the island. In Hirschfeld's version, his models use cowrie shells, a traditional Senegalese fortune-telling tool. Though the influence is Spanish, the props, the fabrics, the models and the setting are all local to Senegal. "In Senegal, they have an amazing tradition of tailors, and they work really fast.
