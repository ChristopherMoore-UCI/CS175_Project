On Tuesday night, the Muslim Brotherhood announced on its Facebook and Twitter pages that the president has delegated authority to either limit or cancel the curfew to governors of Port Said, Ismailia and Suez. In Port Said, about 4,000 people joined the protest, which began at Mariam mosque and continued for hours, winding its way through the streets. On Sunday, he announced a 30-day nighttime curfew for the provinces of Port Said, Suez and Ismailia. The Transport Ministry said that despite the tumult in the Suez Canal region, the unrest has not affected shipping operations to or from the eastern and western ports of Port Said or the traffic at the El Arish port. CNN's Reza Sayed in Port Said and Amir Ahmed, Salma Abdelaziz and Joe Sterling contributed to this report.
