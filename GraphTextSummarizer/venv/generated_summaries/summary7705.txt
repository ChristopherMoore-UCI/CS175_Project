Police operations were carried out in Madiun, East Java, Solo in Central Java, Bogor in West Java and Palmerah, as well as Kebong Kacang in Jakarta,  Antara reported. The suspects are believed to belong to a newly formed terrorist cell under the control of Hasmi, which stands for Harakat Sunni for Indonesia Community, National Police headquarters spokesman Inspector Gen. Suhardi Alius is quoted as saying, according to the official Antara news agency. Indonesian police arrested 11 people Saturday during a series of raids on a terrorist group that was plotting attacks on targets that included a U.S. Consulate in East Java, state media reported. "We have seen the reports, but cannot comment as this is an ongoing Indonesian security investigation," said a U.S. State Department official who would not speak for attribution. Among its targets was the U.S. Consulate General in Surabaya, East Java, he added.
