Employees commit to an organization because they buy into company goals and feel valued, not because they are ordered to sit at their desks. Given that it's harder than ever nowadays to separate the boundary between work from life, does Yahoo really think that requiring that employees to work in the office is the right solution? From millennials to Gen Xers to boomers, men to women, returning veterans to disabled employees -- having flextime and telework can make a huge difference in people's lives as they juggle work and life. What remote workers should do is set clear performance goals and regular times for meetings and calls with core teams. Giving workers flexibility to integrate personal life with work is viewed as antithetical to boosting performance.
