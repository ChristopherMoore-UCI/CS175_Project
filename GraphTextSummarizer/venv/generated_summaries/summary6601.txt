Some say that because mobile phones are getting relatively expensive, with smartphones retailing for hundreds of dollars, these emerging technologies are more useful or necessary for consumers. Bringrr joins a number of other devices and services that employ more technology to try to help tech-enabled people locate the gadgets they've lost. Some consumer electronics make life more complicated instead of simpler, but these phone reminders can reduce stress, according to Enderle. "These phones aren't cheap, and increasingly, our lives are tied to them," said Rob Enderle, principal analyst at the Enderle Group, a technology consulting firm. Bringrr Charge, set to ship in August, will double as a cell phone charger.
