She will also visit Robben Island, where Mandela was held for the majority of the 27 years he was in detention for fighting apartheid. -- First lady Michelle Obama's official trip to southern Africa next week will focus on youth leadership and education, and will include meetings with key figures in the anti-apartheid struggle. The latter part of the trip includes a meeting with Nobel laureate Archbishop Desmond Tutu, and a visit to Soweto and the site of the 2010 World Cup. Before heading to Johannesburg and Cape Town, she will take a tour of an apartheid museum with Nelson Mandela's wife, Graca Machel. The first lady's first stop will be in Pretoria, where she will meet with South African President Jacob Zuma.
