Read: Underwater hotel room opens on remote African island Each shanty in the "town" goes for 850 South African rand ($82) a night, sleeps four people and has electricity and running water. In a clumsy play for verisimilitude, rooms are equipped with donkey geysers (water heaters) and long drops (outdoor toilets). -- Emoya Luxury Hotel in Bloemfontein, South Africa, has been all over the blogosphere in recent weeks. "Millions of people are living in informal settlements across South Africa," reads the Shanty Town homepage. At Emoya's Shanty Town, guests stay in a re-creation of an "informal settlement" -- shacks made of corrugated iron sheets.
