-- French rescuers in Haiti on Wednesday pulled from rubble a girl who they believe could have been trapped since the January 12 earthquake. iReport: Haiti's missing and found | Full coverage Rescuers found the girl after a group of Haitians approached the French embassy in Port-au-Prince and said they could hear a voice in the rubble. The 16-year-old girl was found in poor condition but was stable and talking, French spokesman Marcel Orcel said. They found the girl dehydrated, weak and with low blood pressure, leading the French crew to believe that she'd been buried since the earthquake struck  Haiti 15 days ago, Futilla said. The rescuers followed them to the site and made contact with the girl, rescuer Claude Futilla said.
