The smaller academies allow school personnel to keep better track of students, which officials say has led to a decrease in school violence. Closing schools is hard on the community, school leaders said, even if it's better in the long run for financial stability and students' educations. But 12 schools throughout the Cleveland school district are restarting with the help of the federal government's School Improvement Grants, which could bring more than $36 million to Cleveland schools over four years. At Lincoln-West last year, there were often six or seven police cars in the parking lot in order to manage school violence. Districts could use the  money to reorganize or close down schools completely.
