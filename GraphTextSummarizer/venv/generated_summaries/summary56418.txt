"Go-Go," 40 years after its founding, remains especially popular in its hometown of Washington, D.C. "The groove and vibe he created was unparalleled," said manager Tom Goldfogle. Brown competed with disco and later had an influence on local hip-hop artists, Goldfogle told CNN. "Chuck would describe it as nonstop percussive bass music with its roots in Latin, African and funk," said Goldfogle. According to CNN affiliate WJLA, Brown had recently postponed numerous shows due to failing health. Brown died of multiple-organ failure from sepsis at Johns Hopkins Hospital in Baltimore, Goldfogle said.
