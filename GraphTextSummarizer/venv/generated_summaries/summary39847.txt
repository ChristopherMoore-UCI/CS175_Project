The film won five awards for best costume design, best production design, best makeup and hair, best original music and best original screenplay. "Boyhood" won big at Sunday's BAFTA ceremony, netting prizes for best film, best director and best supporting actress. The film also won awards for best editing and best sound. Julianne Moore won best leading actress for her role as a professor battling Alzheimer's in "Still Alice. "Birdman," which started the night with 10 nominations, walked away with the award for best cinematography.
