" Japanese director Kiyoshi Kurosawa, who won Best Film and Best Screenwriter for "Tokyo Sonata" at the Asia Film Awards. Meanwhile Japan's Hirokazu Koreeda won Best Director for "Still Walking." "Tokyo Sonata," the emotional story of a contemporary Japanese family beat out the night's top contender, "The Good, the Bad, the Weird," a quirky South Korean Western. After winning the Oscar for Best Foreign Film earlier this year, the Japanese hit "Departures" clinched a Best Actor award for Masahiro Motoki for his role as cellist turned mortuary embalmer. The event draws film stars and entertainment heavyweights from around the world, including American director Oliver Stone, who presented the award for Best Director.
