Gathering pressure on Islamists Haftar has vowed to rid Benghazi and the rest of Libya of Islamist militants with his self-declared Libyan National Army. Hostilities have increased since Gen. Khalifa Haftar bombed the bases of Islamist groups on Friday, including the one blamed for the attack on the U.S. Consulate in Benghazi on September 11, 2012, that killed the U.S. ambassador and three other Americans. Rallying point The interim government immediately condemned Haftar's assault on Friday and his vendetta, but his movement has gained clout with Libyan special forces battling Islamist militias.
