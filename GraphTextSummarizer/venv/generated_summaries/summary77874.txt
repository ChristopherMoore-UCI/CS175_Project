In the 1990s it was largely women who tracked the threat from bin Laden, who pieced together slivers of data to form a surprisingly accurate picture of an international terror network that had yet to announce its existence to the world. A good analyst acknowledges her blind spots and networks with other analysts to fill them. At this moment analysts are following that circle of relationships outward, looking at other friends and family members. The amount of data is so large that no one individual can look at all of it. On career appraisals, one report written for the policymaker often counted for more than months spent sifting through data and working with counterparts on the clandestine side of the house.
