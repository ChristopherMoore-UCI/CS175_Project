He cites his "bigtime" influences as movie brats like Martin Scorsese and Brian De Palma, as well as Sergio Leone and Howard Hawks. " Before moving into directing, Tarantino studied acting for six years and says learning to act for the camera gave him an insight into how directors put a movie together -- frame by frame. To me, action scenes are some of the most cinematic moments that you can do in film," he says. I couldn't help but think something like 'they don't let people like me make movies,'" he says, laughing. "I tend to think of myself as more of a novelist who makes movies than a director per se," he declares.
