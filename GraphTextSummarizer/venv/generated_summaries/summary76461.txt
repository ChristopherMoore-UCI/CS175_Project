CNN iReport: Protests in Shumen, Bulgaria The protests against soaring electricity bills have morphed into wider discontent over austerity and the way the country is being run, the official Bulgarian News Agency reported. -- Bulgarian Prime Minister Boyko Borisov tendered his government's resignation Wednesday after eight days of nationwide protests over high energy bills, his office said. "We will perform our functions until the election of a new Cabinet and we will do everything possible to ensure continuity," Borisov said in the formal resignation letter read to parliament, according to the Bulgarian News Agency. The government has done its best to respond to the protesters' demands, the news agency quoted Borisov as saying. Borisov, who has been prime minister since 2009, earlier told lawmakers his GERB party would not be part of a caretaker government.
