He crossed swamps, climbed mountains, cozied up to scorpions in the Sahara desert, and dined on rat stew and grasshoppers. The local delicacies could vary, from grasshoppers to rats." The kindness of strangers Wood acknowledges that his survival was often dependent on the hospitality of local villagers, of which there was an abundance. In December 2013, photojournalist Levison Wood set out to become the first person to walk the length of the Nile River. Even sharing water, he says, demonstrated an incredible act of kindness -- given it's a 20-mile walk to the nearest source for many villagers.
