As of Sunday morning, 26 people remained at area hospitals, with the rest airlifted to burn trauma clinics in Moscow, St. Petersburg, Chelyabinsk and Nizhni Novgorod, according to the Russian Emergency Situations Ministry. The death toll climbed to 112 on Sunday, rising as people died in hospitals, according to the press office of the Perm division of the Russian Emergency Situation Ministry. Prime Minister Vladimir Putin dispatched planes from Moscow equipped to carry people with extensive burns. More than 170 interrogations have been conducted with injured people and eyewitnesses, said Vladimir Markin, a spokesman for the Investigative Committee of the Russian Prosecutor's Office. A court ruled Sunday that the four will remain under arrest for two months, according to local prosecutor Vladimir Rybailo.
