In addition, we need research to find how much physician reporting would reduce risks or lead pilots to camouflage problems. The FAA could require that pilots seek treatment in order to fly, but some pilots may still simply cover up problems. The case raised several specific quandaries about what the responsibilities of medical professionals are and should be -- legally or ethically -- in evaluating pilots. Careful, transparent discussion, involving medical experts, pilots, policymakers and others, to establish  policies with clearly established cutoffs are thus vital. Pilots may pass drug tests in January and realize that the odds of undergoing subsequent tests that year are nil.
