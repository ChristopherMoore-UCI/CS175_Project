Over the years the song, written by John Madara and Dave White, became a feminist anthem, recorded by such artists as Dusty Springfield and Joan Jett. "Ever Since," a low-key collection of songs, was "very sparse, very organic," Gore told The New York Times. Gore, born Lesley Sue Goldstein, had just turned 17 when "It's My Party," produced by Quincy Jones, topped the charts in June 1963. Gore is survived by her partner of more than 30 years, jewelry designer Lois Sasson.
