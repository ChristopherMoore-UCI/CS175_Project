Palfrey swam more than 67 miles from Little Grand Cayman island to Big Grand Cayman island. A lycra suit will provide her some protection from jellyfish, Palfrey said, and long cables called "shark shields" will be strung from the kayaks and boats around her to ward off larger predators. Palfrey, who is a mother of three and grandmother of two, said she hopes that swimming in the wake of Tropical Storm Debby will provide her with calm seas. "She will swim continuously 103 miles or 166 kilometers under standard open-water swimming rules; that means no wetsuit, no shark cage," her husband, Chris Palfrey, said Thursday when the couple spoke to reporters.
