The lengths workers go to to see their families during the country's biggest holiday, which this year began February 19, is one facet of migrant worker life captured by factory hand turned photographer, Zhan Youbing. Map captures biggest human migration on earth Zhan started taking photos for an internal factory publication in 2002. For more than a decade, Zhan was himself one of China's 250-million strong "floating population" of migrant workers. The Lunar New Year is the only time of year China's army of migrant workers gets to go home. Yet it was a deeper feeling of insecurity that drove him to keep documenting the life of migrant workers - a turbulent life over which they have little control.
