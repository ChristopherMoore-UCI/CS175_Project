Despite the flurry of reports, aviation experts say most new commercial airplanes do encounter problems after their launch. Problems on other new aircraft The Airbus A380 also had problems when it started flying in 2007, but aviation expert Janet Bednarek loves to fly it. Every new airplane is going to have these kinds of "teething problems," said John Goglia, a former National Transportation Safety Board member and former airline mechanic. Like the 787, the Boeing 747 was also a "game changer" and had a lot of issues when it started flying in 1970, according to aviation consultant Michael Boyd. "We are flying more airplanes that have been engineered to be safer," he said.
