Curtis D. Tucker was thrilled to receive a Farrah Fawcett poster for his 14th birthday in 1976. Fawcett's hairstyle was in high demand It certainly was the right shot for cartoonist Curtis D. Tucker. The present was one he had fervently requested from his parents and a hot item for many a young, red-blooded male during that bicentennial year: a poster of Farrah Fawcett (then known as Farrah Fawcett-Majors). "The Farrah Fawcett poster really is one of the last iconic pinup images of the 20th century," Buszek said.
