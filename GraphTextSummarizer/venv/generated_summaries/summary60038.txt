UNICEF said that if donations exceed anticipated needs, it will redirect excess funds to other areas where children have the greatest need. Globalgiving.org, another aid organization that allows donors to select the causes they want to support -- including climate change, economic development, human rights and disaster relief -- has established a Japan Earthquake and Tsunami Relief Fund. Save the Children sent emergency response teams to assist its staff in Japan. Relief workers from AmeriCares, which is also accepting donations, are in Japan working to deliver medicine and supplies to hospitals, shelters and health responders.
