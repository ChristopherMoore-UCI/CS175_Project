Newtown calls on Connecticut to 'show America the way' on gun control Closing gun show loophole A group of four senators working behind the scenes on a bipartisan bill to expand background checks on gun sales is making significant progress, according to sources in both parties familiar with their work. Curbing gun trafficking A new House bipartisan gun control bill seeks to make gun trafficking a federal crime. There is some bipartisan support for expanded background checks, especially to keep guns out of the hands of people with mental illness. House Majority Leader Eric Cantor, R-Viginia, told CNN that he supports beefed up background checks for gun sales. The package is similar to other Democratic measures that would address background checks, ban high capacity magazines, and crack down on gun trafficking.
