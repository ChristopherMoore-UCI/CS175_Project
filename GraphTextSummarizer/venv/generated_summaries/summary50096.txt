-- President Obama and Indian Prime Minister Manmohan Singh agreed Tuesday to team up and tackle a checklist of economic, nuclear, security and environmental challenges. Obama also supported Singh's backing for nuclear nonproliferation efforts. Obama said he reaffirmed his administration's commitment to implement a civil nuclear agreement with India, which Singh signed with former President George W. Bush. Singh also touched on the same topics and invited Obama and his family to visit India, an invitation the U.S. leader accepted. Singh also brought up another issue: that the U.S.-India "strategic partnership should facilitate transfer of high technologies to India.
