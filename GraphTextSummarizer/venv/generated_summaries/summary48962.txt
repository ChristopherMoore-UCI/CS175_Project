Information from the database was used in two high-profile stories last year that looked into the wealth of the families of China's ruling elite, which appeared in The New York Times and Bloomberg News. A change in corporate record-keeping in Hong Kong has turned into a flashpoint for the debate about freedom of information in the Chinese territory. A number of stories on property scandals within Hong Kong also relied on information from the database. Hong Kong, a former British colony, is part of China but maintains freedom of the press, speech and no Internet controls. The information is regularly used by both investigative journalists and financial firms to help trace who is running a Hong Kong-registered company.
