When assisted suicide is not the answer Under Oregon's law, doctors treating "death with dignity" patients prescribe doses of barbiturates, Hedberg said. Judicial decisions in two other states, Montana and New Mexico, authorize doctors to prescribe fatal drug doses in such circumstances, although the rulings haven't become state law. Three states -- Oregon, Washington and Vermont -- have "death with dignity" laws that allow terminally ill, mentally competent residents to voluntarily request and receive prescription drugs to hasten their death.
