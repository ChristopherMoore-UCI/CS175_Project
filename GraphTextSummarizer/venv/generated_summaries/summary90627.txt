Already, contraceptive care is essential preventive care for millions of women. They also prevent an unanticipated worsening of health for women with pre-existing health conditions, such as diabetes or heart disease. Birth control is often an additional health expense that many women simply cannot afford if money is tight, putting them at high risk for an unintended pregnancy. Birth control is not only safe and effective preventive care, it is also care that physicians support and millions of women need. As physicians dedicated to improving the health of women, we ask: Why is contraception still seen as controversial in the face of overwhelming evidence about its benefits?
