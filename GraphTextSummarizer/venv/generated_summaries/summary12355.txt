They were hoping to eradicate a bunch of al Qaeda operatives. The Pakistani army has surged more troops into the tribal areas, attempting to eradicate the Taliban and al Qaeda. The government assigned me and my team a political agent named Naeem Afridi. They became our personal bodyguards, and followed us through the tight warren of gun shops and factories, barren little brick rooms where upward of 1,000 guns are manufactured every day. One week before that, I arrived in Pakistan to visit Darra Adamkhel, the massive open-air market located deep in the tribal areas, where a frighteningly high percentage of Islamic holy warriors goes to buy their guns.
