The choice of Zaha Hadid was crucial for the institute's visionary founder Youk Chhang. Due for construction in 2015, the Sleuk Rith Institute, designed by Pritzker-winning Hadid, will be a landmark genocide memorial for Cambodia, breaking away from stereotypes of commemorative architecture. Uplifting architecture From renderings of Hadid's design, we can see five majestic towers rising out of the surrounding forest like beautiful mutant trees. "We believe that this project is one step in moving beyond memorial, to reconciliation and healing, it will be truly transformative, " Hadid tells CNN Style. According to Chhang, women struggled to raise the next generation on their own and have been instrumental in rebuilding Cambodian society, "but they have had too little voice" throughout the country's tragic past.
