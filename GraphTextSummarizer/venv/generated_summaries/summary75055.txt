Iceland, Norway, Finland and Sweden were considered to have the smallest gap between men and women when four key areas were taken into account: economic participation and opportunity, educational attainment, health and survival, and political empowerment. Where it might have room for improvement, however, is in health and survival (38th) and political empowerment (40th). The political empowerment category measures female representation in government and other decision-making structures. No. 1 Iceland scored a 0.8496 on a scale of 0-1, with 1 being perfect equality between men and women. It also ranked sixth in economic participation and opportunity.
