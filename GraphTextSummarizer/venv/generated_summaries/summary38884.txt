Ebola facility in Liberia attacked; patients flee People are scared for a reason: Liberia's infrastructure cannot cope with Ebola. -- On Wednesday, angry residents of West Point, one of the poorest slums of Liberia, clashed with police when they discovered that the government has quarantined the entire area in an effort to contain the spread of Ebola. People from all walks of life in Liberia still denied the existence of Ebola, including many at the waterside, one of the biggest markets in Monrovia, the capital. Tracing people who have come in contact with sick Ebola patients is a huge task for the Ministry of Health.
