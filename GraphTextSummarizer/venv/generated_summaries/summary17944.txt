-- Former Soviet spy Leonid Shebarshin, who was very briefly head of the KGB, was found dead Friday in his apartment in Moscow, an apparent suicide, officials said. A weapon was found near the body, Investigation Committee spokesman Vladimir Markin said. The 76-year-old left a suicide note, the state-run news agency Itar-Tass reported Friday, citing city police. The contents of the note were not disclosed "in the interest of the investigation," according to police, the news agency said. According to Itar-Tass, Shebarshin was born in Moscow in 1935.
