-- A 5.8-magnitude earthquake struck Saturday in the Mediterranean Sea, some 50 miles (80 kilometers) south southwest of Avsallar, Turkey, the U.S. Geological Survey reported. Video shot in Antalya and aired by Turkish broadcaster DHA showed chandeliers shaking. A magnitude 7.6 earthquake in Izmit, Turkey, killed more than 17,000 people in 1999, according to the USGS. Turkey is no stranger to seismic events. A magnitude 7.2 tremor in Duzce the same year killed 894 people, the USGS reported.
