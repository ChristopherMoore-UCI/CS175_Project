-- The Moscow skyline of the Kremlin and St. Basil's Cathedral vanished Friday as a blanket of thick, noxious smoke shrouded the metropolis, leaving many of the city's 10 million residents with sore throats and burning eyes. On Friday, Russian President Dmitry Medvedev visited a Moscow ambulance station, accompanied by top health officials. Some flights to Moscow were diverted Friday because or poor visibility. The Russian Ministry for Health and Social Development reported Friday that 52 people have died as a result of the extensive fires in central Russia. In interviews with the state-run RIA Novosti news agency, health officials likened the pollution levels to smoking several packs of cigarettes a day.
