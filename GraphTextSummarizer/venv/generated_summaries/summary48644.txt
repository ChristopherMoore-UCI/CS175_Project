Dennis Debbaudt trains police on how to respond to people with intellectual disabilities, and argues that such cases require special tactics. When law enforcement officers encounter people with disabilities, things can go wrong very easily. One day last January, Robert Ethan Saylor, a 26-year-old man with Down syndrome, went to see the movie "Zero Dark Thirty." Police violence against people with disabilities is not uncommon, but the cases don't seem to get a lot of publicity. This tragedy raises questions about how people with disabilities fit into society.
