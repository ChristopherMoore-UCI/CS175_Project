He has lent financial and promotional support to Airbnb, the room rental listings service; Flipboard, the iPad news aggregator that CNN's Zite competes with; Foursquare, the location check-in application; and others. This strategy got him into some trouble recently, when he edited a section of Conde Nast's Details magazine in which he promoted tech companies he was financially involved in without disclosing his relationships. He said he sometimes hides his affiliation with companies to avoid overshadowing them. In an analogy-laden interview at the TechCrunch Disrupt conference here, Kutcher described how he picks from the myriad small companies that appeal to him for cash. The "Two and a Half Men" actor has emerged as a prominent investor in Silicon Valley startups, lending his own money as well as money from his investment firm called A-Grade Ventures.
