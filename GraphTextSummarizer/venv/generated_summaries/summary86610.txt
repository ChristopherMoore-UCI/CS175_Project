"He came back thinking there were better ways to deal with things than war," Nygaard said. Olsen, 24, had seen his share of war in two tours of Iraq as a Marine. But war touched Olsen as it does almost everyone who comes that close. Nygaard said Olsen told him about a couple of close calls, one in which he rolled over a roadside bomb that somehow failed to detonate. Olsen had always been a quiet, shy kid, Nygaard said.
