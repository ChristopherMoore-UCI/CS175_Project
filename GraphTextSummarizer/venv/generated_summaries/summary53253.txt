"I learned about serif and sans serif typefaces, about varying the amount of space between different letter combinations, about what makes great typography great. For some reason yet to be fully justified, the San Francisco font appeared to be made up of odd letters torn from a newspaper, a digital ransom note. Could it be that the one thing that Jobs recognized almost 30 years ago -- the absolute pleasure of the shape of letters -- is being forgotten in a race for miniaturization, speed and the relentless pursuit of gadgetry newness? Jobs was the first to give us a real choice of fonts, and thus the ability to express ourselves digitally with emotion, clarity and variety. Ten years after college Jobs designed his first Mac, and it came with something unprecedented -- a wide choice of fonts.
