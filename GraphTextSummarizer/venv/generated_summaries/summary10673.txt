Logan, a 2-year-old terminally ill boy whose parents moved up their ceremony so he could witness it, died Monday night at his home in Jeannette, Pennsylvania, with family at his side, Westmoreland County Deputy Coroner John Ackerman said Tuesday. On Monday, Logan's parents, Christine Swidorsky and Sean Stevenson, held him for long periods after it became apparent that his death was near, his mother wrote on Facebook. The child died of complications from the genetic disorder Fanconi anemia, Ackerman said. Fanconi anemia most frequently results in death, commonly from cancer, between the ages of 25 and 30.
