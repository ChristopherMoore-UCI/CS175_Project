Some have argued that, while not intervening in the running of companies, the government should require firmer commitments to retain research facilities and employees. Companies have chosen to conduct their R&D here in part because of the strength of Britain's universities and a positive tax system, including the "patent box" which incentivizes businesses to invest in research. -- Whether or not the British parliament decides to subject the proposed takeover of UK-based AstraZeneca by American pharmaceutical giant Pfizer to a public interest test, it's clear we have already seen much more political intervention than is typically involved in an acquisition of one company by another. When companies choose to locate their operations in the UK, they do so because it is an attractive place to do business. Over 50% of shares on the UK stock market are owned by investors from the rest of the world and many of the companies have employees and customers on several continents.
