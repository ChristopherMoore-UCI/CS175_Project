"This is a great step forward in our bilateral relations," Mexican Economy Minister Bruno Ferrari said. U.S. President Barack Obama and Mexican President Felipe Calderon announced the agreement during a visit by the Mexican leader to Washington last week. However, Ferrari noted, Mexico reserves the right to re-introduce tariffs if any parts of the agreement are violated by the United States. But safety and security concerns spurred the United States to bar Mexican trucks on U.S. roadways. A pilot program in 2007 to get Mexican trucks on U.S. roads was de-funded.
