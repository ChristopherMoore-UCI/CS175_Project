Food distribution is ongoing in all three countries, with more food on the way. Luescher said rice, lentils, cooking oil and rations are being distributed, food for hospital patients is cooked by partner organizations, and quarantined populations get the food and take it home to prepare. How are people in affected areas that have been isolated from the world getting necessities such as food? According to Bettina Luescher, the organization's chief spokeswoman for North America, the WFP has provided food and assistance to more than 430,000 people affected by the Ebola crisis, feeding people for six months starting eight days after the crisis was declared in Guinea. On the ground, aid workers explain how people should line up for food.
