Sunday's race was won by Mercedes' Nico Rosberg, with McLaren rookie Kevin Magnussen taking second place in Ricciardo's absence with his teammate Jenson Button in third. The Australian driver, making his debut for the all-conquering team, crossed the line in second place before learning of his expulsion. Each car is limited to 100kg of fuel per race. Red Bull has cast doubt over the equipment used by F1's governing body the FIA to measure fuel-flow, despite its manufacturers Gil Sensors receiving "positive feedback" from the FIA. The International Automobile Federation says it will hear the appeal on April 14, the Monday before the fourth race of the season takes place in China.
