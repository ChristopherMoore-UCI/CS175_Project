It includes additional measures for all relevant sectors -- from energy generation, transportation and agriculture to insulating buildings. Renewable energy sources now account for nearly 30% of our electricity demands, and by 2050, our energy supply will be based almost completely on renewable sources. Meanwhile, money that would otherwise have been needed to import expensive fossil resources now instead goes to individuals, cooperatives, farmers, and small and medium-sized enterprises, that can all themselves become energy suppliers. We have made significant progress toward an agreement that for the first time will involve all countries in serious efforts to combat climate change. Indeed, in recognition of the importance of tackling climate change, Germany earlier this month adopted a package of measures that will considerably bolster our efforts.
