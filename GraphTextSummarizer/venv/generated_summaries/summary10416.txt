After 40 years in North Korea, escapee returns to Seoul 'Unspeakable atrocities' in North Korea North and South Korea hold first family reunion in three years The reclusive state has admitted to kidnapping a number of Japanese nationals, mostly to help train spies in Japanese language and culture. In a series of meetings in Ulan Bator arranged by Japanese and North Korean foreign ministry officials, the Japanese couple met 26-year-old Kim Eun-gyong,  her husband and 10-month-old daughter. The abductee issue is a considerable sticking point in Japan-North Korea relations. North Korean authorities insist that she committed suicide, although this is disputed in Japan and repatriated ashes proved not to be a DNA match.
