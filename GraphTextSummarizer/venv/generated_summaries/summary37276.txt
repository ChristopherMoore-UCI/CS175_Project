Despite being the No.1 ranked international team for most of the intervening years, success on the ultimate stage eluded successive All Blacks teams. No matter the 8-7 final win over France was more notable for perspiration than inspiration, the All Blacks had finally laid a 24-year-old hoodoo since their victory in the first-ever World Cup in New Zealand in 1987. After the final at Eden Park, New Zealand were named team of the year in the International Rugby Board annual awards and Henry was coach of the year.
