Siku also continues to shoot on mobile phones and has, with a number of his workshop colleagues, founded YEBELA, a collective of video artists who show their films in the streets of Kinshasa. "After her course here in Kinshasa I fell in love with directing, editing and working in a team," Siku told CNN. "The pupils who were most interested were coming from the roughest townships in Kinshasa," the 49-year-old told CNN. Dhelsing organizes mobile filmmaking master classes in deprived regions of the world to give young people in developing countries an opportunity to tell their stories. "Camera phones are affordable, small and easy to maneuver," Siku told CNN.
