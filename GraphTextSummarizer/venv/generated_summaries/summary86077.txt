"The attempt to grant visas is an attempt to limit and control the number that come, rather than being generous and open," said Albert Fishlow, professor emeritus at Columbia University and the author of "Starting Over: Brazil Since 1985. Facing with illegal immigration from Haiti, Brazil's National Council on Immigration agreed this month to provide 1,200 work visas per year to Haitians affected by the 2010 earthquake who emigrate to Brazil. "But this negative impact is decreasing because we have the demand for skilled labor in Brazil," he said. One reported proposal would create two "lines" for foreigners applying for work visas in Brazil. Unlike other work visas in Brazil, applicants need not have a job contract in hand when they arrive.
