The new numbers came out the same day that acting Health Minister Adel bin Mohammed Fakieh announced he had relieved Deputy Health Minister Dr. Ziad Memish from his post, according to a statement on the health ministry's website. Those with MERS have severe acute respiratory illness, including symptoms such as fever, cough and shortness of breath. In a recently published study in mBio, researchers said they isolated live MERS virus from two single-humped camels, known as dromedaries. Last week, the World Health Organization reported it was aware of 636 "laboratory-confirmed cases" of MERS infections, which had led to 193 deaths. The Saudi  Ministry of Health reported that 282 people have died since 2012 from the Middle East Respiratory Syndrome coronavirus, or MERS-CoV, a major uptick from the previous official death toll of 190.
