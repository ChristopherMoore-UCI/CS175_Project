Khanna: Arab world has the money to solve its problems Parag Khanna, author of "How to Run the World: Charting a Course to the Next Renaissance," is a senior research fellow with the New America Foundation. The days are numbered for Arab authoritarian regimes, and those who argue that Islam/Arab culture is incompatible with democracy are left scrambling. The countries in a possible "second wave" of Arab revolutions have even dimmer prospects. Taylor: Expect regime change in Libya, Yemen Julie Taylor, a Middle East specialist who lived in Egypt for four years, is a political scientist at the RAND Corp. The Tunisian and Egyptian paradigm of regime change in three weeks probably cannot be repeated.
