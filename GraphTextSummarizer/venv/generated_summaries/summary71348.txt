When names cause confusion William John Kane VIII goes by two names. But Mehrabian, who has studied how people perceive names, argues it's too personal of an approach. It's an "established name," Mehrabian said, but "there are names with a lot more positive connotations." Prince George and other royalty are typically bestowed with a long string of names. The British royal family tends to pick traditional names, usually referencing monarchs of the past.
