According to Kitt's official Web site, she was nominated for a Tony three times, a Grammy and Emmy twice. -- Singer and actress Eartha Kitt has died, her publicist, Patty Freedman, told CNN on Thursday. Broadway stardom landed Kitt a recording deal that led to a string of best-selling records, including "Love for Sale," "I Want to Be Evil," "Santa Baby" and "Folk Tales of the Tribes of Africa." Kitt, 81, died in New York, where she was being treated for colon cancer, Freedman said. Kitt was well known for her distinctive voice and made a name for herself in her portrayal of Catwoman in the television series "Batman."
