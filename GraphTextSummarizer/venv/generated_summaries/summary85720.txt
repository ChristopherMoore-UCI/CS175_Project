Retrofitting smart tech into existing city infrastructure has been going on for a while now. Now, Kontokosta says the emphasis is on using smart city technology to help people make better decisions for themselves. In the United States, experts say smart cities built from scratch will be unlikely. The idea is to attract businesses that want smart tech efficiency to move to those districts. You've got to wonder what they'll teach us about how to bring smarter technology to the places we live, play and work.
