Coach fliers may complain about airline seats, but U.S. airlines are filling those seats pretty successfully. That's the gist of one of hundreds of responses to a CNN.com report about airline seat comfort, configuration and cost. But the numbers also show that airline profits are down a bit, probably due to higher fuel prices, according to the Los Angeles Times. America's battle with weight is bleeding over into airline cabins. From the looks of it, seems like carriers aren't having any trouble selling seats, even if they are less than royal thrones.
