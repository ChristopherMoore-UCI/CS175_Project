Having previously been banned, sports classes are now allowed in girls' schools -- and the country's first sports club for women opened in June, with training programs including physical fitness, karate and yoga. The Olympics has begun a process in Saudi Arabia that looks set to dramatically change the lives of women in the country. It made it virtually impossible to rationalize the now infamous words of the founder of the modern Olympic movement -- Pierre de Coubertin -- from 1896: "Olympics with women would be incorrect, unpractical, uninteresting, and unaesthetic. A group of women in Saudi Arabia huddled around a television screen watching and recording the moment that history was made -- and barriers were broken -- blazing a trail for future generations of women in their country. It wasn't just the number of women we were talking about, but also the way in which we were talking about them.
