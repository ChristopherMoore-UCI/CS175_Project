With MS, the body's immune system destroys a fatty substance called myelin that protects nerve fibers in the brain and spinal cord. It is not normally fatal; most patients with MS have "a normal or near-normal life expectancy," the National MS Society says. -- NASCAR driver Trevor Bayne has been diagnosed with multiple sclerosis, according to his racing team. Multiple sclerosis is a chronic disease that affects the central nervous system, often causing pain, numbness in the limbs and a loss of vision. As the disease progresses, patients may experience extreme fatigue, balance problems and muscle weakness.
