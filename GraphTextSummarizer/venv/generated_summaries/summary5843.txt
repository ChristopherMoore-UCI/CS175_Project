"He is a phenomenon," said Mark Swed, Los Angeles Times music critic. He already has organized one youth orchestra in the run-down neighborhoods of south Los Angeles. As music director of the philharmonic, Dudamel said he hopes to spread a little of the El Sistema magic in Los Angeles. Dudamel, 28, engenders a passion among music lovers that is entirely new to the world of classical music. Tickets for his Saturday debut as music director of the Los Angeles Philharmonic disappeared within minutes -- the fastest sellout in the history of the 18,000-seat Hollywood Bowl.
