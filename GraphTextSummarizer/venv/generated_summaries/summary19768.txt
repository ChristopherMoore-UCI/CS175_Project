"[Job seekers] over 50 years old may be used to looking for work in a newspaper, attending job fairs, dropping by a company to inquire about job leads and speaking to those in their network regarding job opportunities," says Lavie Margolin, career coach, speaker and author of "Lion Cub Job Search: Practical Job Search Assistance for Practical Job Seekers." There are essentially three generations of job seekers vying for jobs today: Search for jobs on career websites as well as social networking sites such as Facebook, Twitter or LinkedIn. "They are used to online job searches, emailing résumés and following up via both email and handwritten notes. Because of this, job seekers are finding themselves competing with people of all different ages for the same job; people that can bring different experiences and skill sets to the position.
