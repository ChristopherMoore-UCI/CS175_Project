North Korea issued the objections to exercises Key Resolve and Foal Eagle scheduled for March and April to U.S. commander James D. Sherman, state run news agency KCNA reported. Key Resolve will involve 10,000 South Korean troops and 3,500 U.S. troops in exercises March 11 to 21. A United Nations military commission informed Pyongyang of the upcoming routine exercises, according to a joint statement from U.S. and South Korean military officials. The message was delivered over the phone in English, South Korean news agency Yonhap reported. Ahead of annual, routine military exercises between South Korea and the United States, North Korea issued its usual caustic objections Saturday.
