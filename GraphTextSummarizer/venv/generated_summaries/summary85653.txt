As a child, he traveled with his mother to eastern Cuba to visit family. "I was, like, 3, or 4, or 5 at that time, and I was separated from my family, and that separation from identity, ironically enough, having to do with Cuba itself, has always been, like, one of the subthemes going through my work -- which is what is an identity about? "The truth is that before that period, that book really, there were very few publishing houses in New York City dealing or publishing main -- you know, Latino writers. Among his other books are: "Empress of the Splendid Season," " Mr. Ives' Christmas," "The Fourteen Sisters of Emilio Montez O'Brien" and "Our House in the Last World." Most Latino authors were being published out of university presses or small presses," he said.
