Pope Francis moved a predecessor closer to sainthood on Sunday, beatifying Pope Paul VI on the last day of a gathering in Rome of church leaders from around the world. Paul VI is also known for having pioneered papal world visits, traveling to Africa, Latin America and Asia. The announcement of the beatification came in May, two weeks after Pope Francis canonized two other  predecessors, John XXIII and John Paul II, allowing them to ascend to sainthood. Pope Paul VI based the decision on Catholic teachings on marriage. Pope Paul VI's involves an unborn child  in California, Vatican Radio said.
