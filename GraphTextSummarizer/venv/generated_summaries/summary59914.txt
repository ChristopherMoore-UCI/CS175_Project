As we drive on, he tells us Yemen's security is worsening and Saudi Arabia is spending almost $3 billion on beefing up border security. The men -- and they were all men -- admitted that sneaking over the border is getting much harder amid the increase in guard posts and border fences. Said Kouachi, one of the Charlie Hebdo attackers in Paris, is believed to have slipped in to Yemen not far from Saudi's border, making his way there for terror training from Oman. No fence reaches up this far, the tiny smattering of guards solidifies the impression that at these altitudes, border control works on an honor system. Border guards told us in the past three months alone, they have picked up 42,000 people along the 800-kilometer (about 500 miles) border.
