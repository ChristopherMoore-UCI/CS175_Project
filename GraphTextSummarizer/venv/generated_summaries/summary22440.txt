Americans spend only about 10% of their after-tax incomes on food of all kinds, including restaurant meals and prepackaged foods. But step outside the developed world, and the price of food suddenly becomes the single most important fact of human economic life. And if food prices surge again? When grain prices spiked in 2007-2008, bread riots shook 30 countries across the developing world, from Haiti to Bangladesh, according to the Financial Times. These higher grain prices will flow through to higher food prices.
