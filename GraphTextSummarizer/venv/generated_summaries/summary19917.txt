iReport: Share your photos of affected beaches Scattered tar balls and oil patches have affected Panama City Beach. Due to heavier oiling on Perdido Key, a health advisory has been issued for beaches stretching from the Florida-Alabama line to the entrance of Johnson Beach on Perdido Key, the Pensacola Bay Area visitors bureau said. "There have been no reports of Deepwater Horizon Oil Spill-related oil products reaching the shore beyond the Northwest Florida region," Visit Florida's website said. "On Pensacola Beach there are reports of tar balls and sheen on less than 1 percent of the entire beach," the bureau's website said. iReport: See photos from Okaloosa Island Dime-size to 5-inch tar balls continue to wash up in widely scattered areas of northwest Florida, but all of the state's beaches remain open, according to Visit Florida, the state's tourism corporation.
