The collapse occurred as hundreds of people were gathering in Bab Berdieyinne mosque in Meknes, a town in northern Morocco located 140 km (87 miles) southeast of Rabat, it said. A 400-year-old minaret collapsed Friday on worshipers in Meknes, Morocco, killing 36 and injuring 71, the official news agency Maghreb Arabe Presse reported, citing the Interior Ministry. "We were in the middle of prayers" when the structure collapsed, said one victim told Morocco TV from his hospital bed. It broadcast video showing dozens of people inside the mosque carrying survivors out of the damaged structure to waiting ambulances. The incident occurred after several days of unrelenting rain, the television station reported.
