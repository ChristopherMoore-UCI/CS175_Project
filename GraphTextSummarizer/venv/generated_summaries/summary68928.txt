No damage was caused by the firing Friday morning, said the official, who requested anonymity in keeping with South Korean military custom. -- Two South Korean soldiers fired at a civilian airplane after mistaking it for an aircraft from North Korea , a South Korean military official said Saturday. The two soldiers were stationed off the coast of Gyodong Island, near the North Korean border, when they fired their rifles at an Asiana Airlines flight, the military official said. According to the Yonhap report, the plane was carrying about 119 crew and passengers from China, and was making its descent to Incheon International Airport.
