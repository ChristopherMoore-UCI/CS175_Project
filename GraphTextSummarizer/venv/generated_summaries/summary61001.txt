-- Clashes between pro-Palestinian protesters and Israeli forces erupted along Israel's borders and occupied territories Sunday, leaving at least 12 dead on a Palestinian mourning day marking the birth of the Jewish state. Pro-Palestinian demonstrators also clashed with Jordanian security forces near the border of Jordan and Israel, injuring 25 people including police officers and protesters, according to a Jordanian police spokesman. "Syria condemns Israel's criminal practices against the people in Golan, Palestine and South Lebanon which resulted in a number of martyrs and injured people," the state-run Syrian Arab News Agency reported, citing an official with the country's foreign ministry. Elsewhere in the region, pro-Palestinian protesters clashed with police outside the Israeli Embassy in Cairo, Egypt, on Sunday. The Israeli military said troops fired warning shots after protesters attempted to breach a fence and infiltrate into Israeli territory It was unclear who fired the shots that killed and injured protesters.
