Before him, there were popes from Bethlehem (St. Evaristus, from 97 to 105), Jerusalem (Pope Theodore I, from 642 to 649) and modern-day Libya (Saint Victor I, from 189 to 199). The record for shortest reign goes to Pope Urban VII, who served for 13 days in 1590. -- Pope Francis is a first in many ways. Over the centuries, the length of reign has varied widely. He's the first Latin American pontiff; the first Jesuit; the first Francis -- and the first non-European in 1,272 years.
