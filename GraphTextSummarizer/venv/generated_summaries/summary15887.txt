Rainwater is collected through a network of drains, canals, rivers, storm water, collection ponds and reservoirs with the aim to catch water across two-thirds of the country. Professor Asit Biswas from the Lee Kuan School of Public Policy feels other countries should follow the example set by Singapore and even the current standards can be improved to eventually re-use every last drop of water. Water security has long been a national priority in Singapore as half of its current water supplies are imported from neighboring Malaysia. "First is how to make the water system less energy intensive and the second one is consumer behavior with respect to water." Whilst known for its strong economy, Singapore is lacking one essential asset -- water.
