They say they can't shake the memories of Whitewater or the belief that President Bill Clinton could have done more for the military during his time in the White House. After spending a day talking with Coaldale voters, it seems to me the anger Obama described in his now controversial comments might not have been too far off the mark. Coaldale, like many other small Pennsylvania communities, has seen better times. Tommy says Obama is an unknown and believes Clinton will be a "good start, although we don't expect her to do everything." This election season, however, the county has mirrored trends across the state -- more voters switching to the Democratic Party and more new voters registering as Democrats.
