Every morning, Sissa Abu Dahou puts on traditional male dress, known as a jalabiya, and heads into the streets of the Egyptian city of Luxor for a day of hard work. A widow at just 21, Dahou was forced to fend for herself in Egypt's patriarchal south, where decades ago it was unheard of for women to earn their own living. After her interview on CBC television, the governor of Luxor province offered Dahou a kiosk and a cash advance. Even in recent years, women make up barely 24% of Egypt's workforce, according to the World Bank. her daughter asked during a recorded interview with Egyptian television network CBC.
