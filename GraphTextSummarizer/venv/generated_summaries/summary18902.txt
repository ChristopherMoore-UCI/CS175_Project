President Barack Obama used a Memorial Day ceremony to say it is time for America to properly welcome home Vietnam veterans and show them and their families the respect and gratitude they deserved but didn't receive decades ago. In his remarks at the ceremony, Obama spoke of making amends for the nation's failure to properly welcome home Vietnam veterans in the 1960s and '70s. Obama and other speakers at the ceremony hosted by actor Tom Selleck, spoke of issues close to Vietnam veterans, including the continued effort to identify and repatriate remains of the more than 1,000 missing in action. The first phase of the commemoration over the next two years involves generating support and participation, with a focus on "hometown" events providing personal recognition and thanks to Vietnam veterans, according to information provided to the media. In an emotional moment, he asked Vietnam veterans in the crowd to stand and spoke words he characterized as long overdue: "Welcome home.
