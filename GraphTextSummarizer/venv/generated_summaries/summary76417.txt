Streaming video such as HD movies from Netflix, using bittorrent to download movies and heavy gaming with services like Steam can easily eat up lots of data, especially in households with multiple heavy internet users. (WIRED)  -- AT&T broadband users will soon face a cap on the amount of internet data they can download a month. Derek Turner, the research director for the net neutrality advocacy group Free Press says the limits will discourage online innovation, and that with the growth curve of internet usage, the limits will soon catch many internet user. Congressman wants to ban download caps DSL and UVerse connect fairly directly to a hub -- unlike cable connections where users share a local loop that can become congested. Traditional DSL users will be capped at 150 GB per month, while subscribers to the fiber-backed UVerse system have a 250-GB limit.
