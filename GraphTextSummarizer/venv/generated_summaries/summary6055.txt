The number of drone strikes in 2011 fell by 40% from the record number of strikes in 2010. Obama revealed: The man, the president To the extent that the targets of drone attacks can be ascertained, under Bush, al Qaeda members accounted for 25% of all drone targets compared to 40% for Taliban targets. The number of militants reported killed by drone strikes is 89% of the fatalities under Obama compared to 67% under Bush. Some combination of pushback from the State Department, increased congressional oversight, the closure of the CIA drone base in Pakistan and, perhaps, a declining number of targets in the tribal regions and a greater desire to heed Pakistani sensitivities about drone attacks has led to a sharp fall in the number of strikes since 2010. Covert drone strikes are one of President Obama's key national security policies.
