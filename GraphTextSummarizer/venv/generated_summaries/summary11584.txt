Out of "an abundance of caution," the CDC has been contacting people who were passengers on the same flights as the Florida and Indiana patients with confirmed MERS, Schuchat said. The Florida patient is not a U.S. citizen, said Dr. Anne Schuchat, director of the CDC's National Center for Immunization and Respiratory Diseases. The Florida patient The 44-year-old Florida patient traveled on May 1 from Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, to London, then from London to Boston, Boston to Atlanta, and finally Atlanta to Orlando. In a recently published study in mBio, researchers said they isolated live MERS virus from two single-humped camels, known as dromedaries. In some countries, the virus has spread from person to person, but only when they were in close contact, such as when a person was caring for an ill person.
