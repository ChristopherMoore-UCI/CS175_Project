And U.S. officials consider the Yemen-based Al Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula the most dangerous branch of al Qaeda. With the chaotic situation in Yemen, Western efforts to hunt down al Qaeda are not impossible, but greatly hampered. Many would-be jihadis from the West are recruited into al Qaeda through a slick English-language online magazine, Inspire, that's run out of Yemen. Finally, the unrest in Yemen also has provided an opening for ISIS, which is keen to outflank al Qaeda and prove itself the true defender of the faith. Still, ISIS remains a relatively small player in Yemen compared to al Qaeda.
