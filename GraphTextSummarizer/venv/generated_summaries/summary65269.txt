Anthony Falvo, a U.S. Navy Pacific Fleet spokesman in Pearl Harbor, Hawaii, told CNN on Wednesday that Navy salvage experts are still formulating the details of how they'll cut up the 1,312-ton minesweeper. Navy officials are still trying to determine how the Guardian ended up on the reef. As for what the loss of the Guardian, one of 14 Avenger class mine countermeasures ships in the Navy, means for the service, Falvo said it was too early to speculate. Last week, the Navy said it hoped to lift the ship off the reef in its entirety. James Stockman, a Navy spokesman, said last week.
