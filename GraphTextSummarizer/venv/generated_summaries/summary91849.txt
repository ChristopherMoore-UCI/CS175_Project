His new book, "Unaccountable: What Hospitals Won't Tell You and How Transparency Can Revolutionize Health Care," is an eye-opening look at the culture of medicine. Makary is a surgeon at Johns Hopkins Hospital in Baltimore and a professor at Johns Hopkins School of Public Health. -- Profit-hungry doctors and hospitals, who take "sales commissions" from drug and medical device companies for using a certain drug, or implanting a certain device in patients. -- You would think surgeon Marty Makary might have a lot of doctors and hospitals gunning for him. "It's the patients who can change it," he says, by pushing to change the way doctors and hospitals do their jobs.
