The explosion comes after China announced this week that it had closed 1,355 small coal mines this year, according to Xinhua The effort was part of a larger plan to restructure the mining industry to  prevent deadly accidents and reduce greenhouse gas emissions, Xinhua reported,  citing the National Energy Administration (NEA). Small coal mines, which use outdated technology, will be replaced  with larger coal mines, increasing capacity. Xinhua news agency earlier reported that 30 miners were trapped after the explosion in Henan province. -- A gas explosion Saturday killed at least 20 workers at a coal mine in central China, state media reported. China has one of the world's deadliest records for miners, with poor safety  standards accounting for thousands of deaths each year.
