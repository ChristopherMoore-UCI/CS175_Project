"A majority of blacks now believe that a solution to the country's racial problems will eventually be found," said CNN polling director Keating Holland. Let us resolve to join together as a nation to ensure that racial prejudice in America, as well as an ethic of non-achievement based on excuses and low expectations, dies the same death it did in the November ballot box. When Sen. Obama announced his candidacy for president in 2007, most people, black and white, thought it would be, at best, an interesting sideshow. Americans wanted to move beyond racial categorization and the politics of division. Today Barack Obama will be sworn in as the 44th president of the United States of America.
