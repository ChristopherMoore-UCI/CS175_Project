Over the horizon, we can see the dawn of the next great chapter of U.S. economic expansion: the conversion from coal-fired electricity to a new energy economy based on the exploitation of the vast quantities of natural gas entrapped in U.S. shale formations. Everybody agrees that the uncertainty over the future of the euro currency worries U.S. financial markets. As Obama likes to note, more people are at work today in the U.S. private sector than on the day he took office. U.S. corporations are earning record profits. Somebody has to buy, and with China and India slowing down, Germany -- a much richer country -- would emerge as what the U.S. was in the early 2000s: the world's consumer of last resort.
