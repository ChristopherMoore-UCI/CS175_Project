"Online social networks are so popular and so likely to reveal people's actual personalities because they allow for social interactions that feel real in many ways," said psychologist Mitja Back of Johannes Gutenberg University in Mainz, Germany, who published the report with colleagues in Psychological Science. The consumer-advocacy site attributed that rise to growing concerns about privacy. There have been several high-profile cases of fake Facebook identities. "One can almost be a totally different human on Facebook than in real life," wrote Rebecca Greenfield for The Atlantic. Some users have been known to create profiles under false or incomplete names to hide from employers or job recruiters.
