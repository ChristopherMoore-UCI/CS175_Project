We all know oil is lighter than water. There are three primary plans of attack for cleaning oil spills in the water: burn the oil, apply chemical dispersants to break it down, or manually remove it. Here, the government creates controlled oil spills in a giant tank more than two football fields long. Today, cleanup experts are practicing manual techniques to remove oil. Taking oil off the surface of the water is kind of like peeling the filling off of an oreo cookie; you're skimming it.
