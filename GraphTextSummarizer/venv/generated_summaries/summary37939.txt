Millar said that he has encountered proposals for social media interaction with drama series, where an audience vote would determine plot twists and denouements, essentially turning television drama into a "Choose Your Own Adventure"-style experience. And for social media companies, any tie-in with television can only be advantageous. While X-Factor, remains by far the biggest regular hitter when it comes to TV-related tweets in the UK -- 3.7 million of the 38.7 million made about shows broadcast free-to-air on British television from April 1 to November 1 this year -- it is not just entertainment shows getting in on the act. Media analysts are coy on the specifics of what is lined up next for social television. Numerous studies have discovered that up to 80% of television viewers of all ages now incorporate a "second screen" -- laptop, smartphone or tablet computer- into their viewing habits.
