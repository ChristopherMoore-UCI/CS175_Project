The ECB's latest crisis-busting solution to buy government debt -- dubbed Outright Monetary Transactions Such actions include lending to banks at cheap rates, buying-up national debt, negotiating bailouts for financially beleaguered countries and even brokering political support. Prime Ministers Mario Monti and Mariano Rajoy had both urged the European Central Bank to buy bonds of eurozone countries to help governments manage unsustainable levels of interest on debt. Vaubel argues that by using the government-funded ESM as a condition of central bank support, the ECB "accepts orders from finance ministers" which is "prohibited." Joerg Rocholl, president of the European School of Management and Technology, told CNN the central bank is taking its mandate to the extreme, even with conditions imposed on countries seeking aid.
