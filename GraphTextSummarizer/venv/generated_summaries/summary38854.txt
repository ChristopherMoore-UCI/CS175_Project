Righting a wrong Ethel Arms has a line she uses when the topic of high school rites of passage and prom comes up: "We didn't have a prom because of the civil rights movement." It puts the memory of 1963 in perspective and justifies the sacrifice. As often is the case when people witness a historic period, many black high school students in Birmingham in 1963 did not recognize the moment that was upon them. This would change on May 2, 1963, when hundreds of children, some as young as 6, left school to march in Birmingham in opposition to segregation. Segregation in Birmingham permeated everything, down to the Bibles that judges used to swear witnesses in -- there was one holy book for white witnesses and another for black witnesses.
