San Francisco police completed a thorough investigation, District Attorney George Gascon said in a statement issued Friday. -- No charges will be filed against San Francisco 49ers wide receiver Michael Crabtree, who was investigated over an alleged sexual assault, officials said. The San Francisco Police Department released a statement on January 18 saying it was investigating "an allegation of a sexual assault involving San Francisco 49ers Michael Crabtree." After the district attorney announced there would be no charges, 49ers General Manager Trent Baalke said the team was pleased with Gascon's decision. "Following a review of all the evidence, my office has determined that no charges will be filed against Michael Crabtree at this time," Gascon said.
