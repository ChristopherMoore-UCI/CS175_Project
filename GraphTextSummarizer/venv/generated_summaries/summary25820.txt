His aim is to prove that Pistorius intentionally shot and killed his girlfriend, Reeva Steenkamp, after a heated argument in the early hours of Valentine's Day last year. Prosecution witnesses have testified to hearing a woman scream, followed by gun shots, but the defense disputes their testimony, saying the only scream came from Pistorius after he had fired the fatal shots. And in an apparent bid to show their romance was not as rocky as portrayed by the prosecution, Pistorius on Tuesday read a card that Steenkamp had planned to give him on Valentine's Day. Last week, Pistorius, 27, took the stand for the first time since the trial started on March 3 to testify about what happened on the fateful day. Gripping trial If Pistorius is found guilty of premeditated murder, he faces 25 years to life in prison.
