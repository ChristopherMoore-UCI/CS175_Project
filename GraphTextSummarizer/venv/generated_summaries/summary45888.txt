When the acolyte surpassed the master and became a bigger name in show business, he and his producers invited Winters to join Williams' successful sitcom "Mork & Mindy" in 1981. Winters cast a long shadow in Williams' work, comedian Gilbert Gottfried said. After all, Winters was better known to the generation of Williams' parents. Indeed, Winters' influence began in Williams' childhood, when he saw how his father belly-laughed at Winters on television. Winters emerged from an egg as a middle-aged offspring of Williams' Mork and Pam Dawber's Mindy.
