Here are some firsts from past State of the Union addresses: First African-American: Obama is the only African-American to have addressed a joint session or joint meeting of Congress. Although Woodrow Wilson holds the record for most speeches delivered before Congress with 26, Franklin Roosevelt holds the record for the most State of the Union/annual message addresses with 12. " Speechless: Two presidents never prepared any type of State of the Union or annual message: William Henry Harrison and James Garfield. 8 memorable State of the Union moments Postponed: The first and only postponement of a State of the Union occurred in 1986. On TV: The first televised State of the Union was delivered by Harry Truman on January 6, 1947.
