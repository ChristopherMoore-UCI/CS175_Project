One of the most repeated quotes came from the founder of Dell, a longtime Apple competitor in personal computers. By that time, in the late 1990s, Apple was on its fourth CEO in just a few years, and Jobs had recently returned as interim CEO when the company acquired his failed education-equipment vendor called NeXT. At a tech conference in October 1997, Dell was asked what he would do with Apple if he were in charge of the beleaguered company. Apple's troubles were well known within the industry, so Dell's comment, while brusque, wasn't out of line with other pundits'.
