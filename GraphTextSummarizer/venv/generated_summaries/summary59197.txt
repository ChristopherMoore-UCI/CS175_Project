Ten years ago, the U.S. Congress passed a law that allows victims of human trafficking to stay in the country if they testify in court against perpetrators of the crime. Danielle Conley, an immigration attorney who helps victims of human trafficking, said this law known as the Trafficking Victims Protection Act helps both victims and prosecutors. Victims get a visa to live in the United States and prosecutors get powerful testimony to build strong cases against suspects of human trafficking.
