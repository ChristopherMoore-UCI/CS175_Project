Steve Arthur works in California's San Joaquin Valley, drilling for water wells on drought-ravaged farms and ranches. In mid-July, California officials approved statewide emergency water restrictions. Sparkling blue water has given way to a skateboarders' dreamland and a reminder of horrific drought. Kern River Outfitters shut down its entire rafting operation this year because attempting to paddle downstream would have been a dangerous, futile effort over exposed Kern River rocks. "The California drought has reduced projected Kern River flows to a level that is inadequate to run the top-quality rafting trips our guests expect," the company's website says.
