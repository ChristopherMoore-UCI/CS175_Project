Politics aside, those were good days for artists, says Natiq al Alousi, 49, an Iraqi sculptor who considers his commissioned work of Saddam to be an achievement. Receiving small recognition from Saddam was important to him as a budding artist, al Alousi says. "There was never a day, for any artist in any form of art, who was forced to work for Saddam Hussein or the country," he says.
