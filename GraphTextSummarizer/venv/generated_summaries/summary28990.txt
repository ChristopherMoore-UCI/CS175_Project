The building has gained plaudits from around the world and won the best cultural building award at the 2011 RIBA International Awards. When the opera house opened reports suggested that Guangzhou's humid climate and sub-standard construction led to problems with some of the granite slabs and glazing. Designed by British architect Zaha Hadid it looks more cosmic than communist, but Hadid has likened it to two rocks washed up from the Pearl River and deposited on its bank. A spokesperson for Zaha Hadid Architects admitted that some "superficial elements" needed adjustment post-opening, but they have since been addressed. In a career spanning three decades, Hadid has built a reputation for designing seemingly unbuildable structures and the opera house's construction explored new areas for both architect and builders.
