-- Strikes by trade unions in Belgium disrupted travel Monday on the Eurostar and Thalys international train lines, the companies announced, as European leaders gathered in the capital Brussels for an informal summit. Thalys cancelled dozens of train services to and from major European destinations including Paris and Amsterdam because of the Belgian strike. Eurostar passengers trying to get to Brussels from London had to change from trains to buses in Lille, France, on Sunday night and Monday, the company announced. But CNN journalists in Brussels Monday did not see protests Monday morning or notice major traffic disruptions as a result of the strike. The general strike was necessary, the union said January 17, "to convince the government and employers to keep in mind the social reality of employees and social welfare recipients.
