Lepper, 57, who headed Poland's populist Self-Defense Party, served as deputy prime minister from 2006 to 2007, as part of a coalition government led by the Law and Justice Party. -- Poland's former deputy prime minister Andrzej Lepper was found dead in his apartment in the capital, Warsaw, on Thursday, his party said in a statement. The party statement said he had left its members "suddenly, leaving a better world of truth and love, as a true Catholic who believed sincerely and warmly." His party paid tribute to a "distinguished politician, statesman" who had founded and led the party and Trade Union of Farmers' Self Defense. He also served as agriculture minister.
