Performance aside, workplace friendships benefit organizations for another reason: Employees with richer friendships tend to stay on with their company for longer periods of time. They present opportunities for colleagues to mingle with those from other departments and connect over shared interests. In recent years, a number of forward-thinking workplaces have begun leveraging March Madness in intelligent ways. While awkward collaborations may spark brief experiences of closeness, they rarely translate into lasting friendships. How do strong colleague relationships elevate our performance?
