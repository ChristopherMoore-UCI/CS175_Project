Once all groups have presented their information, point out to students that, although it was created in 1787, the Constitution is more than an historic document: It is the basis of the laws and civil liberties that affect all of us today. Ask students: What elements of the Constitution do you think have allowed it to survive the test of time and become the oldest written constitution still in effect? Have groups determine the connections between their chosen stories and the Constitution, and then share their findings with the class.
