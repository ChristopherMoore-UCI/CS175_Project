Past elections have included a smooth transition of power, a rarity in a region with a history of election chaos, civil wars and coups. If a candidate does not win 50% of the vote, a runoff election will be held next month in the West African nation. Wade is seeking re-election despite deadly demonstrations after the country's highest court cleared him to seek a third term. "Wade built schools, roads and hospitals," said Ayo Johnson, an analyst on African affairs. "Early in the morning, you saw long queues of people in front of polling stations but, later in the day, there were much less people and it seems that the turnout is below 50%," he said.
