More outbreaks of gastroenteritis have been reported aboard cruise ships since 2001, according to CDC statistics. Cruise ships are ripe for spreading illness. The government agency says the spike is tied to an increase in both passengers and ships sailing the seas as well as an overall increase in norovirus outbreaks. And cruise skeptics who have never been on cruises have another opportunity to ask: What were they thinking? Nursing homes, dormitories and cruise ships are common transmission grounds.
