The campaign had four distinct phases: clear Marjah of Taliban, hold all ground seized, build infrastructure and governance and transfer control to Afghan security forces. Although they achieve their initial mission, when Anderson returns four months later, the Marines have lost 20% of their fighting force to casualties, and battle daily to maintain their shrinking zone of control. Anderson spent two months embedded with Bravo Company, the first Marines dropped into the center of Marjah, where they encountered a maze of improvised explosive devices, bunkers, trenches and ambushes set by well-trained Taliban fighters. "The Battle For Marjah" also looks at the relationship between the Marines and the Afghan people. Ryan Sparks, is a mentor and friend to many of the young Marines, often making split-second decisions when lives are in danger.
