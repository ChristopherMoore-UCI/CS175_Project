The U.S. Anti-Doping Agency said Friday that it has filed doping charges against champion cyclist Lance Armstrong. Armstrong responds to agency's doping allegations CNN's Scott Thompson and Dana Ford contributed to this report. "There is not one shred of credible evidence to support USADA's charges," Armstrong's attorney, Robert Luskin, said in a statement. In February, Justice Department prosecutors said they closed a criminal investigation after reviewing allegations against Armstrong. Armstrong has always insisted that he never took performance-enhancing drugs.
