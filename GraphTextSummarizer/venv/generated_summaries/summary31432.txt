South Korean officials had said they wanted to see action rather than words from Pyongyang, including moving ahead with family reunions. -- North and South Korea have agreed to hold reunions later this month of families separated since the Korean War, Seoul said Wednesday. If they go ahead, the meetings of divided Korean families would be the first to take place since 2010. Past disappointment Reunions were due to take place last September, but Pyongyang canceled them with only a few days notice, accusing Seoul of souring ties between the two countries.
