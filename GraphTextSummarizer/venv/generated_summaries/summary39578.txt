The owners also realized that setting up candy counters and staffing them with high school students (preferably female and pretty) was an easy way to turn a buck, and all the new brands of packaged candy made their way into theaters. The latest trend is theaters offering "luxury" options: premium screening rooms with cocktails, wine lists and upscale menus. Trying to sell more snacks, they instituted intermissions in the middle of films and commissioned cartoons of singing, dancing treats to push the goods. Andrew F. Smith's book "Popped Culture" details the way movie theaters got into the popcorn business. Owners of nickelodeons and movie palaces refused to sell food until the 1920s, according to "Shared Pleasures," a history of movie presentation by Douglas Gomery.
