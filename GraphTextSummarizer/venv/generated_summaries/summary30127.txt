Opinion: In GOP, support for same-sex marriage is growing Increasingly, gay rights isn't a partisan issue; the battle lines are drawn by class rather than political allegiance. Does the futility of Obama's gesture, or the problems faced by same-sex marriage, mean it's game over for gay rights? They are brave words in that seven out of the nine swing states he's contesting in November have constitutional amendments outlawing same-sex marriage -- most of them passed by popular referenda. Crucially, while America is trending Democratic in demographic terms -- as the nonwhite proportion of the population increases -- this doesn't translate into growing support for gay rights. They believed that ultimate victory would be secured with passage of the Equal Rights Amendment, which would amend the Constitution to outlaw discrimination with regard to sex.
