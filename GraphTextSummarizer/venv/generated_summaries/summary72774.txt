Fletcher, the former mayor turned City Council candidate, campaigned that Ferguson would benefit from his experience more than a newcomer, saying his contacts from almost three decades in politics would be an invaluable asset in getting Ferguson back on its feet. When the rain let up for a few minutes, a stream of voters trickled into the First Presbyterian Church in downtown Ferguson to cast their votes. Jackson echoed the thoughts of other Ferguson residents who experienced something new in this election: candidates coming to their door. This was the first city election since white police Officer Darren Wilson shot and killed unarmed black teenager Michael Brown last August. Even though 70% of Ferguson's 21,000 people are black, five of the six current City Council members and the mayor are white.
