The report says suicides in Japan totaled 3,281 in May 2011, up nearly 20% from the same month last year. -- The number of suicides in Japan hit a two-year high in the month of May, according to data released Wednesday by the nation's authorities. The spike reverses a steady decline in the number of suicides since the end of last year, when the government launched a national public awareness campaign. In the area hit hardest by the nuclear crisis, Fukushima saw 19 more suicides in May 2011 compared with May last year, with a total of 68. The most populous region of Tokyo saw the highest number of suicides in May at 325, the agency said.
