According to a European Commission statement, restrictions "violate international trade rules" and hurt producers of car components and computers, among other industries. Rare earth minerals' importance and limited supplies have created friction between China and the U.S., among other countries, for years. Rare earths minerals are hugely important to the technology industry, and the U.S. and the European Union are fierce in their language against China. China has defended its actions by saying they are in line with World Trade Organization regulations, and that the restrictions are due to environmental concerns. They are not actually "rare," and can be found in other countries -- including the U.S. -- but they are difficult to mine safely.
