Despite the tough conditions and the initial objections of her family, Dow decided to pursue a career in law following advice from one of her teachers. While making her first professional steps, Dow says she quickly came across an innate gender inequality built into law. "We did studies on child support and how culture influences the law on child support; we looked at access to property by women; we looked at widowhood and widowhood ceremonies, the extent to which they strengthen and you know undermine women," she says.
