Picking up a pink bunch of roses, Hess shows me he'll be looking at leaves and stems for plant rust, mildew, whitefly and many more bugs. They are picked up locally by the freighter and while they vary in shape and quality, they all have to meet tough quality standards and strict European Union regulations. Lufthansa's cargo pilot, Robert Nilles, tells me this is important "because roses are easily perishable goods, it's very important not to disrupt the code chain. " After a 10 hour flight, they land at Frankfurt and here, it's all about speed and punctuality. -- Every year, come Valentine's Day, I cringe at the thought of walking down the street with red roses, balloons and chocolates.
