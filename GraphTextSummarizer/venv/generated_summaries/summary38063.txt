With each member of Congress representing a very large number of people, representatives receive tremendous attention from special interests. And, it is also not surprising that many members become millionaires once they leave Congress -- if they are the rare ones who weren't rich to begin with. The idea of 3,000 or more members of Congress, constituting a Washington-based power elite, would scare people if we continue to apply the 1780s approach to governing. The role of a member of a much larger Congress should be part-time, making it more attractive for people of varying backgrounds to run. What if members of Congress went to Washington quarterly for two week conventions?
