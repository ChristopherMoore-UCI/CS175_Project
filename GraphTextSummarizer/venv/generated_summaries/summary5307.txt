Leap like you're Lord of the Dance Dancing has always been an integral part of Irish life, comprising everything from traditional Irish folk dances to the vigorous step dances and jigs popularized in modern times by "Lord of the Dance" creator Michael Flatley. According to data collected by the Irish Sports Monitor in 2013, 47% of Irish men and women aged 16 and over participate in some form of regular exercise. Another perk of Irish dancing is core development, since the body and arms are mostly kept still as the dancer performs the elaborate footwork. While some choose common activities (gym-based workouts, swimming, running, cycling), others enjoy singularly Irish ones like dancing (think: Riverdance) and the sport of hurling.
