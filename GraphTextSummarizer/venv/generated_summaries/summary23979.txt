The death toll from an explosion in the central Pakistani city of Dera Ghazi Khan climbed to at least 25 on Wednesday, a senior government official said. The attacker detonated his car in front of the main gate at Khosa's house, said Hassan Iqbal, a senior government official. The blast ripped through a market located near the house of a senior adviser to the chief minister of Punjab province, said Mohammed Hasnain, a rescue official. CNN's Samson Desta and journalist Umar Aziz Khan contributed to this report. The adviser, Sardar Zulfiqar Muhammad Khosa, is a former provincial governor and a senior opposition party politician.
