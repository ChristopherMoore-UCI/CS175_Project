Bernie Tiede, who is white, has had a different experience with Texas justice. Race, though, continues to be, unquestionably, a factor in Texas justice. The list of what are often called the top 10 infamous cases of wrongful execution includes five victims from Texas' death row. The language feels like an understatement considering that 515 inmates have been put to death in Texas since 1982, about 60% of the national total. Texas law regarding the death penalty often feels like a cultural remnant from the days of cowboys and the Old West: You kill; you get killed.
