The City of Vancouver set up a system in which the public could provide video evidence, such as photos and videos from cell phones, anonymously to police. LEVA is the only digital multimedia evidence processing lab in the world designed to train video investigators and analyze massive amounts of forensic video evidence, Fredericks says. "What needs to be developed is a solution for governments to accept public video and images for such situations, as there were likely several Zapruders who captured evidence," he writes, referencing the famous film of the Kennedy assassination. There's a huge amount of video evidence to process, he says, creating a logistics issue.
