Amid growing foreign policy crises, President Barack Obama is encouraging Congress and the country to focus on issues here at home -- namely how to improve the livelihoods of working families. "We have unpaid family leave right now, but for a whole lot of families, it means they can't use it because they just can't afford it," Obama said. On Monday, the President and first lady Michelle Obama will hold a Working Families Summit in Washington, which highlights economic issues affecting American families at home and in the workplace. But the President said paid family leave isn't just a women's issue, as he reflected on his days of becoming a new working dad.
