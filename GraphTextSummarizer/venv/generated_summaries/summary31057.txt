Contrary to popular beliefs, China is not the global engine of growth; it is merely the largest arithmetic component of global growth. -- After many years of euphoria over China's rapid growth and the country's apparently inevitable rise to global economic dominance, the China story has taken a serious turn for the worse. What is far more likely is that in the coming years, China's gross domestic product growth rate will continue to decline as the country focuses on stimulating consumption. What the Chinese government needs to do is recalibrate growth so that average household incomes can rise and consumers have more money to spend. China's growth has been a boon to large businesses, the state, the powerful and the wealthy elite.
