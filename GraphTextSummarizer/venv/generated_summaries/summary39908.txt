Her attorney, Sarah Moshe, had said the U.S. State Department approved a waiver, allowing Olivas to return home and deliver the salutatorian speech at Frankfort High School on Saturday. An undocumented immigrant who was brought to the United States by her parents when she was 4, Olivas traveled to Ciudad Juarez, Mexico, last month to beat a deadline to apply for a visa. Moshe calculated on two different legal calendars that the 180th day would fall on April 17, so Olivas departed for Mexico that day. Steve Edwards, principal at Frankfort High School, called Olivas a "phenomenal kid. The consulate in Mexico granted Olivas an appointment for May 4.
