When Newsom became mayor of San Francisco, he found a city with little money to meet social needs and behind Estonia and South Korea in digital governance. It funded world-class universities to prepare beach boys and California girls for lives of endless promise. Open up the doors of government satellite data and you get Google Earth. Californians moved faster, their problems grew more complex and their demands on government accelerated. Government grew larger and more expensive, with less and less to show for it.
