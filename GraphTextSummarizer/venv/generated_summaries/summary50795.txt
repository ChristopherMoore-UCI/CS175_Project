"World Vision Somalia received a demand today from Al-Shabaab to close operations in Somalia," World Vision said in a statement Monday. According to its website, the group has regional offices in Africa, Asia, the Middle East and Latin America and has aid operations in Somalia. The group said its operations in south central Somalia have been temporarily suspended as "we plan our next steps." ADRA said its work in Somalia as solely focused on implementing emergency relief and development since 1992. "Along with their missionary work, the proliferation of corruption and indecency has become prevalent as a result of their presence," the statement said, warning other non-governmental groups against engaging in "similar activities" All three organizations are Christian aid groups. Al-Shabaab, the Islamist militant group battling Somalia's U.N.-backed transitional government, ordered World Vision, Adventist Development and Relief Agency and Diakonia to cease their operations.
