As happiness research over the past half century or so has shown, belonging is a strong a predictor of happiness. Enter the "Virtual Happiness Project" in the Netherlands, which explores the relationship between the Web 2.0 (in particular) and happiness. Though the final report hasn't been released yet, the project's global survey results and experiments suggest something that a lot of Facebook users already sense: social interaction is a driver for happiness, and the Web 2.0 is a valid way to experience it. Happiness can be found everywhere, including social networks and on blogs.
