An American naval cargo ship has run aground off the Japanese coast, east of Okinawa, and efforts are underway to refloat it, the U.S. Navy said Thursday. The Navy said it was stuck 6 nautical miles off Uruma, on the island of Okinawa. "The U.S. Navy is working closely with Japanese authorities to assess the situation and determine the best course of action to refloat the vessel," the Navy said. " The ship carries 131 crew members -- 38 civilians and 67 Army and 26 Marine Corps personnel, the Navy said. CNN's Will Ripley reported from Tokyo, and Brian Walker wrote from Atlanta.
