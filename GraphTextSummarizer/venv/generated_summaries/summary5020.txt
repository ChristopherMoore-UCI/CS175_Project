Angola also has the highest crude death rate in the world, with 23.4 deaths a year for every 1,000 people, the CIA World Factbook says. The average life expectancy in the tiny European principality is 89.73 years, according to the CIA World Factbook. Twenty-nine nations have life expectancies over 80, but there are also eight under 50. It's also 10 years more than the United States, which comes in at No. 49 with an average life expectancy of 78.37. No country has a higher life expectancy than Monaco -- and it's not even close.
