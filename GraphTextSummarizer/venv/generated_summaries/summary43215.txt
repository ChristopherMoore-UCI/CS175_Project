The lawsuit accuses S&P of knowingly giving AAA ratings to financial products the agency's analysts understood to be unworthy. A responsible credit rating industry is critical to protecting the public interest -- and there is bipartisan support for reform that restores accountability, transparency and integrity to the system. Handing out sparkling ratings to deeply flawed securities represents a serious breach of trust on the part of a credit rating agency. During the open comment period on its report, the SEC received just 32 letters on the issue -- six of them coming from rating agencies. In the wake of that catastrophe, there is bipartisan agreement that the credit ratings process needs serious reform.
