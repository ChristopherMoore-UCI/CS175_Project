The plan calls for introducing water meters, making students pay more for higher education, and requiring more Irish workers to pay income tax. Irish Prime Minister Brian Cowen announced the plan this week after agreeing to a bailout package from the International Monetary Fund and European Union, needed to tackle Ireland's massive debt. The protests were organized by the Irish Congress of Trade Unions (ICTU), which has called the four-year plan for spending cuts and tax hikes "savage and regressive. They say it fails to explain how the Irish people can carry the banks' massive debts and sets out no strategy for creating jobs. The plan saves 10 billion euros ($13.4 billion) through welfare cuts and an additional 5 billion euros ($6.7 billion) through higher taxes.
