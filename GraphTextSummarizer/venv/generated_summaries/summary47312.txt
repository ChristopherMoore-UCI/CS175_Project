Social media users migrating to smaller circles Ephemeral conversation is easy to promise but hard to get right. Even worse, if the FBI or NSA demanded that American companies secretly store those conversations and not tell their users, breaking their promise of deletion, the companies would have no choice but to comply. We started losing ephemeral conversation when computers began to mediate our communications. The need for ephemeral conversation isn't some weird privacy fetish or the exclusive purview of criminals with something to hide. These ephemeral apps are the first concerted push against the permanence of Internet conversation.
