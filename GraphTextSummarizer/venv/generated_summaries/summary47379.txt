Embattled New York Gov. David Paterson vowed Sunday he won't give in to White House pressure to drop out of next year's election, insisting, "I am not failing to stand up for my party." But informed sources told CNN that White House political director Patrick Gaspard met with Paterson to let him know about the administration's concerns he could not win the governor's race next year. New York Gov. David Paterson, right, with President Obama last week in Albany, New York. Paterson was lieutenant governor when a scandal led to then-Gov. Eliot Spitzer's departure from the office in March 2008. "I realize that there are people who don't want me to run." Last Sunday, The New York Times reported that the White House was urging Paterson to withdraw.
