As instant replay became so ubiquitous on sports telecasts, a subtle shift occurred in everyday life. Yet there is this nagging feeling that real life has been converted into a series of moments that are no longer expected to be here and gone, but instead are regarded as first run-throughs: performances that will be witnessed repeatedly, on demand. There was a time when instant replay had never been seen by American sports fans. Instant replay became an integral part of sports broadcasts. And before long, we began to demand the same thing from life itself.
