In the worst single incident for Kenyan police in living memory, 38 officers were killed in northern Kenya over the weekend by heavily armed cattle rustlers, according to injured police reservists who were on the scene. Both Turkana and Samburu leaders have frequently asked for more security in the region from the government. Read more: Attack on Sunday school leaves child dead, others wounded The police officers were part of an operation to recover cattle belonging to the Samburu tribe that had been stolen by the Turkana tribe, according to the minister. In the most recent incident, Turkana raided the Samburu last month and took a large amount of cattle, according to the internal security ministry. The Turkana and Samburu, both pastoralist groups, have frequently engaged in tit-for-tat cattle rustling operations over many years.
