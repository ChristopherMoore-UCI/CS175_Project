Once primarily the domain of dads who don't mind embarrassing their children, tablet photography has become so common that Apple added serious camera features to its new full-sized iPad. Should tablets be used as cameras? Early tablet photographers clearly aren't too concerned about what other people think, or the hardware's limitations. Aside from the camera quality, there are a number of reasons for the stigma against tablet photography -- including what professional photographer and instructor Steve Simon calls "the dorky factor." Why, when it's very likely they have a smartphone with a better camera, do people use tablets to take photos and videos?
