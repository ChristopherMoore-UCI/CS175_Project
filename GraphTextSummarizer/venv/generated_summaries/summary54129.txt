Her story of a young girl named Scout, her wise father, Atticus, and the racially charged events in fictitious Maycomb, Alabama, resonated widely. When I graduated from high school in 1978, I lived in that house with my parents not far from Lee's home in Monroeville, Alabama. For many years, she shuttled between a home in New York City and Monroeville, keeping an active and lively circle of family and friends. My mother even recalls her older brother, James, telling her back when Lee's story was published that "a woman from Monroeville wrote a book," as if he were mentioning a school classmate. " The manuscript for "Go Set a Watchman," she says, was unexpectedly rediscovered and "after much thought and hesitation, I shared it with a handful of people I trust and was pleased to hear that they considered it worthy of publication.
