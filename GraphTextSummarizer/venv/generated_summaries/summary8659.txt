Al-Maliki is in the United States for a meeting with President Barack Obama to plan "a deeper security relationship" with Washington to fight terrorism and deal with regional security issues. Suicide bombings claim more lives in Iraq Violence has been on the upswing in Iraq since spring as tensions have grown, particularly between the country's Shiite majority and its Sunni minority. Eight people were killed on Thursday in shootings and bombings across Iraq, police in Tikrit said. Gunmen in Baghdad shot dead three people, Baghdad police said. Iraqi Prime Minister Nuri al-Maliki, who is Shiite, touched on the violence in a New York Times op-ed column this week.
