It is a venue which has welcomed countless political leaders and business executives, during internet booms and banking busts, in the midst of a Middle East crisis and even during the lead up to two Gulf Wars. But that inter-play between geo-politics and business -- during the best and worst of times -- is what keeps the forum relevant. While the author of the Magic Mountain talked about the complexity of time around World War I, in the 1990s time was compressed here. U.S. President Bill Clinton outlined his party's historic move to the political center before a packed audience of global business executives. The name comes from an early 20th century novel by Thomas Mann -- reflecting on life in an alpine health retreat, and the mystery of time in this breath-taking setting.
