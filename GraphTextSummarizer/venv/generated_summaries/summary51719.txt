Still, he has kept the ratings singing for NBC, and in a much more crowded late night universe than Carson ever experienced. In truth, NBC's latest late night moves could and probably should have waited a few more years. Jack Paar, who had a comparatively short run in the "Tonight Show" chair in the years before Carson, was the show's pre-eminent conversationalist. Carson relied far more heavily on "blue card" crib notes that have become talk show staples during interview segments. NBC's later slot historically has gone to a host who's a generation younger, with the idea of grooming him for the "Tonight Show" desk.
