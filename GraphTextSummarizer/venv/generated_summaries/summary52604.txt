This year, he quit his job as a mechanical engineer at NASA and started Digital Dudz, a company that makes costumes that integrate tablets and smartphones. Put your iOS or Android smartphone in a special fleshy case, add a carefully bloodied, ripped T-shirt and you have the iWound. Former NASA employee Mark Rober has created an app that shows animations of moving, oozing innards. Portable battery packs, LED lights and cheap motors can all be used to make ingenious costumes. Rober first got the idea for mixing Halloween and smartphones in 2011.
