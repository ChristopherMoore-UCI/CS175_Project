The members of S.A.L. have been forced to abandon their homes in this tightly-packed community of concrete houses and narrow alleyways. The artists have helped each other build crude shacks out of plywood and rusting metal sheeting, along a winding dirt alleyway they have playfully nick-named "Big Man Street." "All the parks have become home to most," he chanted in Creole, as the sun set over smoking campfires. He is a member of a neighborhood hip-hop group called Sekte Atis Lib, or S.A.L. They are six childhood friends from the same poor neighborhood who rap in Creole. He then issued an appeal for other aid organizations to come and help the community.
