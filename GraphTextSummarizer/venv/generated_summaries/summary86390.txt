"I think it's very important to explain robotic surgery," says David Rosa from da Vinci creators, Intuitive Surgical. "When you sit down at da Vinci console, it can do things that you cannot do through other means," Rosa says. "The Achilles heel of robotic surgery is that the robot cannot feel the tissue, so the surgeon can make an inadvertent injury to a major structure, and that is a unique problem with this kind of surgery," he said. The company's tele-operated robots have performed more than 1.5 million operations from abdominal general surgery and gynaecology procedures to thoracic and lung operations.
