More people were also watching horse racing on TV, with 11 million tuning in to Britain's biggest competition, The Grand National -- up 2 million on last year. Attendance at races starring Frankel were up 20% on last year. This year however, racing attracted a new audience who knew the names of horses, recognized industry heavyweights and understood the importance of big competitions. But some relief is in sight -- albeit slight -- after the British Horseracing Authority last month raised the minimum weight by two pounds to 8 stone (50 kg), from January next year. However, the overall figure of 280,000 for all five days was down 14,000 from last year.
