Digital advertising, of which native advertising is a part, accounts for 59% of The Atlantic's overall revenue, according to the company.) Going forward, The Atlantic (and any other publication that chooses to run sponsored content) should adopt and clearly communicate an explicit ethics statement regarding advertorials and their corresponding comments. As The Washington Post reported, The Atlantic marketing team was carefully pruning the comments, ensuring that they were predominantly positive, even though many readers were leaving negative comments. If the Church of Scientology was not willing to compromise its advertising to be better content, then The Atlantic should not have accepted the advertising. At face value, The Atlantic did the right thing for its business model, which depends upon advertising sales.
