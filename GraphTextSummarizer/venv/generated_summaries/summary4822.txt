Serious questions remain, however, about the level of desire among potential buyers worried about cost, ease of use and likely resale value. According to a report released Wednesday by researchers at Indiana University, automakers are unlikely to manufacture enough cars to reach the president's goal because of a potential lack of buyer demand. The administration has created a number of incentives for consumers to purchase electric cars, including a $7,500 tax credit to first-time buyers. Bedi nevertheless praised Obama's goal, noting that it "has really jump started" the market for electric cars. In 2008, there were nearly 57,000 electric cars on U.S. roads, according to an estimate by the U.S. Energy Information Administration.
