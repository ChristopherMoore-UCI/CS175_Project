Mading Maker had previously used his uncle's surname (Marial) when he moved to the U.S. but reverted to his father's name (Mading Maker Deng) when he was sworn in as a U.S. citizen. He had no country to run for -- South Sudan was not a member of International Olympic Committee (IOC) and he had yet to be granted U.S. citizenship. " Return to homeland The following summer was even more momentous for Mading Maker as he returned to South Sudan to visit his parents for the first time in two decades. A peace agreement signed between north and south Sudan in January 2005 would eventually lead to full independence for South Sudan six years later. While Mading Maker began making great strides in the U.S. back in his homeland things were also looking up.
