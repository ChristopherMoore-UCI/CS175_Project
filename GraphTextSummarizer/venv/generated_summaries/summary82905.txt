In her twenties, mathematician Ann Cairns was quite literally thrown in the deep end, working on British Gas rigs in the remote North Sea in the early 1980s. "I hope my daughter has learned that happiness and success are bound together -- you can't separate your business life from your family life." "It started off with designing pipelines, testing them, blowing up the pipes and seeing what pressure they'd burst at," said the mother-of-one, originally from Newcastle in the north of England. "I think if you're in the engineering world you love fixing things," she said of coping under extreme pressure. Three years later Cairns joined Mastercard as the head of international business, managing all operations for the company outside of the U.S.
