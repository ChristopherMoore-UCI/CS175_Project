U.S. destroyer, oil tanker collide 'Weirdest thing' floats in South Pacific The U.S. Navy said its guided missile destroyer collided with a Japanese-owned oil tanker in the Strait of Hormuz early Sunday morning. The Navy did not provide details about the collision, saying only the accident was not related to combat. The Navy did not say what, if any, damage the Otowasan incurred in the collision. No one was injured in the collision that occurred about 1 a.m. local time when the USS Porter collided with the Panamanian-flagged bulk oil tanker M/V Otowasan, the Navy said in a statement.
