New abuse-deterrent painkiller approved FDA aims to tighten control of hydrocodone FDA fights drug overdoses with new labels for prescription painkillers DEA turns to texting to fight prescription drug abuse Prescription drug abuse is now a health epidemic in America, worse than crack in the 1980s, and Scudo's story is one example. Cynthia Scudo is a portrait of an all-American mom of eight and grandmother of 18 who survived an extraordinary journey lasting a decade: She became addicted to prescription painkillers and then to heroin. To feed her cravings, Scudo leveraged her maternal authority: She found drug dealers who were friends of her eldest children. Her addiction to a handful of prescription drugs, with OxyContin as first and foremost, led to heroin because it gave her the same high at a cheaper price.
